# Major Rivers of the World

In 1858, British explorer John Hanning Speke stood at the northern shore of a vast lake in East Africa and declared it the source of the Nile. He named it Lake Victoria after his queen. His rival Richard Burton, still ill with malaria at their base camp, refused to believe him. The ensuing controversy about the "true source of the Nile" consumed European geography for decades and sent several expeditions back to Africa — all because rivers are not simple lines on a map. Their sources, lengths, basins, and outlets are deeply contested, regularly remeasured, and constantly tested by SSC examiners who love the superlatives.

---

## Key River Parameters Explained

- **Length:** Measured from source to mouth; controversial because sources are often debated
- **Drainage basin:** Total land area that drains into a river (catchment area)
- **Discharge:** Volume of water flowing per unit time (m³/s) — Amazon has highest discharge
- **Tributaries:** Smaller rivers feeding into a larger one

## Delta and Estuary
- **Delta:** Triangular depositional feature at river mouth where sediment accumulates (e.g., Nile Delta, Ganges-Brahmaputra Sundarbans Delta, Mississippi Delta)
- **Estuary:** Funnel-shaped mouth; tidal; mixed fresh and salt water; no delta formation (e.g., Thames Estuary, Hudson Estuary)
- Deltas form where: river sediment load > tidal/wave energy (calm seas)
- Estuaries form where: tidal energy > sediment deposition (strong tides)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Nile is the longest river** (~6,650 km) — Amazon (~6,400 km) is debated 2nd. **Amazon has the greatest discharge and largest drainage basin.**
2. **Congo is the deepest river** in the world, not the longest or highest-discharge.
3. **Yangtze (China) is the longest river in Asia**, not the Indus, Ganges, or Ob.
4. **Volga is the longest river in Europe** — NOT the Rhine, Danube, or Thames.
5. **Missouri is actually slightly longer than Mississippi** — but Mississippi-Missouri system is counted together.
6. **Brahmaputra in Tibet is called Yarlung Tsangpo** — same river; enters India through Arunachal Pradesh.
7. **Nile flows NORTH** — it originates in equatorial Africa and drains into the Mediterranean Sea.
8. **Three Gorges Dam is on the Yangtze**, not the Yellow River.
9. **Itaipú Dam is on the Paraná River** (Brazil-Paraguay border), not Amazon.
10. **Murray-Darling is Australia's longest river system** — Murray alone is not the longest.
11. **Narmada and Tapi flow west** (into Arabian Sea) — all other major Indian rivers flow east.
12. **Amazon delta is NOT prominent** — Amazon has an estuary, not a classic delta; Sundarbans is the world's largest delta (Ganges-Brahmaputra).
13. **Mekong flows through 6 countries** (China, Myanmar, Laos, Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam) — among the most internationally shared rivers.
14. **Lake Victoria** is the primary source of the White Nile — the Kagera River is the most distant headwater.
15. **Victoria Falls is on the Zambezi**, not the Nile or Congo.
16. **Mackenzie is the longest river in Canada**, not the St. Lawrence (which is more famous due to Great Lakes).
