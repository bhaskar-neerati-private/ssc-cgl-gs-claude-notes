# World Lakes, Waterfalls & Glaciers

In October 1869, a Pemon indigenous guide led the British-American explorer Charles Brewer-Carías toward a remote plateau in Venezuela. Ahead of him, water seemed to fall from the sky itself — a waterfall dropping 979 metres from a flat-topped tepui into the jungle below, the longest uninterrupted waterfall on Earth. It had been known to the Pemon as "Kerepakupai Merú" for centuries. The explorer Jimmie Angel, who crash-landed nearby in 1933, gave it his own name. "Angel Falls" it became — and it remains a reminder that the most dramatic water features on Earth are often hidden, named by outsiders, and tested by SSC examiners who want to know whether you know both names.

### The Great Lakes of North America
- **H-O-M-E-S:** Huron, Ontario, Michigan, Erie, Superior (mnemonic)
- Connect to Atlantic via St. Lawrence River
- Lake Michigan is the **only Great Lake entirely within USA**
- **Lake Ontario** is smallest Great Lake by area (but not the shallowest)
- **Lake Erie** is shallowest; pollution concern (algal blooms)
- Niagara Falls is between Lake Erie and Lake Ontario

### African Great Lakes (Rift Valley Lakes)
- Formed by East African Rift System
- Victoria (N Rift), Tanganyika, Malawi/Nyasa, Albert, Edward, Turkana, Kivu
- **Lake Tanganyika** shared by 4 countries; 2nd deepest; longest freshwater lake

## Glaciers of the World

A **glacier** is a large, persistent body of ice moving under its own weight due to gravity and internal deformation.

### Types of Glaciers
- **Valley/Alpine glacier:** Flows down mountain valleys; most common in Himalayas, Alps, Andes
- **Ice sheet:** Continent-sized; Antarctica (~14 million km²), Greenland (~1.7 million km²)
- **Ice cap:** Smaller dome-shaped ice covering high ground
- **Piedmont glacier:** When valley glaciers flow into flat plains and spread
- **Tidewater glacier:** Flows into ocean; calves icebergs

### Glacial Landforms
- **Cirque/Corrie/Cwm:** Amphitheatre-shaped hollow where glacier originates
- **Arête:** Sharp ridge between two cirques
- **Horn/Pyramidal peak:** Peak carved by glaciers from 3+ sides (e.g., Matterhorn, Switzerland)
- **U-shaped valley:** Carved by glacier (V-shaped valleys are river-carved)
- **Hanging valley:** Smaller tributary glacier valley left high above main valley when ice melts
- **Glacial lake/Tarn:** Lake in former cirque after glacier melts
- **Moraine:** Rock debris deposited by glacier
  - Terminal moraine: At glacier's front
  - Lateral moraine: Along sides
  - Medial moraine: Down the middle of glacier (when two glaciers merge)
- **Fjord:** Flooded glacial U-shaped valley; Norway, New Zealand, Chile
- **Drumlin:** Streamlined hill of glacial deposit; oval; pointed end faces direction of ice movement
- **Kettle lake:** Lake formed by melting buried ice block

### Glacial Retreat and Climate Change
- Most glaciers worldwide are retreating due to global warming
- **Himalayan glaciers:** Among fastest retreating; threaten water supply for billions
- **Arctic sea ice:** Extent declining annually; Northwest Passage opening
- **Greenland ice sheet melting:** Major contributor to sea level rise
- IPCC reports: Glaciers and ice sheets are melting at accelerated rates since 1950s

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Caspian Sea is the largest lake by area** — but NOT freshwater; **Lake Superior is the largest freshwater lake by area**.
2. **Lake Baikal is the deepest lake** AND contains ~20% of world's surface freshwater — AND is the world's oldest lake.
3. **Lake Titicaca is the highest navigable lake** (3,812 m) — NOT the highest lake overall; that's Tso Lhamo in Sikkim (5,330 m) or Pamela Lake in Tibet.
4. **Angel Falls (Venezuela) is the world's tallest waterfall** (979 m) — NOT Niagara or Victoria.
5. **Victoria Falls is the widest waterfall** and is on the Zambezi River — NOT the tallest.
6. **Niagara Falls is between Lake Erie and Lake Ontario** (drops from Erie to Ontario level).
7. **Lake Michigan is the only Great Lake entirely within USA** — the other 4 are USA-Canada border.
8. **Wular Lake (J&K) is the largest freshwater lake in India** — NOT Dal Lake (which is more famous).
9. **Chilika Lake (Odisha) is the largest coastal lagoon in India** — brackish; NOT freshwater.
10. **Sambhar Lake (Rajasthan) is the largest inland saltwater lake in India** — NOT Chilika (which is coastal/brackish).
11. **Kunchikal Falls (455 m) is India's highest waterfall** — NOT Jog Falls (253 m), which is only the 4th highest.
12. **Jog Falls is on the Sharavathi River** (Karnataka) — NOT the Kali River or any other.
13. **Siachen Glacier is India's largest glacier** (world's largest non-polar glacier at 76 km) — in Karakoram.
14. **Gangotri is the source of the Ganga** (Bhagirathi headstream) — NOT a lake; it's a glacier in Uttarakhand.
15. **Aral Sea has shrunk to ~10% of its original size** — catastrophic environmental disaster caused by Soviet irrigation projects.
16. **Matterhorn** is an example of a pyramidal peak/horn — formed by glacial erosion from multiple sides in the Alps (Switzerland-Italy border).
