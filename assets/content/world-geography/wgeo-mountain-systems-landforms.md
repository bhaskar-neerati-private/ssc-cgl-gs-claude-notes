# World Mountain Systems & Landforms

In May 1953, Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay stood on the summit of Mount Everest — 8,849 metres above sea level, the highest point on Earth's surface. But here is a question SSC loves to hide: if "highest" means distance from Earth's centre, the answer changes. Mount Chimborazo in Ecuador (6,268 m above sea level) is actually **farther from Earth's centre** because it sits near the equatorial bulge, where Earth's radius is greatest. The world's mountains are a geography lesson in definitions — and SSC tests both the conventional facts and the surprising exceptions.

---

## Types of Mountains

| Type | Formation | Examples |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Fold Mountains** | Tectonic plates collide; rocks folded upward | Himalayas, Alps, Andes, Rockies, Appalachians, Urals |
| **Block/Fault-Block Mountains** | Rocks between faults uplifted (horst) or dropped (graben) | Vindhyas, Satpuras, Black Forest (Germany), Vosges (France) |
| **Volcanic Mountains** | Lava accumulation from volcanic eruptions | Mt. Fuji, Mt. Kilimanjaro, Mt. Vesuvius, Mt. Rainier |
| **Residual/Relict Mountains** | Erosion of old plateaus leaving hard rock hills | Aravalli (oldest fold mountains in India — now eroded to relict), Eastern/Western Ghats |

## Major Mountain Systems of the World

### The Himalayas
- **Type:** Young fold mountains (formed ~50 million years ago when Indian plate collided with Eurasian plate)
- **Extent:** ~2,400 km from Indus Gorge (NW) to Brahmaputra Gorge (NE)
- **Three ranges:** Greater Himalayas (Himadri) — Outer Himalayas (Siwaliks) — Lesser Himalayas (Himachal)
- **Highest peaks:**

| Peak | Height | Location |
|------|--------|----------|
| Everest | 8,849 m | Nepal-Tibet border |
| K2 (Godwin-Austen) | 8,611 m | Pakistan (PoK) — 2nd highest |
| Kangchenjunga | 8,586 m | India-Nepal border — 3rd highest |
| Lhotse | 8,516 m | Nepal-Tibet |
| Makalu | 8,485 m | Nepal-Tibet |

- **K2** is technically in Pakistan-administered Kashmir; India claims the territory
- **Kangchenjunga** is the highest peak entirely or partly in India

### The Andes
- Longest mountain range in the world: ~7,000 km (South America, western coast)
- Countries: Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina
- **Highest peak:** Aconcagua (6,961 m) — highest peak outside Asia; in Argentina
- Contains: Active volcanoes, Lake Titicaca (highest navigable lake, 3,812 m), source of Amazon River

### The Rockies (Rocky Mountains)
- North America; ~4,800 km
- Extends from British Columbia (Canada) to New Mexico (USA)
- **Highest peak:** Mt. Elbert (4,401 m) in Colorado
- Divide drainage between Pacific and Atlantic/Gulf of Mexico (Continental Divide)

### The Alps
- Europe; ~1,200 km; runs through France, Switzerland, Italy, Austria, Germany, Slovenia
- **Highest peak:** Mont Blanc (4,808 m) — France/Italy border; highest peak in Western Europe
- Rivers originating: Rhine, Rhône, Po, Inn (Danube tributary)

### The Caucasus
- Between Black Sea and Caspian Sea; Russia/Georgia/Azerbaijan
- **Mt. Elbrus (5,642 m)** — highest peak in Europe (if Caucasus is considered Europe; higher than Mont Blanc)
- **NOTE:** If Europe ends at Caucasus crest, Elbrus is Europe's highest; if Bosphorus-only definition, Mont Blanc wins — SSC generally accepts **Elbrus** as Europe's highest

### The Urals
- Natural boundary between Europe and Asia; Russia
- Old fold mountains, heavily eroded — average height ~600 m
- **Highest peak:** Mt. Narodnaya (1,895 m)
- Rich in mineral resources (iron, copper, gold)

### The Atlas Mountains
- NW Africa (Morocco, Algeria, Tunisia)
- **Highest peak:** Jbel Toubkal (4,167 m), Morocco
- Separates Mediterranean coast from Sahara

### African Peaks

| Peak | Height | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Mt. Kilimanjaro** | 5,895 m | Highest in Africa; volcanic; Tanzania; has glaciers (retreating) |
| Mt. Kenya | 5,199 m | Second highest in Africa; Kenya; volcanic |
| Rwenzori (Margherita Peak) | 5,109 m | Third highest; Uganda/DRC; "Mountains of the Moon" |

### North American Peaks

| Peak | Height | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Denali (Mt. McKinley)** | 6,190 m | Highest in North America; Alaska, USA |
| Mt. Logan | 5,959 m | Highest in Canada |
| Pico de Orizaba | 5,636 m | Highest in Mexico; volcanic |

### Australian / Oceania
- **Mt. Wilhelm** (4,509 m) — highest in Oceania (Papua New Guinea)
- **Mt. Kosciuszko** (2,228 m) — highest in Australia (mainland)
- **Puncak Jaya/Carstensz Pyramid** (4,884 m) — sometimes counted as Oceania's highest (on New Guinea island, which may be classified with Australia continent in "Seven Summits" lists)

| Continent | Peak | Height |
|-----------|------|--------|

## Major Landforms

### Plateaus
- Elevated flatlands; formed by volcanic activity, erosion, or tectonic uplift
- **Tibetan Plateau:** "Roof of the World"; average 4,500 m; largest and highest plateau
- **Deccan Plateau:** India; volcanic (Deccan Traps — lava flows ~65 million years ago); black cotton soil
- **Patagonian Plateau:** South America (Argentina); cold desert
- **Columbia Plateau:** USA; basaltic volcanic plateau
- **Arabian Plateau:** SW Asia; vast desert plateau
- **Chota Nagpur Plateau:** India; mineral-rich (jharkhand)

### Plains
- Low, flat areas; formed by river deposition, marine erosion, or tectonic subsidence
- **Indo-Gangetic Plain:** Formed by Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra river deposits; most fertile region in India
- **Amazon Basin:** World's largest river basin with extensive floodplains
- **Great Plains:** North America; wheat belt
- **Siberian Plain:** Largest plain in the world (Western Siberian Plain)

### Valleys and Gorges
- **Rift Valley:** Formed when land sinks between parallel faults; Great Rift Valley (East Africa)
- **River Valley:** Carved by river erosion; V-shaped (youth) → U-shaped (maturity/glacial)
- **Grand Canyon:** Colorado River, USA; 446 km long, up to 1.6 km deep

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **K2 is 2nd highest peak**, not 3rd; **Kangchenjunga is 3rd highest** and the highest on Indian soil.
2. **Andes is the longest mountain range** (~7,000 km); Himalayas are tallest but not longest.
3. **Aconcagua is highest peak outside Asia** — located in Argentina (not Chile).
4. **Mt. Elbrus (5,642 m) is higher than Mont Blanc (4,808 m)** — usually accepted as Europe's highest.
5. **Kilimanjaro is NOT a fold mountain** — it's a volcanic mountain (extinct stratovolcano).
6. **Aravalli is the oldest mountain range in India** — also one of the oldest in the world; heavily eroded.
7. **Tibetan Plateau is the highest plateau** ("Roof of the World"), not the Deccan Plateau.
8. **Denali (Alaska, USA) is North America's highest** — sometimes still called "McKinley" in old questions.
9. **Mont Blanc is the highest peak in the Alps and Western Europe**, not in Europe overall if Elbrus is counted.
10. **Fold mountains are the most common type** and include the world's major ranges (Himalayas, Alps, Andes, Rockies).
11. **Vindhyas and Satpuras are block mountains** (fault-block), not fold mountains.
12. **Eastern Ghats and Western Ghats are residual mountains** — eroded remnants.
13. **Vinson Massif** is the highest peak in Antarctica — not Everest or some other well-known peak.
14. **Western Siberian Plain is the world's largest plain** (not the Indo-Gangetic Plain, though it's India's largest).
15. **Grand Canyon** is carved by the Colorado River (NOT the Mississippi, Amazon, or any Indian river).
16. **Chota Nagpur Plateau** — located in Jharkhand (NOT Chhattisgarh, though they border); contains most of India's mineral wealth.
