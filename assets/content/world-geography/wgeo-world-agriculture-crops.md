# World Agriculture & Crop Regions

In the 1960s, a Texas-born plant scientist named Norman Borlaug was working in a wheat field in Mexico when he discovered a dwarf wheat variety that produced dramatically higher yields. He took it to India in 1965 — when India faced imminent famine. Within five years, India's wheat production had doubled. Borlaug received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 and is credited with saving a billion lives. He called it the **Green Revolution** — but it was built on one core geographic insight: that where a crop grows, the soil type, climate, and water supply must all align. The SSC CGL geography section tests precisely this alignment — which crop, which region, which soil, which country leads production.

## Types of Agriculture

| Type | Description | Where Practiced |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Subsistence Agriculture** | Growing food for own consumption; low surplus | Tropical Africa, SE Asia, parts of India |
| **Shifting Cultivation (Jhum/Slash-and-Burn)** | Clear forest, farm 2-3 years, move on | NE India, Congo, Amazon |
| **Intensive Subsistence** | High labour per hectare; rice/wheat | Monsoon Asia |
| **Commercial Agriculture** | Large-scale; mechanised; for market | USA, Canada, Australia |
| **Plantation Agriculture** | Single cash crop; large estates; export | Tea (Assam), Coffee (Brazil), Rubber (SE Asia) |
| **Mixed Farming** | Crops + livestock together | Western Europe, USA Midwest |
| **Dairy Farming** | Commercial milk/butter production | Denmark, Netherlands, New Zealand, India |
| **Truck Farming/Market Gardening** | Vegetables near cities | Peri-urban areas everywhere |

## Food Crops

### Rice
- **Climate:** Hot and wet (25°C+; >1,500 mm rainfall) or irrigated
- **Soil:** Clayey soils that retain water; alluvial
- **World's top producers:** China (#1), India (#2), Bangladesh, Indonesia, Vietnam
- **India's rice regions:** West Bengal, Punjab (irrigated), Odisha, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Assam
- **Double/Triple cropping:** Possible in humid tropics
- **International Rice Research Institute (IRRI):** Manila, Philippines

### Wheat
- **Climate:** Moderate temperature (15–20°C); 375–875 mm rainfall; cool growing, warm dry harvest
- **Two types:** Winter wheat (sown in autumn, harvested in summer) and Spring wheat (sown in spring)
- **World's top producers:** China (#1), India (#2), Russia, USA, Australia, Ukraine
- **India's wheat regions:** Punjab, Haryana, UP (wheat belt)
- **World wheat belts:** Canadian Prairies, US Great Plains, Russian Steppes, Argentine Pampas, Australian SE
- **Spring wheat:** Canada, Northern USA, Russia (short growing season)

### Maize (Corn)
- **Climate:** 21–27°C; 500–750 mm rainfall; tolerates various soils
- **Top producers:** USA (#1), China (#2), Brazil, Argentina
- **USA Corn Belt:** Iowa, Illinois, Indiana, Ohio, Missouri
- **Uses:** Food, livestock feed, ethanol, industrial starch

### Millets (Jowar, Bajra, Ragi)
- **Drought-resistant** — grown in semi-arid regions
- **Jowar (Sorghum):** Maharashtra, Karnataka
- **Bajra (Pearl Millet):** Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra
- **Ragi (Finger Millet):** Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu

### Barley
- Climate: Cool and dry; 300–1,000 m altitude preferred
- Used for: Beer, animal feed, bread
- Top producers: Russia, France, Germany, Australia, Ukraine

### Potato
- Climate: Cool temperature (15–20°C); well-drained fertile soil
- Top producers: China, India, Russia, Ukraine, USA
- India: UP, West Bengal, Bihar

## Cash/Commercial Crops

### Cotton
- **Climate:** Hot (25–35°C); 500–1,000 mm rainfall; 200+ frost-free days; sunshine during boll formation
- **Soil:** Black cotton soil (regur) ideal; also alluvial
- **World's top producers:** India (#1 by area, sometimes #1 by production), China, USA, Pakistan
- **India's cotton regions:** Maharashtra, Gujarat, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh (Deccan — black soil)
- **USA cotton belt:** Texas, Mississippi, Alabama, Georgia
- **Gossypium hirsutum (American cotton):** Most widely grown variety worldwide

### Jute
- **Climate:** Hot and humid (25–35°C); >1,500 mm rainfall; alluvial, low-lying fields
- **Nicknamed "Golden Fibre"**
- **Top producers:** Bangladesh (#1), India (#2)
- **India's jute regions:** West Bengal (Hugli region), Bihar, Assam
- **Uses:** Gunny bags, rope, carpet backing, hessian cloth

### Tea
- **Climate:** Hot and wet (20–30°C); 150–300 cm rainfall; well-drained hill slopes; acidic soil
- **World's top producers:** China (#1), India (#2), Kenya (#3), Sri Lanka (#4)
- **India's tea regions:** Assam (Brahmaputra valley — largest); Darjeeling (finest quality); Nilgiris (South India)
- **Types:** Black tea, Green tea, Oolong — differ in processing
- **Indian Tea Association:** Kolkata

### Coffee
- **Climate:** 15–28°C; 150–200 cm rainfall; 600–1,800 m altitude (Arabica); red/laterite soil
- **Two main types:** Arabica (high altitude, fine flavour) and Robusta (lowland, stronger)
- **World's top producers:** Brazil (#1), Vietnam (#2), Colombia (#3), Indonesia, Ethiopia
- **India's coffee regions:** Karnataka (Coorg/Kodagu, Chikmagalur — #1 in India), Kerala, Tamil Nadu

### Sugarcane
- **Climate:** Hot (20–30°C); 150 cm rainfall; 10–15 months growing period; alluvial/black soil
- **World's top producers:** Brazil (#1), India (#2), China, Thailand
- **India's sugarcane regions:** UP, Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu
- **Sugar mills:** Highest concentration in UP

### Rubber
- **Climate:** Hot (25–35°C); 200+ cm rainfall; laterite soil; equatorial/tropical
- **Rubber Tree:** Hevea brasiliensis (originally from Amazon)
- **World's top producers:** Thailand (#1), Indonesia (#2), Vietnam, Malaysia, India
- **India's rubber regions:** Kerala (#1), Karnataka, Tamil Nadu

### Cocoa
- **Climate:** Hot (20–30°C); >1,500 mm; shade from taller trees
- **Top producers:** Ivory Coast (Côte d'Ivoire) (#1), Ghana (#2), Indonesia, Nigeria
- **Used for:** Chocolate, cocoa butter, cocoa powder

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **China is #1 in rice, wheat, and many other crops** — BUT India is #1 in cotton area and often trades places for production.
2. **Brazil is #1 in coffee production** — NOT Colombia (which is famous for quality, not quantity).
3. **Bangladesh is #1 in jute production** — India is #2 (even though Kolkata processed most jute in British era).
4. **Thailand is #1 in rubber** — NOT Malaysia (which dominated historically). India's rubber = Kerala.
5. **Ivory Coast (Côte d'Ivoire) is #1 in cocoa** — NOT Ghana or Nigeria (which are also famous).
6. **Tea: China #1, India #2, Kenya #3, Sri Lanka #4** — Darjeeling is finest but not highest production.
7. **Jute is called "Golden Fibre"** — NOT cotton (cotton is called "White Gold").
8. **Cotton: Black cotton/regur soil = Deccan plateau** — India's cotton belt; Maharashtra, Gujarat, Telangana.
9. **IRRI is in Manila, Philippines** — NOT India.
10. **ICRISAT is in Hyderabad** (dryland crops — sorghum, millets, groundnut, chickpea, pigeonpea) — NOT IRRI (rice).
11. **Saffron in India only from Kashmir** — Iran is the world's dominant producer.
12. **India produces ~80% of world's turmeric** — one of the most one-sided global production statistics.
13. **Ukraine = "breadbasket of Europe"** (wheat); India/Pakistan is "breadbasket of South Asia" — not the same.
14. **Plantation agriculture = single export crop on large estates** — distinct from subsistence (small, diverse, own consumption).
15. **Shifting cultivation is called "Jhum" in NE India**, "Milpa" in Mexico/Central America, "Taungya" in Myanmar — SSC loves the regional name.
16. **Green Revolution in India** began ~1965–70; Norman Borlaug provided dwarf wheat varieties; Punjab and Haryana were key states; transformed wheat and rice production.
