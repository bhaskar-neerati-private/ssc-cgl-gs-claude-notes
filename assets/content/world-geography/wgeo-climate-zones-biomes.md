# World Climate Zones & Biomes

In 1884, a young Russian-German botanist named Wladimir Köppen spent weeks staring at global temperature maps and vegetation atlases, searching for a pattern. He noticed something simple but profound: wherever a particular type of vegetation grew, the climate followed predictable rules. Tropical forests grew where no month averaged below 18°C. Deserts formed where annual rainfall fell below 250 mm. By 1900, he had a working classification of world climates — the **Köppen Climate Classification** — still the most widely used framework in the world and the one SSC CGL implicitly tests through questions about regions, vegetation, and typical rainfall patterns.

---

## Köppen Climate Classification (Main Types)

| Letter | Climate | Key Characteristic |
|--------|---------|-------------------|
| **A** | Tropical | All months ≥ 18°C; no winter |
| **B** | Dry (Arid/Semi-arid) | Evaporation > Precipitation |
| **C** | Temperate | Mild winters; coldest month 0°C to 18°C |
| **D** | Continental | Cold winters; coldest month < 0°C |
| **E** | Polar | No warm season; warmest month < 10°C |

## Major Climate Zones in Detail

### A — Tropical Climates

**Af — Tropical Rainforest**
- Found: Within 10° of equator — Amazon Basin, Congo Basin, SE Asia (Indonesia, Malaysia)
- Rainfall: >2,000 mm/year; every month >60 mm
- Temperature: ~26–28°C all year; no distinct seasons
- Vegetation: Dense, multi-layered evergreen forest; highest biodiversity on Earth
- Soil: Laterite (red, nutrient-poor — nutrients in living biomass, not soil)

**Aw — Tropical Savanna (Wet-and-Dry)**
- Found: 10°–20° latitude — Sub-Saharan Africa, interior Brazil (Cerrado), northern Australia
- Rainfall: Distinct wet season and dry season; 750–1,500 mm
- Vegetation: Tall grasses with scattered trees (Acacia, Baobab); home to iconic African wildlife
- Examples: African savanna (Serengeti), Brazilian Cerrado, Indian Deccan plateau fringe

### B — Dry Climates

**BWh — Hot Desert**
- Found: 20°–30° latitudes (near subtropical highs) — Sahara, Arabian, Thar, Atacama, Kalahari, Sonoran
- Rainfall: <250 mm/year; extremely variable
- Temperature: Hot days, cold nights (high diurnal range); can exceed 50°C
- Vegetation: Xerophytes (cacti, succulents), sparse or absent
- **Sahara** (Africa): Largest hot desert in world; ~9.2 million km²
- **Thar Desert** (India/Pakistan): Receives SW monsoon rains — technically not the driest

**BWk — Cold Desert**
- Found: Polar fringe and rain-shadow regions — Atacama (world's driest), Gobi, Patagonian
- **Atacama** (Chile/Peru): Driest non-polar desert; some stations average <1 mm/year
- **Gobi** (China/Mongolia): Cold desert; largest desert in Asia

**BSh/BSk — Steppe (Semi-arid)**
- Transitional zone at edges of deserts
- Short grasses; seasonal grazing

### C — Temperate Climates

**Csa/Csb — Mediterranean**
- Found: 30°–40° west coasts — Mediterranean Basin, California (USA), central Chile, SW Australia, SW South Africa (Cape)
- Rainfall: **Wet winters, dry summers** (distinctive pattern)
- Vegetation: Shrubland — **chaparral** (California), **maquis** (Mediterranean), **fynbos** (South Africa), **mallee** (Australia)
- Crops: Grapes, olives, citrus, wheat

**Cfa — Humid Subtropical**
- Found: 25°–35° east coasts — SE USA, SE South America (Pampas coast), coastal China, Japan, SE Australia
- Rainfall: Year-round; summer maximum; hot humid summers, mild winters
- Vegetation: Mixed forests, grasslands
- India: Northeastern states partly fall here

**Cfb — Marine West Coast**
- Found: 40°–60° west coasts — Western Europe, Pacific NW USA, New Zealand, southern Chile
- Cool summers, mild winters; year-round rain; overcast skies
- Vegetation: Temperate deciduous forest, grasslands

### D — Continental Climates

- Found: Interior of large continents, NH only (no large landmass at these latitudes in SH)
- **Dfa/Dfb:** Humid continental — NE USA, central Europe, Russia west of Urals
- **Dfc:** Subarctic (Taiga) — Siberia, Canada; short summers, long severe winters
- **Vegetation:** Taiga/Boreal forest — largest terrestrial biome; dominated by conifers (spruce, fir, pine)

### E — Polar Climates

**ET — Tundra**
- Found: Arctic Circle fringe, Antarctic Peninsula, high mountain tops
- Warmest month: 0–10°C; permafrost layer below surface
- Vegetation: Mosses, lichens, dwarf shrubs, sedges — no trees
- Animals: Polar bears, arctic foxes, migratory birds

**EF — Ice Cap**
- Found: Antarctica, Greenland interior
- Warmest month <0°C; permanent ice and snow
- Nearly no vegetation

## Major Biomes of the World

| Biome | Climate | Characteristic |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| **Tropical Rainforest** | Equatorial | Highest biodiversity; evergreen; 3+ canopy layers |
| **Tropical Savanna** | Tropical wet-dry | Grassland with trees; seasonal migration |
| **Desert** | Arid/Semi-arid | Xerophytes; extreme temperature range |
| **Mediterranean Shrubland** | Mediterranean | Fire-adapted; drought-resistant plants |
| **Temperate Grassland** | Semi-arid continental interior | Prairies (NA), Pampas (SA), Steppes (Eurasia), Veld (SA), Downs (Aus) |
| **Temperate Deciduous Forest** | Humid temperate | Broad-leaved trees shed leaves in winter |
| **Taiga/Boreal Forest** | Subarctic | Coniferous (evergreen needles); largest terrestrial biome by area |
| **Tundra** | Polar/Alpine | No trees; permafrost; brief summers |
| **Ice Cap** | Polar | Permanent ice; near-zero biodiversity |
| **Temperate Rainforest** | Marine west coast | Tall conifers; high rainfall; NW USA Pacific coast |

## Tropical Forests — Key Facts

- **Amazon Rainforest:** Largest rainforest; Brazil/Peru/Colombia; "lungs of the Earth"; largest by area and biodiversity
- **Congo Rainforest:** Second largest; Africa; DRC
- **Sundarbans:** World's largest mangrove forest; India/Bangladesh; Bengal Tiger habitat
- **Silent Valley:** Kerala; unique tropical evergreen forest; no roads cut through; National Park

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Sahara is the largest hot desert** (~9.2 million km²); **Antarctica** is the largest cold desert (~14 million km²) — SSC asks "largest desert in world" = Antarctica (by total area including ice cap).
2. **Mediterranean climate = wet winters, dry summers** — opposite of most climates; SSC loves this reversal.
3. **Atacama (Chile) is world's driest non-polar desert** — drier than Sahara by most measures.
4. **Taiga/Boreal forest is the largest terrestrial biome** by area, NOT tropical rainforest.
5. **Laterite soil** forms in tropical rainforest climate — nutrient-poor, red, leached by heavy rain.
6. **Chernozem (black soil)** of temperate grasslands (Steppes/Prairies) = world's most fertile.
7. **Fynbos** is the Mediterranean-type vegetation of South Africa's Cape region — not savanna.
8. **Amazon is NOT in Africa** — Congo is Africa's largest rainforest. Amazon is South America.
9. **Tundra has permafrost** — permanently frozen subsoil; prevents deep-rooted trees from growing.
10. **Mangroves grow in tropical coastal areas** (intertidal zones), not rainforest interiors.
11. **Gobi desert is in Asia** (China/Mongolia), not Africa; **Kalahari** is in southern Africa.
12. **Prairies = North America; Pampas = South America; Steppes = Eurasia; Veld = South Africa; Downs = Australia** — mix-ups are common SSC traps.
13. **EF (Ice Cap) climate in Antarctica** — warmest month below 0°C; not tundra (which has 0–10°C months).
14. **Hot deserts form at ~30° latitudes** (sub-tropical high pressure), not at the equator.
15. **Sundarbans** is the world's largest mangrove forest — NOT Australia or Amazon delta.
16. **Tropical Savanna** has wet and dry seasons; **Tropical Rainforest** has rain year-round — do not confuse.
