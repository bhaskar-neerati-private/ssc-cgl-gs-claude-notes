# Universe, Solar System & Earth's Structure

In 1930, a 24-year-old Kansas farmboy named Clyde Tombaugh sat at the Lowell Observatory in Arizona, blinking between two photographic plates taken days apart, searching for a tiny shifting dot. He found it — and for 76 years, Pluto was our ninth planet. Then in 2006, the International Astronomical Union voted it a "dwarf planet." The lesson: even the most fundamental facts of planetary science are periodically revised — and SSC loves testing the facts, the revisions, and the definitions that come out of those revisions.

---

## The Universe

- **Age:** ~13.8 billion years — origin in the **Big Bang**
- **Size:** Observable universe ~93 billion light-years in diameter
- **Composition:** ~68% dark energy, ~27% dark matter, ~5% ordinary (baryonic) matter
- **Galaxy types:** Spiral (Milky Way, Andromeda), Elliptical, Irregular
- **Milky Way:** Barred spiral galaxy; ~100,000 light-years across; ~200–400 billion stars; Sun is ~26,000 light-years from galactic centre; takes ~225 million years to complete one galactic orbit (cosmic year)
- **Nearest galaxy to Milky Way:** Canis Major Dwarf Galaxy (~25,000 ly); nearest large galaxy: Andromeda (~2.5 million ly)
- **Light-year:** Distance light travels in one year ≈ 9.46 × 10¹² km (NOT a unit of time)

## Stars and Their Life Cycle

| Stage | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Nebula | Cloud of gas and dust — stellar nursery |
| Protostar | Collapsing cloud begins nuclear fusion |
| Main Sequence | Stable phase (Sun is here); hydrogen → helium fusion |
| Red Giant | Hydrogen exhausted; outer layers expand |
| White Dwarf | Small star remnant after red giant sheds layers |
| Neutron Star / Pulsar | Remnant of massive star supernova |
| Black Hole | Remnant of very massive star; gravity so strong light cannot escape |

- **Sun's type:** Yellow dwarf (G-type main sequence star)
- **Nearest star to Earth:** Sun (150 million km); nearest star beyond Sun: Proxima Centauri (4.24 ly)
- **Brightest star in night sky:** Sirius (Dog Star) in Canis Major constellation

## The Solar System

**Formation:** ~4.6 billion years ago from a solar nebula  
**8 planets** (after Pluto demoted 2006 — IAU criteria: orbit Sun, hydrostatic equilibrium, cleared orbital neighbourhood)

### Inner/Terrestrial Planets (rocky, dense, slower)
| Planet | Key Facts |
|--------|-----------|
| Mercury | Smallest planet; no atmosphere; largest temperature range (−180°C to +430°C); 88-day year; no moons |
| Venus | Hottest planet (~465°C due to greenhouse effect); rotates east→west (retrograde); slowest rotation; brightest planet; Earth's twin in size; no moons |
| Earth | Only known life; one natural satellite (Moon); blue planet; 71% water |
| Mars | Red Planet (iron oxide); largest volcano Olympus Mons (21 km); longest canyon Valles Marineris; 2 moons (Phobos & Deimos); thin CO₂ atmosphere |

### Outer/Jovian Planets (gaseous/icy, large, faster orbital speeds)
| Planet | Key Facts |
|--------|-----------|
| Jupiter | Largest planet; Great Red Spot (storm 3× Earth's size); most moons (95 confirmed); faintest ring system; Ganymede = largest moon in Solar System |
| Saturn | Least dense planet (floats on water); most spectacular rings (ice + rock); 146 moons; Titan = largest moon, only moon with thick atmosphere |
| Uranus | Rotates on its side (axial tilt 97.8°); coldest atmosphere (−224°C); ice giant; faint rings; 27 moons |
| Neptune | Farthest planet; Great Dark Spot; strongest winds in Solar System (2,100 km/h); 16 moons; Triton orbits retrograde |

### Other Solar System Bodies
- **Asteroids:** Rocky bodies mainly in Asteroid Belt (between Mars and Jupiter)
- **Comets:** Ice + dust + rock; develop coma + tail when near Sun; tail always points away from Sun
  - Short-period comets: from Kuiper Belt; long-period: from Oort Cloud
  - **Halley's Comet:** ~76-year period; last perihelion 1986; next ~2061
- **Meteoroid → Meteor (shooting star) → Meteorite** (if reaches Earth's surface)
- **Kuiper Belt:** Beyond Neptune; source of short-period comets; Pluto is here
- **Oort Cloud:** Outermost shell; source of long-period comets

## The Sun

- **Distance from Earth:** ~150 million km (1 AU — Astronomical Unit)
- **Diameter:** ~1.4 million km (109× Earth's diameter)
- **Temperature:** Surface (photosphere) ~5,500°C; Corona (outer atmosphere) ~1–3 million°C
- **Energy source:** Nuclear fusion — hydrogen → helium (4 million tonnes converted to energy per second)
- **Layers:** Core → Radiative Zone → Convective Zone → Photosphere → Chromosphere → Corona
- **Solar phenomena:**
  - **Sunspots:** Cooler, darker regions; magnetic activity; 11-year sunspot cycle
  - **Solar flares:** Sudden energy bursts; can disrupt radio communication on Earth
  - **Solar wind:** Stream of charged particles; creates auroras (Aurora Borealis/Australis) at poles

## Earth's Internal Structure

| Layer | Depth | Composition | State |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------|
| **Crust** | 0–35 km (continental) / 0–10 km (oceanic) | Silica + Aluminium (Sial) / Silica + Magnesium (Sima) | Solid |
| **Mantle** | 35–2,900 km | Silicates of iron & magnesium; Olivine | Solid upper; viscous lower (asthenosphere) |
| **Outer Core** | 2,900–5,100 km | Iron + Nickel (liquid) | Liquid — generates Earth's magnetic field |
| **Inner Core** | 5,100–6,371 km | Iron + Nickel (solid) | Solid (high pressure despite high temperature) |

- **Mohorovičić Discontinuity (Moho):** Boundary between crust and mantle (~35 km depth under continents)
- **Gutenberg Discontinuity:** Boundary between mantle and outer core (~2,900 km)
- **Lehmann Discontinuity:** Boundary between outer and inner core (~5,100 km)
- **Earth's shape:** Oblate spheroid — slightly flattened at poles, bulging at equator; equatorial radius (6,378 km) > polar radius (6,357 km)
- **Geoid:** Earth's actual shape (irregular), defined by mean sea level

## Moon

- **Distance from Earth:** ~384,400 km (average)
- **Diameter:** ~3,474 km (about 1/4 of Earth)
- **No atmosphere, no water** (ice confirmed at poles in permanently shadowed craters)
- **Synchronous rotation:** Moon rotates on its axis in exactly the same time it revolves around Earth (~27.3 days) — so same face always visible (near side / far side)
- **Tidal locking:** Same phenomenon, same result as synchronous rotation
- **Tides:** Moon's gravity (and to lesser extent Sun) causes ocean bulges
  - **Spring tides:** Sun + Moon + Earth aligned (full/new moon) → highest high tides, lowest low tides
  - **Neap tides:** Sun, Moon at right angles (quarter moon) → smallest tidal range
- **Phases:** New Moon → Waxing Crescent → First Quarter → Waxing Gibbous → Full Moon → Waning Gibbous → Third Quarter → Waning Crescent → New Moon (~29.5 days = synodic month)
- **Lunar eclipse:** Earth between Sun and Moon (Full Moon only)
- **Solar eclipse:** Moon between Earth and Sun (New Moon only); total solar eclipse when Moon fully covers Sun's disc

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Venus is NOT the largest** — it's the hottest. Jupiter is the largest.
2. **Mercury is NOT the hottest** — Venus is, because Mercury has no atmosphere to trap heat.
3. **Light-year is a unit of distance**, not time.
4. **Pluto is a dwarf planet** since 2006 (IAU decision); the Solar System has **8 planets**.
5. **Nearest star to Earth is the Sun**, not Proxima Centauri (which is nearest beyond Sun).
6. **Comet tails always point away from the Sun** (pushed by solar wind), not the direction of travel.
7. **Saturn is the least dense planet** — less dense than water. Jupiter is largest but not least dense.
8. **Uranus has retrograde rotation** (east-to-west on its side); **Venus also rotates retrograde** but differently (rolls slowly east-to-west upright).
9. **Ganymede** (Jupiter's moon) is the **largest moon** in the Solar System, not Titan.
10. **Titan** (Saturn's moon) is the only moon with a **thick atmosphere**.
11. **Inner core is solid**, outer core is liquid — despite inner core being hotter (pressure keeps it solid).
12. **Aurora Borealis** = Northern Lights; **Aurora Australis** = Southern Lights — caused by solar wind hitting Earth's magnetic field at poles.
13. **Spring tides** occur at both new moon AND full moon — not just spring season.
14. **Moon's synchronous rotation** explains why we always see the same side — "far side" is not the dark side; it receives sunlight but faces away from Earth.
15. **Moho discontinuity** separates crust from mantle; **Gutenberg** separates mantle from outer core.
16. **Sirius** is the brightest star in the **night sky** — the Sun is the brightest star overall but we exclude it as it's daytime.
