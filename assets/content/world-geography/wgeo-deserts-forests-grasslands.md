# World Deserts, Forests & Grasslands

In 1911, the Norwegian explorer Roald Amundsen raced British captain Robert Falcon Scott to the South Pole across 2.5 million square kilometres of polar desert — the most hostile terrain on Earth. Antarctica, with less than 200 mm of annual precipitation, is technically the world's largest desert. When Amundsen won, Scott wrote in his diary: "Great God! This is an awful place." He was not wrong. But it was also, by every meteorological definition, a desert. This is the kind of definitional precision that SSC CGL geography tests repeatedly — the gap between what we imagine (sand dunes, camels) and what the science actually says.

---

## What is a Desert?

A desert is defined by **aridity** (dryness), not by temperature or sand.
- Annual precipitation: **<250 mm**
- **Hot deserts:** High temperatures, extreme diurnal range (day vs night), sand dunes + rocky terrain (reg/hamada)
- **Cold deserts:** Low temperatures year-round; sparse, cold, dry
- **Polar deserts:** Technically the driest deserts — precipitation falls as snow but total amounts are minimal

### Types of Desert Terrain
- **Erg:** Sandy desert with dunes (e.g., Sahara — only ~25% is erg)
- **Reg/Serrir:** Rocky flat plains (most common in Sahara)
- **Hamada:** Rocky plateau desert
- **Oasis:** Area with water and vegetation in desert; typically over underground aquifer

### India's Thar Desert
- Located in Rajasthan and extending into Pakistan (Sind)
- Receives some SW monsoon rains — makes it less arid than Sahara/Arabian
- Rich in mineral resources (lignite, uranium, mineral salts, gypsum)
- **Indira Gandhi Canal** (Rajasthan Canal) — world's longest irrigation canal; brings Himalayan water to Thar

## World's Major Forests

### Tropical Rainforests
- **Location:** Within 10° of equator — Amazon Basin, Congo Basin, SE Asian archipelago
- **Characteristics:** Year-round warmth + high rainfall; multi-layered canopy (emergent, canopy, understorey, forest floor); highest biodiversity
- **Amazon:** ~5.5 million km²; Brazil (~60%), Peru, Colombia; deforestation rate: ~10,000 km²/year; "lungs of the Earth" (produces ~20% of world's oxygen)
- **Congo:** ~1.8 million km²; Central Africa; DRC has most; 2nd largest tropical rainforest
- **SE Asian:** Indonesia (Borneo, Sumatra), Malaysia, Myanmar — severely threatened by palm oil plantations

### Tropical Dry Forests
- Seasonal; lose leaves in dry season
- India: Deccan plateau, parts of the Western Ghats rain shadow

### Temperate Rainforests
- High rainfall + mild temperatures year-round; west coasts in temperate latitudes
- **Pacific NW USA:** Hoh Rainforest (Olympic National Park); giant conifers (Sitka spruce, Douglas fir)
- **Chilean temperate rainforest:** Alerce trees (Fitzroya) — among world's longest-lived organisms
- **New Zealand:** Podocarp forests

### Boreal Forests (Taiga)
- **Location:** 50°–70°N; Russia, Canada, Alaska, Scandinavia
- **Area:** ~12 million km² — **largest terrestrial biome**
- **Dominant species:** Conifers (spruce, fir, pine, larch) — needleleaf, evergreen (except larch)
- **Climate:** Long cold winters, short cool summers; poorly drained soils; permafrost in many areas
- **Russia's Siberian taiga** = single largest forest on Earth
- **Products:** Timber, pulp and paper; important carbon sink

### Temperate Deciduous Forests
- **Location:** 40°–60°N; Eastern USA, Western Europe, NE China, Japan
- **Dominant species:** Oak, maple, beech, birch, elm — broad-leaved; lose leaves in autumn
- **Seasons:** Four distinct seasons; rich humus; fertile brown soils

### Mangrove Forests
- **Location:** Tropical/subtropical coasts; intertidal zones; estuaries
- **Adaptations:** Salt tolerance; prop roots/pneumatophores (breathing roots) above waterline
- **Sundarbans:** ~10,000 km²; largest mangrove forest in world; India/Bangladesh; Bengal Tiger
- **India's mangroves:** Sundarbans (West Bengal), Bhitarkanika (Odisha), Gulf of Kutch, Andaman & Nicobar
- **Ecological role:** Coastal protection against tsunamis/cyclones; fish nurseries; carbon storage

## World's Major Grasslands

### Tropical Grasslands (Savannas)
- Between 10°–20° latitudes; distinct wet and dry seasons
- **African Savanna:** Serengeti (Tanzania/Kenya); Masai Mara; world's largest land animal migration (wildebeest/zebra)
- **Brazilian Cerrado:** South American tropical savanna; second most biodiverse biome after Amazon
- **Llanos:** Venezuela/Colombia — seasonally flooded savanna

### Temperate Grasslands
- **No trees; fertile chernozem (black) soil; moderate rainfall (250–750 mm)**

- **Prairies:** Wheat belt of North America; rich black/dark brown soils
- **Pampas:** Cattle ranching and wheat; Argentina is major beef exporter
- **Steppes:** Ukraine = "breadbasket of Europe"; black earth (chernozem) soil

## Deforestation and Conservation

- **Deforestation hotspots:** Amazon (Brazil), Congo Basin, SE Asia (Indonesia, Malaysia)
- **Causes:** Agriculture (slash-and-burn, palm oil), logging, cattle ranching, infrastructure
- **Effects:** Loss of biodiversity, soil erosion, altered rainfall patterns, carbon release → climate change
- **Protected areas:** National parks, wildlife sanctuaries, biosphere reserves, UNESCO World Heritage Sites
- **CITES:** Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (1973) — protects species from overexploitation through trade

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Antarctic desert is the world's largest desert** — NOT Sahara; Sahara is the largest **hot** desert.
2. **Atacama (Chile) is the driest non-polar desert** — some stations average <1 mm/year.
3. **Rub' al Khali is the world's largest continuous sand desert** (part of Arabian Desert) — not the whole Sahara.
4. **Gobi is in China and Mongolia** — a cold desert, NOT a hot sandy desert.
5. **Kalahari is semi-arid**, not a true desert — it receives more rainfall than classic deserts.
6. **Namib desert is coastal** — cooled by Benguela Current; one of world's oldest deserts.
7. **Boreal/Taiga is the largest terrestrial biome**, covering ~12 million km² — not tropical rainforest.
8. **Amazon provides ~20% of world's oxygen** (or "20% of world's freshwater discharge") — but "lungs of Earth" is a popular term SSC may use.
9. **Congo is the 2nd largest tropical rainforest**, not the largest (that's Amazon).
10. **Sundarbans is the world's largest mangrove forest** — located in India/Bangladesh; Bengal Tiger habitat.
11. **Chernozem (black soil) of temperate grasslands** is the world's most fertile — not the laterite of tropics.
12. **Prairies = North America** (Great Plains); don't confuse with Pampas (South America).
13. **Thar desert is in India AND Pakistan** — not just India (extends into Pakistani Punjab and Sind).
14. **Taiga trees are conifers (needle-leaf)** — except larch, which is deciduous (needle-leaf but drops needles).
15. **Mangroves grow in intertidal zones** (coastal, saline water), not freshwater or interior forests.
16. **Serengeti/Masai Mara** are in Tanzania/Kenya — not South Africa (that's Kruger National Park).
