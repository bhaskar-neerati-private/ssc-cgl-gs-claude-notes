# Geographical Superlatives & Key Terms

In 1856, the Great Trigonometric Survey of India calculated the height of a distant Himalayan peak and found it taller than any mountain previously measured. They named it Peak XV. It was later renamed after George Everest, the Surveyor General who led the survey — though Everest himself never saw the mountain or set foot in Nepal. Today that mountain, remeasured most recently in 2020 at 8,848.86 m (now conventionally stated as 8,849 m), is the most tested number in SSC geography. But it is just one of dozens of superlatives — the longest, deepest, largest, smallest, hottest, coldest — that SSC examiners love to put in question stems.

## Key Geographical Terms

### Latitude and Longitude Terms
- **Equator:** 0° latitude; longest circle around Earth
- **Prime Meridian:** 0° longitude; through Greenwich, London
- **International Date Line (IDL):** ~180° longitude; where date changes
- **Tropics:** Tropic of Cancer (23.5°N) and Tropic of Capricorn (23.5°S)
- **Polar Circles:** Arctic Circle (66.5°N), Antarctic Circle (66.5°S)

### Coastal and Marine Terms
- **Gulf:** Large inlet of sea/ocean partially enclosed by land (e.g., Persian Gulf, Gulf of Mexico)
- **Bay:** Smaller than gulf; curved indentation (e.g., Bay of Bengal, Hudson Bay)
- **Strait:** Narrow passage between two large water bodies (e.g., Strait of Gibraltar)
- **Cape:** Pointed piece of land projecting into the sea (e.g., Cape of Good Hope, Cape Comorin/Kanyakumari)
- **Peninsula:** Land surrounded by water on three sides (e.g., Indian Peninsula, Arabian Peninsula)
- **Isthmus:** Narrow strip of land connecting two larger land masses (e.g., Isthmus of Panama)
- **Lagoon:** Shallow stretch of water separated from sea by coral reef or sandbank
- **Atoll:** Ring-shaped coral reef enclosing a lagoon
- **Fjord:** Long, narrow, deep inlet carved by glacier; steep sides (Norway, New Zealand, Chile)
- **Delta:** Triangular depositional feature at river mouth
- **Estuary:** Funnel-shaped mouth; tidal influence; mixed freshwater-seawater

### Landform Terms
- **Plateau:** Elevated flatland (tableland); flat top, steep sides
- **Mesa:** Isolated plateau with steep sides
- **Butte:** Smaller than mesa; isolated hill with steep sides
- **Escarpment:** Long steep slope at edge of plateau
- **Valley:** Low area between hills/mountains; carved by rivers (V-shape) or glaciers (U-shape)
- **Gorge/Canyon:** Deep narrow valley with steep rocky walls
- **Alluvial fan/plain:** Sediment deposited where river meets flat land
- **Peneplain:** Almost flat land produced by long-term erosion
- **Moraine:** Debris deposited by glacier (terminal = at glacier front)
- **Cirque:** Amphitheatre-shaped hollow carved by glacier (also called Corrie or Cwm)
- **Arête:** Narrow ridge between two cirques
- **Horn/Pyramidal peak:** Steep rocky peak carved by glaciers from multiple sides (e.g., Matterhorn)

### Rock and Soil Terms
- **Igneous rock:** From cooled magma/lava (granite, basalt)
- **Sedimentary rock:** Formed from sediment compaction (sandstone, limestone, shale)
- **Metamorphic rock:** Changed by heat/pressure (marble from limestone, quartzite from sandstone)
- **Laterite soil:** Red, leached, tropical soil; low fertility; formed in hot wet climate
- **Chernozem:** Black soil of temperate grasslands; highly fertile
- **Regur (Black cotton soil):** India's Deccan Plateau; good for cotton; swells when wet
- **Alluvial soil:** Deposited by rivers; very fertile; Indo-Gangetic Plain

### Weather and Atmospheric Terms
- **Orographic rainfall:** Caused by moist air rising over mountains
- **Convectional rainfall:** Rising warm air causing thunderstorms; tropics
- **Frontal rainfall:** Along boundaries of two air masses; temperate regions
- **Monsoon:** Seasonal reversal of winds (from sea in summer, from land in winter)
- **Foehn/Chinook:** Warm dry wind on leeward side of mountains (Alps/Rockies)
- **Sirocco:** Hot dry wind from Sahara toward Mediterranean
- **Mistral:** Cold dry wind from Alps toward Mediterranean (France)
- **Harmattan:** Dry dusty wind from Sahara into West Africa
- **Bora:** Cold dry wind from Adriatic toward coast

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Deepest lake = Baikal (1,642 m)** — NOT Caspian Sea (which is largest lake by area).
2. **Largest lake by area = Caspian Sea** — but it's considered a lake AND a sea; **largest freshwater lake = Lake Superior**.
3. **Mawsynram (not Cherrapunji) holds the record for highest average annual rainfall** — Cherrapunji holds highest single-year records. Both in Meghalaya.
4. **Angel Falls (Venezuela) is the world's tallest waterfall** — Victoria Falls is widest; Niagara is most visited.
5. **Hottest place = Death Valley, California** (56.7°C) — NOT Sahara or any African location.
6. **Coldest place = Vostok Station, Antarctica** (−89.2°C) — NOT Siberia (though Oymyakon is coldest inhabited).
7. **Greenland is the largest island** — Australia is a continent, not counted as an island.
8. **Tibetan Plateau is both the highest AND the largest plateau** — NOT Deccan (which is India's largest).
9. **Mid-Atlantic Ridge is the longest mountain range** — but it's underwater; Andes is longest on land.
10. **Lake Titicaca is the highest navigable lake** (3,812 m) — Lake Baikal is deepest but NOT highest.
11. **Isthmus of Panama** connects North and South America — Panama Canal was cut through it.
12. **Fjords = Norway** (also New Zealand and Chile) — NOT India or Africa.
13. **Sundarbans Delta is the world's largest delta** — NOT the Nile Delta or Amazon.
14. **Dead Sea shore is the lowest point on land** (~430 m below sea level) — Mariana Trench is deepest underwater point.
15. **Suez Canal has NO LOCKS** (sea-level canal); **Panama Canal has LOCKS** — very commonly tested.
16. **Kunchikal Falls (Karnataka) is India's highest waterfall** — NOT Jog Falls (which is most famous but 4th highest in India).
