# World Resources, Industries & Trade Routes

In 1859, Edwin Drake drilled the world's first commercial oil well in Titusville, Pennsylvania — just 21 metres deep. By 1973, oil had become so central to the global economy that when OPEC nations imposed an embargo, petrol queues stretched for miles in the United States and the stock market collapsed worldwide. A 21-metre hole in Pennsylvania had reorganised the entire planet's industrial geography. Today, the question of where resources are found, where they are processed, and how they move determines which nations hold power — and which SSC questions you will encounter.

## Energy Resources

### Oil (Petroleum)

| Region/Country | Notes |
|----------------|-------|
| **Saudi Arabia** | Largest proven reserves; Ghawar — world's largest oil field; OPEC founder |
| **Venezuela** | 2nd largest proven reserves (heavy crude) |
| **Russia** | Major producer; Siberian fields; Gazprom |
| **Iraq, Kuwait, UAE, Iran** | Major Middle East producers |
| **USA** | World's largest producer (shale oil revolution); Permian Basin |
| **Nigeria, Angola** | Africa's top producers |
| **India** | Mumbai High (offshore); Rajasthan (Barmer); net importer |

- **OPEC** (Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries): Founded 1960; Headquarters Vienna, Austria; 13 members; controls ~40% of world oil production
- **World's largest oil exporter:** Saudi Arabia
- **Strait of Hormuz** carries ~20% of global oil trade

### Natural Gas
- Russia has the world's **largest natural gas reserves** (Siberia — Gazprom)
- Qatar is the world's **largest LNG (Liquefied Natural Gas) exporter**
- USA became largest gas producer due to shale gas (fracking)

### Coal

| Type | Uses |
|------|------|
| Anthracite | Highest grade; purest; highest carbon content; used for heating |
| Bituminous | Most abundant; steel production (coking coal); power generation |
| Lignite (Brown Coal) | Lowest grade; high moisture; used in power plants |
| Peat | Partially decayed organic matter; lowest energy; Ireland, Finland |

- **Top coal producers:** China (by far the largest), India, USA, Australia, Indonesia
- **India's coal reserves:** Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, West Bengal (Damodar Valley)
- **Appalachian coalfields:** USA; **Ruhr Valley:** Germany (coal + steel); **Donbass:** Ukraine

### Iron Ore
- **World's top producers:** Australia (#1), Brazil (#2), China, India
- **Largest iron ore mine:** Carajás, Brazil (Vale company)
- **India's iron ore:** Odisha (Keonjhar-Sundergarh), Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh, Karnataka (Bellary-Hospet)

| Mineral | Top Producers | Uses |
|---------|--------------|------|
| **Copper** | Chile (#1, Escondida mine), Peru, DRC, China | Electrical wiring, electronics |
| **Bauxite (Aluminium ore)** | Australia (#1), Guinea, Brazil, India | Aluminium production |

| **Mica** | India (#1 globally) — Jharkhand, Andhra Pradesh | Electrical insulation, cosmetics |

## Major Industries and Their Locations

### Iron and Steel Industry
- Located near: coal + iron ore + water + transportation
- **Pittsburgh (USA):** Appalachian coal + Great Lakes iron ore → Steel City
- **Ruhr Valley (Germany):** Coal + Rhine transport; Europe's steel heartland
- **South Wales, Sheffield (UK):** Historical steel centres
- **Jamshedpur (India):** TATA Steel; Damodar coal + Jharkhand iron ore
- **Bhilai, Durgapur, Rourkela (India):** Public sector steel plants; set up with Soviet, UK, German help

### Textile Industry
- **Cotton Textile:**
  - **Lancashire, UK:** Historical cotton-spinning heartland (18th–19th century Industrial Revolution)
  - **Manchester, UK:** "Cottonopolis"
  - **Ahmedabad, India:** "Manchester of India"; cotton mills on Sabarmati
  - **Mumbai (Bombay), India:** Another historical cotton hub
- **Silk Textile:**
  - **Lyon, France:** Historical silk capital
  - **China:** World's largest silk producer; Suzhou, Hangzhou
  - **Mysuru/Bangalore, India:** Major silk production

### Automobile Industry
- **Detroit, USA:** "Motor City"; Ford, GM, Chrysler (Big Three)
- **Stuttgart/Munich, Germany:** BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Porsche
- **Toyota City (Nagoya), Japan:** Toyota; Japan = historically 2nd largest car manufacturer
- **South Korea:** Hyundai (Ulsan)
- **China:** World's largest car producer and consumer

### Shipbuilding
- **South Korea:** #1 in shipbuilding (Hyundai Heavy Industries, DSME); major ports Ulsan, Busan
- **China:** World's largest shipbuilder by tonnage
- **Japan:** Historically dominant; still major player
- **India:** Vishakhapatnam (RINL), Mumbai (Mazagon Dock — defence ships)

### IT / Software
- **Silicon Valley, USA (San Jose/San Francisco area):** Global tech hub; Google, Apple, Meta, Intel
- **Bangalore (Bengaluru), India:** "Silicon Valley of India"; Infosys, Wipro, TCS HQs; #1 IT city India
- **Seattle, USA:** Microsoft, Amazon headquarters
- **Hyderabad (HITEC City), India:** Major IT hub; Microsoft, Google offices
- **Shenzhen, China:** Electronics manufacturing; hardware tech

## World Trade Routes

### Maritime (Sea) Trade Routes

| Route | Key Passage | Significance |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **Europe → Asia** | Suez Canal | Shortest Europe-Asia sea route; oil, containers |
| **USA East Coast → Asia** | Panama Canal | Shortcut Atlantic-Pacific; containers |
| **Asia → Europe (alternate)** | Cape of Good Hope | If Suez Canal blocked; longer |
| **Americas → SE Asia** | Drake Passage / Magellan Strait | Southern alternatives (rare) |
| **Intra-Asia** | Strait of Malacca | Busiest; Middle East oil → East Asia |
| **Persian Gulf → World** | Strait of Hormuz | Oil chokepoint |

### Chokepoints of World Trade

| Chokepoint | Location | Volume |
|------------|----------|--------|
| **Strait of Malacca** | SE Asia | ~25% of world trade; busiest shipping lane |
| **Suez Canal** | Egypt | ~12-15% of world trade; 12% of global oil |
| **Strait of Hormuz** | Persian Gulf | ~20% of world's petroleum |
| **Panama Canal** | Panama | ~3-5% of world trade |
| **Bab-el-Mandeb** | Red Sea entry | Yemen-Djibouti; key to Suez route |
| **Strait of Dover** | English Channel | Narrowest part; dense European shipping |

### Air Transport Hubs
- **Busiest airport by passengers:** Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta (USA) / Dubai International (UAE) — varies by year
- **Busiest by cargo:** Hong Kong International, Memphis (FedEx hub)
- **India's busiest airport:** Indira Gandhi International Airport, Delhi

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **China produces the most coal** in the world — not India or USA.
2. **Australia is the #1 iron ore producer** (not Brazil, which is #2).
3. **OPEC headquarters is in Vienna, Austria** — not Riyadh or Geneva.
4. **Qatar is the largest LNG exporter** — not Russia (Russia has largest reserves).
5. **Jamshedpur (Tata Steel) uses Damodar Valley coal + Jharkhand iron ore** — understand the geographic logic of iron & steel location.
6. **Ahmedabad is "Manchester of India"** — NOT Mumbai (which was also a cotton city but Ahmedabad gets this specific title).
7. **Bangalore is "Silicon Valley of India"** — NOT Hyderabad (HITEC City) or Pune.
8. **Brazil is the world's #1 coffee producer** — NOT Colombia or Ethiopia (though both are famous).
9. **Bangladesh is the #1 jute producer** — India is #2. Do not confuse with India being the top processor.
10. **Diamond production:** Russia is #1 by volume; **Botswana is #1 by value** (gem-quality diamonds).
11. **Lithium Triangle** (Chile, Argentina, Bolivia) has the world's largest lithium brine deposits — but Australia now produces the most lithium.
12. **Suez Canal has no locks** — it's at sea level; **Panama Canal has locks** — lifts ships up and over.
13. **Strait of Malacca is the busiest strait** in the world for shipping.
14. **Lancashire (UK) + Manchester** = historical cotton; now India and China dominate cotton textiles.
15. **India is the world's #1 mica producer** — Jharkhand and Andhra Pradesh.
16. **South Korea (#1) beats Japan in shipbuilding** — a frequent mix-up in SSC questions about Asian industry leaders.
