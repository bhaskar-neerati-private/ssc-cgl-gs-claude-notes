# World Political Geography — Countries, Capitals & Borders

On the night of November 9, 1989, East German guards stood aside and let citizens tear down the Berlin Wall — and overnight, the political map of Europe changed. What had been two countries became one. What had been the USSR dissolved in 1991 into 15 nations. The world political map is not geology; it shifts with history. As recently as 2011, South Sudan became the world's newest nation. The SSC CGL examiner knows this history well — and questions test not just capitals, but borders, landlocked nations, island nations, and the geographic superlatives that define political boundaries.

---

## World Political Overview

- **Total countries in world:** 195 (193 UN members + 2 observer states: Vatican City and Palestine)
- **Newest nation:** South Sudan (independence July 9, 2011 from Sudan)
- **Largest country by area:** Russia (~17.1 million km²)
- **Smallest country by area:** Vatican City (~0.44 km²)
- **Most populous country:** India (surpassed China in 2023, per UN estimates)
- **Least populous country:** Vatican City
- **Most countries in a continent:** Africa (54 countries)

## Special Geographic Terms

### Landlocked Countries
- Countries with no ocean coast
- **Doubly landlocked** (surrounded entirely by landlocked countries): Only two in world — **Liechtenstein** (Europe) and **Uzbekistan** (Central Asia)
- **Largest landlocked country:** Kazakhstan (~2.7 million km²)
- **Most populous landlocked country:** Ethiopia (~125 million)
- India's landlocked neighbours: Nepal, Bhutan, Afghanistan (no sea coast)

### Island Nations in Indian Ocean
- Maldives, Sri Lanka, Madagascar, Mauritius, Seychelles, Comoros, Réunion (French), Andaman & Nicobar (India)

### Countries on Multiple Continents (Transcontinental)
- **Russia:** Europe + Asia (Ural Mountains = boundary)
- **Turkey:** Europe (Thrace) + Asia (Anatolia); divided by Bosphorus
- **Egypt:** Africa + Asia (Sinai Peninsula)
- **Kazakhstan:** Europe + Asia

### Countries with Multiple Capitals
| Country | Capitals | Reason |
|---------|----------|--------|
| South Africa | Pretoria / Cape Town / Bloemfontein | Three branches of government |
| Bolivia | Sucre / La Paz | Constitutional + de facto seat |
| Netherlands | Amsterdam / The Hague | Capital city / seat of government |
| Malaysia | Kuala Lumpur / Putrajaya | Original / administrative capital |

## Important Borders and Lines

| Line | Separates | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **Durand Line** | India/Pakistan-Afghanistan (now Pakistan-Afghanistan) | Drawn by Mortimer Durand 1893; Afghanistan disputes it |
| **Radcliffe Line** | India-Pakistan (and India-Bangladesh) | Drawn by Cyril Radcliffe; 1947 Partition |
| **McMahon Line** | India-China (Arunachal Pradesh) | Drawn 1914; China disputes it |
| **Line of Actual Control (LAC)** | India-China (current de facto boundary) | Disputed; not surveyed or agreed |
| **Line of Control (LoC)** | India-Pakistan (J&K) | De facto boundary in Kashmir since 1972 |
| **17th Parallel** | North and South Vietnam (1954–1975) | Divided Vietnam after Geneva Accords |
| **38th Parallel** | North and South Korea | Divided Korea at Korean War ceasefire |
| **49th Parallel** | USA-Canada | De facto boundary from Lake of the Woods to Pacific |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Pakistan's capital is Islamabad**, NOT Karachi (Karachi is the largest city and former capital).
2. **Australia is both a country and a continent** — largest country in Oceania; 6th largest in world.
3. **Smallest country is Vatican City** (0.44 km²), NOT Monaco or San Marino.
4. **South Sudan** (2011) is the world's newest independent nation — NOT Kosovo (2008) or East Timor (2002).
5. **Brazil's capital is Brasília**, NOT São Paulo or Rio de Janeiro (Rio was former capital).
6. **South Africa has THREE capitals** — Pretoria (executive), Cape Town (legislative), Bloemfontein (judicial).
7. **Turkey's capital is Ankara**, NOT Istanbul (which is largest city and former capital as Constantinople).
8. **Doubly landlocked: only Liechtenstein and Uzbekistan** — surrounded entirely by landlocked countries.
9. **Kazakhstan is the world's largest landlocked country** (not Mongolia or Bolivia).
10. **Sri Lanka's capital is Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte**, NOT Colombo (Colombo is commercial capital).
11. **McMahon Line** separates India and China; **Radcliffe Line** separates India and Pakistan — do NOT confuse.
12. **38th Parallel** divides North and South Korea; **17th Parallel** divided North and South Vietnam.
13. **Algeria is the largest country in Africa by area** (since South Sudan split from Sudan in 2011).
14. **Dubai is not the capital of UAE** — Abu Dhabi is the capital.
15. **Myanmar's capital is Naypyidaw** (since 2006, moved from Yangon/Rangoon).
16. **Ethiopia has the most populous city in landlocked countries** — Addis Ababa; also hosts African Union HQ.
