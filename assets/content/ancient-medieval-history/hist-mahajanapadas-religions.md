# Mahajanapadas, Rise of Buddhism & Jainism

## Two Princes Who Walked Away From Power

Around 599 BCE, a prince named Vardhamana Mahavira was born in Vaishali (Bihar). Around 563 BCE (traditional date), a prince named Siddhartha Gautama was born in Lumbini (Nepal). Both would renounce royal privilege, undergo years of rigorous asceticism and meditation, and emerge with teachings that would eventually reach hundreds of millions of people on every continent. Both rejected the Vedic sacrificial system. Both rejected the authority of Brahmins as gatekeepers to spiritual liberation. Both taught that the path to freedom lay in individual effort, ethical conduct, and control of the mind — not in expensive rituals or priestly intercession.

The timing was not accidental. The 6th–5th centuries BCE were a period of rapid urbanisation and mercantile expansion in northern India. A new class of wealthy traders (Vaishyas) had the means but not the spiritual prestige granted by Brahminical religion. Buddhism and Jainism offered liberation to all — regardless of birth.

## Part 1 — The Sixteen Mahajanapadas (c. 600–325 BCE)

By 600 BCE, the Later Vedic tribal units had consolidated into 16 major kingdoms and republics called **Mahajanapadas** (great realms/footings).

| # | Mahajanapada | Capital | Modern Location |
|---|-------------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | **Magadha** | Rajagriha (later Pataliputra) | Bihar (most powerful) |
| 2 | **Kosala** | Shravasti | Eastern UP |
| 3 | **Vatsa** | Kaushambi | Allahabad area, UP |
| 4 | **Avanti** | Ujjain (N.) / Mahishmati (S.) | Madhya Pradesh |
| 5 | **Kuru** | Hastinapur/Indraprastha | Delhi/Haryana area |
| 6 | **Panchala** | Ahichhatra/Kampilya | Western UP |
| 7 | **Matsya** | Viratanagara | Rajasthan |
| 8 | **Shurasena** | Mathura | Western UP |
| 9 | **Chedi** | Shuktimati | MP/UP border |
| 10 | **Kashi** | Varanasi | UP |
| 11 | **Anga** | Champa | Eastern Bihar/Bengal |
| 12 | **Vajji (Vrijji)** | Vaishali | Bihar (famous republic) |
| 13 | **Malla** | Kushinara/Pava | Eastern UP/Bihar |
| 14 | **Chedi** | — | Bundelkhand |
| 15 | **Gandhara** | Taxila | Pakistan/Afghanistan |
| 16 | **Kamboja** | Rajapura (Rajauri) | Afghanistan/NW Frontier |

**Key facts:**
- **Magadha** became the most powerful, eventually absorbing all others under the Mauryas
- **Vajji** (capital Vaishali) was the world's earliest known republic — a confederacy of clans with an elected council
- **Kashi, Kosala, Magadha, Avanti** were the four most important rivals in the 6th century BCE
- Buddha died at **Kushinara** (Malla Mahajanapada); born in Lumbini (Kosala periphery)

### Why Magadha Was Supreme
1. **Natural advantages**: rich iron deposits of south Bihar → strong weapons
2. **Fertile land**: Ganga-Son confluence; productive agriculture
3. **Strategic location**: controlled trade routes
4. **Strong rulers**: Bimbisara, Ajatashatru, Mahapadma Nanda, Chandragupta Maurya

## Part 2 — Buddhism

### Life of Gautama Buddha

| Event | Traditional Date | Place |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **Birth** | 563 BCE | Lumbini (Nepal) — on Vaisakh Purnima |
| **Four Sights** | — | Left palace after seeing old man, sick man, corpse, monk |
| **Renunciation (Mahabhinishkramana)** | 29 years old | Kapilavastu |
| **Enlightenment (Nirvana/Bodhi)** | 528 BCE | Bodh Gaya (under Peepal/Bodhi tree) |
| **First Sermon (Dhammachakkapavattana Sutta)** | — | **Deer Park, Sarnath** (near Varanasi) — "Turning of the Wheel of Dharma" |
| **Death (Mahaparinirvana)** | 483 BCE | **Kushinara** (Kushinagar, UP) |

**Buddha's original name**: Siddhartha Gautama; clan name Shakya; also called **Shakyamuni** (Sage of the Shakyas) and **Tathagata** (Thus-gone one)

### The Four Noble Truths (Arya Satya)
1. **Dukkha** — Life is suffering/unsatisfactory
2. **Samudaya** — Suffering arises from craving/desire (tanha)
3. **Nirodha** — Cessation of suffering is possible (Nirvana)
4. **Magga** — The Eightfold Path leads to cessation of suffering

### The Eightfold Path (Ashtangika Marga)
Right Understanding → Right Thought → Right Speech → Right Action → Right Livelihood → Right Effort → Right Mindfulness → Right Concentration

**Middle Path**: between extreme asceticism and extreme indulgence — the balanced way

### Buddhist Concepts
- **Nirvana**: liberation from the cycle of rebirth — extinction of desire and ego
- **Triratna** (Three Jewels): **Buddha** (the teacher), **Dhamma** (the teaching), **Sangha** (the monastic community)
- **Anatta** (no-self): no permanent, unchanging soul — rebirth continues due to karma
- **Anicca** (impermanence): all phenomena are transient
- **Ahimsa**: non-violence; rejected Vedic animal sacrifice
- **Karma**: actions determine future births

### Buddhist Councils

| Council | When | Where | King | Significance |
|---------|------|-------|------|-------------|
| **1st** | 483 BCE (after Buddha's death) | Rajagriha | Ajatashatru | Compiled Vinaya Pitaka (monastic rules) & Sutta Pitaka (discourses) |
| **2nd** | 383 BCE | Vaishali | Kalasoka | Split between orthodox Sthaviras and reform Mahasanghikas |
| **3rd** | 250 BCE | Pataliputra | **Ashoka** | Compilation of Abhidhamma Pitaka; sent missionaries abroad |
| **4th** | 1st century CE | Kashmir | **Kanishka** | Division into Mahayana and Hinayana |

### Buddhist Texts — Tripitaka (Three Baskets)

| Pitaka | Content |
|--------|---------|
| **Vinaya Pitaka** | Monastic rules and discipline |
| **Sutta Pitaka** | Buddha's discourses; includes Dhammapada (most famous) |
| **Abhidhamma Pitaka** | Philosophical analysis; metaphysics |

### Schools of Buddhism

| School | Also Called | Key Belief | Spread |
|--------|------------|-----------|--------|
| **Hinayana** | Theravada | Individual liberation; Buddha as teacher (not god); follows original Pali canon | Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand |
| **Mahayana** | Greater Vehicle | Bodhisattvas (enlightened beings) help all beings; Buddha as divine | China, Japan, Korea, Tibet |
| **Vajrayana** | Tantric Buddhism | Ritual, mantra, mandala; esoteric practices | Tibet, Nepal, Bhutan |

## Part 3 — Jainism

### Life of Mahavira (the 24th Tirthankara)

| Event | Detail |
|-------|-------|
| **Birth** | 599 BCE (or 540 BCE — disputed); Vaishali (Bihar) |
| **Clan** | Licchavi; Kshatriya; father Siddhartha (Vajji chief); mother Trishala |
| **Renunciation** | At 30 years old |
| **Enlightenment (Kevala Jnana)** | After 12 years of rigorous asceticism; at Jrimbhikagrama |
| **Death** | 527 BCE; Pavapuri (near Rajgir, Bihar) |
| **Title** | **Mahavira** ("Great Hero"), **Jina** ("Conqueror" — origin of "Jainism"), **Nirgrantha** ("free from bonds") |

Mahavira was the **24th and last Tirthankara** (ford-maker/crossing-maker). The 23rd was **Parshvanatha** (9th century BCE — historically accepted as real).

### The Five Great Vows (Pancha Mahavratas) — for monks
1. **Ahimsa** — Non-violence (the most important Jain principle)
2. **Satya** — Truthfulness
3. **Asteya** — Non-stealing
4. **Brahmacharya** — Celibacy (added by Mahavira; Parshvanatha had only 4 vows)
5. **Aparigraha** — Non-possessiveness

### Key Jain Doctrines
- **Anekantavada** (many-sidedness of truth): reality is complex; no single viewpoint captures the whole truth
- **Syadvada** (maybe/conditional predication): all philosophical claims are conditional
- **Karma**: physical particles that stick to the soul based on actions; liberation requires shedding karma
- **Triratna** (Three Jewels of Jainism): **Right Faith**, **Right Knowledge**, **Right Conduct**
- **Ahimsa** as the supreme principle — Jains strain water before drinking to avoid killing micro-organisms; some wear masks; sweep the path before walking

### Jain Sects

| Sect | Origin | Difference |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Digambara** (sky-clad) | Remained in Deccan | Monks go completely naked; women cannot attain liberation |
| **Shvetambara** (white-clad) | Moved to Gujarat/Rajasthan | White robes; women can attain liberation |

Split occurred around 3rd century BCE / 1st century CE when a section moved south during a famine.

## Comparison: Buddhism vs Jainism

| Feature | Buddhism | Jainism |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Founder | Siddhartha Gautama | Mahavira (24th Tirthankara) |
| Birth place | Lumbini (Nepal) | Vaishali (Bihar) |
| Enlightenment | Bodh Gaya (Bihar) | Jrimbhikagrama (Bihar) |
| Death | Kushinara (UP) | Pavapuri (Bihar) |
| On God | Agnostic (neither confirmed nor denied) | No creator God; universe is eternal |
| Caste | Rejected birth-based caste | Rejected but absorbed into Jain society |
| Key principle | Middle Path; Four Noble Truths | Ahimsa; extreme asceticism |
| Spread | Sri Lanka, SE Asia, China, Japan | Mostly within India (especially Rajasthan, Gujarat) |
| Texts | Tripitaka (Pali); Sanskrit texts | Agamas (12 Angas); different in two sects |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Buddha's First Sermon was at Sarnath** (Deer Park, near Varanasi) — called Dhammachakkapavattana Sutta. His Enlightenment was at **Bodh Gaya**. His death (Mahaparinirvana) was at **Kushinara**. Birth at **Lumbini**. These four places must be memorised precisely.

2. **Mahavira was the 24th Tirthankara**, not the founder of Jainism. Parshvanatha (23rd) preceded him. Rishabhadeva (Adinatha) was the 1st Tirthankara.

3. **Triratna of Buddhism**: Buddha, Dhamma, Sangha. **Triratna of Jainism**: Right Faith, Right Knowledge, Right Conduct. Two different religions, two different "Triratnas" — very commonly confused in SSC.

4. **The 3rd Buddhist Council** was held at Pataliputra under **Ashoka** (~250 BCE). It resulted in the compilation of the Abhidhamma Pitaka and the dispatch of missionaries including Mahendra (Ashoka's son) to Sri Lanka.

5. **The 4th Buddhist Council** was held in Kashmir under **Kanishka** (1st century CE). It led to the formal division of Buddhism into Mahayana (Greater Vehicle) and Hinayana (Lesser Vehicle/Theravada).

6. **Vajji (Vaishali)** was the world's earliest known republic — a gana-sangha (oligarchic republic). Both Buddha and Mahavira had connections to Vaishali. The Licchavis (Mahavira's mother's clan) were a powerful republican clan there.

7. **Magadha's four advantages** over other Mahajanapadas: iron ore (weapons), fertile Gangetic soil, strategic position, powerful rulers (Bimbisara, Ajatashatru). This set the stage for the Mauryan Empire.

8. **Hinayana** (Lesser Vehicle) = Theravada — the older, more orthodox school; spread to Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, Cambodia. **Mahayana** (Greater Vehicle) — more liberal; Bodhisattvas; spread to China, Japan, Korea, Tibet. Do not call Hinayana "inferior" — it's just a label applied by Mahayanists.

9. **Digambara** Jains = sky-clad (naked monks); based in Deccan/Karnataka. **Shvetambara** = white-robed; based in Gujarat/Rajasthan. The split occurred after Bhadrabahu led a group south during a famine.

10. **Buddhism rejected the authority of the Vedas** and the varna system; it accepted karma but rejected the Vedic concept of Atman (permanent soul). **Jainism also rejected Vedic authority** but had an extreme emphasis on ahimsa that Buddhism did not share.

11. **Anekantavada** (Jainism) = "many-sidedness of truth" — the philosophical doctrine that truth is complex and no single viewpoint is complete. This makes Jainism uniquely pluralistic among world religions.

12. **Ahimsa** is MORE central to Jainism than to Buddhism. Jain monks strain water, sweep paths, and wear mouth-covers to avoid killing even microscopic organisms. Buddhist monks could eat meat if the animal was not killed specifically for them (the "three pure meats").

13. **Pavapuri (Bihar)** is where Mahavira attained Nirvana/death — a major Jain pilgrimage site. Not to be confused with Bodh Gaya (Buddha's enlightenment) or Sarnath (Buddha's first sermon).

14. **Bimbisara** (Magadha; ~544–492 BCE) and his son **Ajatashatru** (492–460 BCE) were the kings who interacted with both Buddha and Mahavira. Bimbisara was a contemporary and supporter of Buddha; Ajatashatru (who killed his father) also became a devotee of Buddha and presided over the 1st Buddhist Council after Buddha's death.

15. **The 16 Mahajanapadas** come from two sources: **Anguttara Nikaya** (Buddhist Pali text) and **Bhagavati Sutra** (Jain text). Both list 16 kingdoms but with slight differences.

16. **Buddha's clan was Shakya** (not a Kshatriya claim by birth in the strictest Brahminical sense, but close — the Shakyas were a republican warrior clan). He was prince of **Kapilavastu** (now in Nepal near the Indian border). His father was **Shuddhodana**; mother **Maya** (died 7 days after birth); foster mother **Mahaprajapati Gautami**.
