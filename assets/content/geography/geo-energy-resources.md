# Energy Resources — Hydro, Thermal, Nuclear & Renewable

## Powering a Billion Lives

India runs on energy. Of its total electricity generation, roughly 70% comes from thermal plants burning coal, ~13% from hydro, ~3% from nuclear, and the growing remainder from renewables. The ambition is enormous: India has pledged 500 GW of renewable capacity by 2030 and net-zero emissions by 2070.

This lesson covers the full energy landscape — where India generates power, which dams hold back which rivers, which nuclear reactors were built first, and where the sun and wind do the most work.

---

## Thermal Power

Thermal power — generated by burning coal, gas, or oil to spin turbines — remains India's dominant electricity source. The biggest thermal plants are called **Super Thermal Power Stations (STPS)** when capacity exceeds 1,000 MW.

**NTPC (National Thermal Power Corporation Limited):**
- India's **largest power generation company** (across all fuels now)
- **Maharatna PSU**
- HQ: **New Delhi**
- Now also operates hydro and renewable plants (renamed "NTPC Limited")

**Major thermal power plants:**

| Plant | State | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Vindhyachal STPS** | Madhya Pradesh (Singrauli) | **India's largest thermal power plant** (~4,760 MW); NTPC |
| **Mundra Thermal Power** | Gujarat | India's **largest private** thermal power plant; Adani Power |
| **Korba STPS** | Chhattisgarh | NTPC; major coal belt |
| **Rihand STPS** | UP (Sonbhadra) | NTPC |
| **Ramagundam STPS** | Telangana | NTPC; **first super thermal power plant in India** |
| **Talcher STPS** | Odisha | NTPC; near Talcher coalfield |
| **Farakka STPS** | West Bengal | NTPC |
| **Kahalgaon STPS** | Bihar | NTPC |
| **Dabhol Power Plant** | Maharashtra (Ratnagiri) | Originally Enron (troubled project); now NTPC gas-based |

## Hydroelectric Power

Hydroelectric power uses falling water to spin turbines — clean, renewable, and reliable once built. India has the **5th largest hydroelectric potential** in the world.

**NHPC (National Hydroelectric Power Corporation):**
- India's largest hydroelectric power company
- **Navratna PSU**
- HQ: **Faridabad, Haryana**

### Dam Superlatives — the Most-Tested Facts

| Category | Dam | River | State |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Highest dam in India** | **Tehri Dam** (260.5 m) | **Bhagirathi** | **Uttarakhand** |
| **Longest dam in India** | **Hirakud Dam** (25.8 km) | **Mahanadi** | **Odisha** |
| **Highest gravity dam in Asia** | **Bhakra Dam** (226 m) | **Sutlej** | **Himachal Pradesh / Punjab** |
| **Largest arch dam in India** | **Idukki Dam** | **Periyar** | **Kerala** |

**Tehri Dam** (Uttarakhand) — a rockfill-cum-earth dam; at 260.5 m it is India's tallest dam; the Tehri reservoir (Swami Ramtirthagiri reservoir) submerged the old town of Tehri. Generates 2,400 MW.

**Bhakra Dam** (HP/Punjab) — part of the Bhakra-Nangal project; the Nangal Dam is downstream. Reservoir = **Govind Sagar Lake**. Jawaharlal Nehru called it "the new temple of resurgent India." Though not India's highest (that's Tehri), it is Asia's **highest straight gravity dam** — a key distinction SSC tests.

**Hirakud Dam** (Odisha) — on the Mahanadi River; at 25.8 km it is the world's longest earthen dam. Two observation towers: Gandhi Minar and Nehru Minar.

## Nuclear Power

India is one of a handful of countries with a fully indigenous nuclear fuel cycle — from uranium mining to reactor operation to waste management.

**India's operational nuclear capacity:** ~7,480 MW across 22 reactors at 7 plants (and growing).

### Key Nuclear Institutions

| Body | Full Name | HQ |
|---|---|---|
| **DAE** | Department of Atomic Energy | New Delhi (under PMO) |
| **NPCIL** | Nuclear Power Corporation of India Ltd | **Mumbai (Anushakti Nagar)** |
| **BARC** | Bhabha Atomic Research Centre | **Trombay, Mumbai** |
| **AERB** | Atomic Energy Regulatory Board | Mumbai |
| **IGCAR** | Indira Gandhi Centre for Atomic Research | Kalpakkam, Tamil Nadu |

**Homi J. Bhabha** = "**Father of India's Nuclear Programme**" — visionary physicist who set up DAE and BARC; died in a 1966 air crash. BARC is named after him.

### India's Three-Stage Nuclear Programme

Developed by Homi J. Bhabha to leverage India's scarce uranium but enormous thorium reserves:

| Stage | Reactor Type | Fuel In | Fuel Out | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Stage 1** | PHWR (Pressurised Heavy Water Reactor) | **Natural uranium** | Plutonium | Operational at most plants |
| **Stage 2** | FBR (Fast Breeder Reactor) | Plutonium | More fissile material (thorium → U-233) | PFBR Kalpakkam under commissioning |
| **Stage 3** | AHWR (Advanced Heavy Water Reactor) | **Thorium** | Energy | Future goal |

India has the **world's 2nd largest thorium reserves** (Kerala and Tamil Nadu coastal Monazite sand) — the entire Stage 3 strategy is built around this endowment.

### Nuclear Power Stations

| Station | State | First Reactor Year | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tarapur (TAPS)** | **Maharashtra** | **1969** | India's **first nuclear power plant**; uses BWR technology; US-supplied initially |
| **Rawatbhata (RAPS)** | **Rajasthan** (Kota) | 1973 | Rajasthan Atomic Power Station; PHWRs; oldest PHWR station |
| **Kalpakkam (MAPS)** | **Tamil Nadu** | 1984 | Madras Atomic Power Station; India's **first indigenously designed reactor**; also hosts PFBR (Fast Breeder Reactor) |
| **Narora (NAPS)** | **Uttar Pradesh** | 1991 | — |
| **Kakrapar (KAPS)** | **Gujarat** | 1993 | — |
| **Kaiga** | **Karnataka** | 2000 | — |
| **Kudankulam (KKNPP)** | **Tamil Nadu** | 2013 | **India's largest nuclear power plant** by capacity; Russian VVER-1000 technology; India-Russia joint project |

### Nuclear Tests

| Test | Year | Code Name | Leader | Location |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pokhran I** | **1974** | **"Smiling Buddha"** | **Indira Gandhi** | Pokhran, Rajasthan |
| **Pokhran II** | **May 1998** | **"Operation Shakti"** | **Atal Bihari Vajpayee** | Pokhran, Rajasthan |

After Pokhran II, India declared itself a nuclear weapons state.

### Nuclear Fuel

- **Uranium:** India's reserves are limited; Jaduguda (Jharkhand) = India's **only operational uranium mine**; also in Meghalaya (Domiasiat), Rajasthan, AP
- **Thorium:** India has world's 2nd largest reserves; Kerala and Tamil Nadu coastal sand (Monazite — mixed with rare earth elements)

## Renewable Energy

India's renewable energy story is one of the world's fastest-moving in scale and ambition.

### Solar Power

India is among the **world's top 5 solar power producers**.

**Solar irradiation map:** Rajasthan and Gujarat receive the most sunlight (~2,200+ kWh/m²/year) — hence the concentration of major solar parks there.

| Solar Park | State | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Bhadla Solar Park** | **Rajasthan** (Jodhpur) | **World's largest solar park** (~2,245 MW+); operational |
| **Pavagada Solar Park** | Karnataka (Tumkur) | One of world's largest (~2,050 MW) |
| **Rewa Ultra Mega Solar** | Madhya Pradesh | First solar project supplying power to Delhi Metro |
| **Charanka Solar Park** | Gujarat (Patan) | — |
| **Kamuthi Solar Plant** | Tamil Nadu | Previously world's largest single-location solar plant (648 MW) |

**Rajasthan = largest solar power-producing state in India**

**International Solar Alliance (ISA):**
- **Proposed by India (PM Modi) at COP21, Paris, November 2015**
- Focus: Mobilise solar energy deployment in 120+ "sunshine countries" between the Tropics of Cancer and Capricorn
- HQ: **Gurugram (Gurgaon), Haryana** (at National Institute of Solar Energy campus)

### Wind Power

India is among the **world's top 5 wind power producers**.

**Tamil Nadu = traditionally India's largest wind power-producing state** — though Rajasthan is growing fast.

| Wind Farm / Region | State | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Muppandal Wind Farm** | Tamil Nadu (Kanyakumari) | One of Asia's largest onshore wind farms |
| **Jaisalmer Wind Park** | Rajasthan | Large wind energy concentration |
| Satara, Sangli | Maharashtra | Major wind zones |
| AP, Karnataka | — | Significant wind energy states |

**Offshore wind:** India is developing offshore wind potential in Gujarat (Gulf of Khambhat) and Tamil Nadu coasts.

### Tidal Power

India has identified sites but no large commercial tidal plant yet.

- **Gulf of Khambhat (Cambay, Gujarat)** — **highest tidal energy potential in India** (tidal range of 8–11 m)
- **Gulf of Kachchh (Kutch, Gujarat)** — also significant tidal potential
- **Sundarbans (West Bengal)** — studied for tidal energy

### Geothermal Energy

India's geothermal potential is concentrated in the Himalayan arc and volcanic regions.

- **Puga Valley (Ladakh)** — **India's best geothermal prospect**; natural hot springs; near Leh; studied for development
- **Manikaran (Himachal Pradesh)** — famous hot springs; small geothermal initiatives
- **Chumathang (Ladakh)** — another geothermal zone

### Biogas / Biomass

- India has massive biomass potential from agricultural residues (paddy straw, sugarcane bagasse) and livestock dung
- **Gobar Gas (biogas from cattle dung)** — widely used in rural India
- **SATAT scheme** (Sustainable Alternative Towards Affordable Transportation) — converting agricultural waste to Compressed Biogas (CBG) for vehicles

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Tehri Dam = India's highest dam (260.5 m)** — Bhagirathi, Uttarakhand; it is a rockfill dam, NOT a gravity dam.
2. **Bhakra Dam = highest gravity dam in Asia (226 m)** — Sutlej, HP; NOT India's highest overall (Tehri is taller). SSC loves this distinction.
3. **Hirakud Dam = India's longest dam (25.8 km)** — Mahanadi, Odisha; also the world's longest earthen dam.
4. **Govind Sagar = reservoir created by Bhakra Dam** (NOT Tehri; Tehri's reservoir is called Tehri Reservoir / Swami Ramtirthagiri reservoir).
5. **Tarapur (Maharashtra) = India's FIRST nuclear power plant (1969)** — NOT Kalpakkam; Kalpakkam = first indigenously designed reactor.
6. **Kudankulam (Tamil Nadu) = India's LARGEST nuclear power plant** — Russian VVER-1000 technology; India-Russia civil nuclear project.
7. **Pokhran I = 1974, "Smiling Buddha," under Indira Gandhi; Pokhran II = 1998, "Operation Shakti," under Vajpayee** — the names and leaders are commonly swapped in tricky options.
8. **Homi J. Bhabha = Father of India's Nuclear Programme**; BARC (Trombay, Mumbai) is named after him.
9. **India's Three-Stage Nuclear Programme ends with thorium (Stage 3)** — India has world's 2nd largest thorium reserves in Kerala-TN Monazite sand.
10. **Jaduguda (Jharkhand) = India's only operational uranium mine**.
11. **Bhadla Solar Park (Rajasthan, Jodhpur) = world's largest solar park** — SSC may test this as current affairs or static GK.
12. **International Solar Alliance (ISA) proposed at COP21 Paris (2015); HQ Gurugram, Haryana**.
13. **Ramagundam (Telangana) = India's first super thermal power plant** (NTPC); Vindhyachal (MP) = India's largest thermal power plant.
14. **NHPC HQ = Faridabad, Haryana** (NOT New Delhi; NTPC is New Delhi — these two PSUs are confused regularly).
15. **Gulf of Khambhat (Cambay), Gujarat = India's highest tidal energy potential**.
16. **Puga Valley (Ladakh) = India's best geothermal energy prospect** — a lesser-known but repeated SSC static GK fact.
