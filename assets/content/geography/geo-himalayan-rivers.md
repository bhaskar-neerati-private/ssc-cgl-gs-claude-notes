# Himalayan Rivers — Indus, Ganga & Brahmaputra Systems

## Why Himalayan Rivers Are Special

The rivers born in the Himalayas share three defining characteristics that set them apart from peninsular rivers:

1. **Perennial** — they never run dry, even in summer, because they are fed by snowmelt and glaciers year-round
2. **Antecedent** — they are older than the Himalayas themselves; as the mountains slowly rose, these rivers cut downward through the rising rock, carving spectacular gorges (the Indus, Ganga tributaries, and Brahmaputra all predate the Himalayas)
3. **Long and large** — they collect water from enormous basins, flow across the plains, and build the world's largest delta system

Three master river systems drain the Himalayan region into India: Indus, Ganga, and Brahmaputra.

## 1. The Indus System

**Origin:** The Singi Khamban ("Lion's Mouth") glacier in western Tibet, near **Lake Mansarovar** but slightly to the north. The Indus flows **westward** through Tibet, enters India through Ladakh near Demchok, passes through the Ladakh plateau, cuts through the Himalayas via the Indus Gorge, then flows northwest into Pakistan.

**Total length:** ~3,180 km; only about **709 km** flows within India (mostly in Ladakh/J&K).

### The Five Rivers of Punjab (Panj-Ab = Five Waters)

The word **Punjab** literally means "Land of Five Rivers" (Panj = five, Ab = water in Persian). All five rivers ultimately join the Indus in Pakistan.

| River | Ancient Name | Origin | Key Facts |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Jhelum** | Vitasta | Verinag Spring, Kashmir | Flows through Kashmir valley; **Wular Lake** (India's largest natural freshwater lake) is on the Jhelum |
| **Chenab** | Asikni / Chandrabhaga | Bara Lacha La area, Lahaul-Spiti | Largest of the five Punjab rivers by volume; merges with Jhelum in Pakistan |
| **Ravi** | Parushni / Iravati | Near Rohtang Pass, Himachal Pradesh | Flows through Chamba valley; historically the "river of Lahore" |
| **Beas** | Vipasa / Arjikiya | Beas Kund, near Rohtang Pass, HP | Joins the Sutlej in Punjab (India); Alexander the Great's army refused to cross the Beas — it was his easternmost point of march |
| **Sutlej** | Satadru | Rakas Lake (near Mansarovar, Tibet) | Enters India via **Shipki La** pass (HP); longest of the five Punjab rivers; Bhakra-Nangal Dam is on it |

**Indus Water Treaty (1960):** Brokered by the World Bank between India and Pakistan. Division:
- **India gets:** Eastern rivers — Ravi, Beas, Sutlej
- **Pakistan gets:** Western rivers — Indus, Jhelum, Chenab

**Bhakra-Nangal Dam:** On the Sutlej River in Himachal Pradesh — India's largest multi-purpose river valley project (not the first — DVC on Damodar was first; Bhakra-Nangal is the largest by dam height and reservoir). Creates **Gobind Sagar Reservoir** (largest artificial lake in India).

## 2. The Ganga System

**India's national river.** The Ganga basin is the largest river basin in India (~8.6 lakh sq km) and supports about 40% of India's population.

### Origin — The Prayags of the Upper Ganga

The Ganga does not spring from a single source. Two rivers meet to form it — **Bhagirathi** (traditional/sacred source) and **Alaknanda** (larger tributary).

**Bhagirathi** emerges from **Gaumukh** (Cow's Mouth) — the snout of the **Gangotri Glacier** (Uttarakhand). This is the actual, physical, measurable source.

**Alaknanda** collects several tributaries through a series of holy confluences called **Prayags** (meeting places):

| Prayag | Where | Rivers joining |
|---|---|---|
| Vishnuprayag | Uttarakhand | Alaknanda + Dhauliganga |
| Nandaprayag | Uttarakhand | Alaknanda + Nandakini |
| Karnaprayag | Uttarakhand | Alaknanda + Pindar |
| Rudraprayag | Uttarakhand | Alaknanda + Mandakini |
| **Devprayag** | Uttarakhand | **Alaknanda + Bhagirathi = GANGA begins** |

From Devprayag, the Ganga flows to Rishikesh → **Haridwar** (where it enters the plains) → east across UP → Bihar → West Bengal → Bay of Bengal.

**Triveni Sangam at Prayagraj (Allahabad):** The confluence of Ganga + Yamuna + the mythical underground Saraswati. Site of the Kumbh Mela.

### Major Tributaries of the Ganga

**Left bank (joining from the north — rivers from Nepal/Terai):**

| Tributary | Key Facts |
|---|---|
| **Ramganga** | Uttarakhand → UP; joins near Kannauj |
| **Gomti** | Entirely in UP; source in Gomat Taal (UP); flows through Lucknow |
| **Ghaghara (Ghaghra)** | Also called Karnali in Nepal; largest left bank tributary of Ganga by volume; joins at Chhapra (Bihar) |
| **Gandak** | Originates in Nepal (Narayani in Nepal); joins Ganga at Hajipur/Sonpur (Bihar) |
| **Kosi** | "**Sorrow of Bihar**" — highly flood-prone; shifts course regularly; originates in Nepal; joins near Kursela (Bihar); carries enormous sediment |
| **Mahananda** | West Bengal; joins before Ganga enters Bangladesh |

**Right bank (joining from the south — rivers from peninsular India):**

| Tributary | Key Facts |
|---|---|
| **Yamuna** | Most important right bank tributary; origin: Yamunotri Glacier (Uttarakhand); length 1,376 km — longest tributary of Ganga; joins at Prayagraj |
| **Son** | Originates at Amarkantak (MP) — same point as Narmada but flows northeast; joins Ganga near Patna |
| **Damodar** | Flows through Chota Nagpur; joins Hooghly (Ganga distributary) in West Bengal; "Sorrow of Bengal" (flood-prone before DVC dams) |

### The Yamuna and Its Tributaries

The Yamuna is essentially the Ganga's twin — it originates close by in the same Uttarakhand Himalayan system.

- **Origin:** Yamunotri Glacier, Uttarakhand (Bandarpunch massif)
- **Length:** 1,376 km — longest tributary of the Ganga
- **Major cities on Yamuna:** Delhi, Mathura, Agra, Etawah, Allahabad (Prayagraj)

**Tributaries of Yamuna (from south/peninsular side):**
- **Chambal** — originates from Janapav Hill, near Mhow (MP); forms Rajasthan-MP boundary; famous for Chambal ravines (badland topography); Chambal Sanctuary (gharial, dolphins); eventually joins Yamuna near Etawah (UP)
- **Betwa** — MP; joins Yamuna in UP (Hamirpur); Ken-Betwa river linking project is India's first inter-linking project
- **Ken** — MP; joins Yamuna in UP; Ken-Betwa link connects Ken to Betwa
- **Tons** — Longest tributary of Yamuna; joins Yamuna at Prayagraj

**Tributaries of Yamuna (from north/Himalayan side):**
- **Tons** (yes, same name — but the major one is from the Uttarakhand Himalayas, joining at Prayagraj via the Himalayan path)
- **Hindon** — flows through western UP; joins near Delhi

### The Ganga Delta — World's Largest

As the Ganga approaches the sea (in West Bengal and Bangladesh), it splits into distributaries:
- **Hooghly** — the western distributary; on its banks stands **Kolkata**; the major navigable channel in India
- **Padma** — the main channel into Bangladesh; joins the Brahmaputra (Jamuna) and Meghna before reaching the Bay of Bengal

The combined **Ganga-Brahmaputra-Meghna delta** is the **world's largest delta** — the **Sundarbans** (UNESCO World Heritage Site; largest mangrove forest in the world; Bengal tiger; Irrawaddy dolphin).

## 3. The Brahmaputra System

Of all Indian rivers, the Brahmaputra has perhaps the most dramatic biography.

**Origin:** **Angsi Glacier** (also called Chemayungdung glacier) in southwestern Tibet — very close to Mansarovar, at approximately 5,150 m altitude.

**The river has different names in different countries:**

| Stretch | Name | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Tibet | **Tsangpo** (Yarlung Tsangpo) | Flows eastward across Tibet |
| Arunachal Pradesh | **Dihang** | After the U-turn; cuts through gorge |
| Assam | **Brahmaputra** | Flows westward across Assam |
| Bangladesh | **Jamuna** | After entering Bangladesh |

### The Great U-Turn Around Namcha Barwa

After flowing ~1,700 km eastward across Tibet as the Tsangpo, the river makes a spectacular **180° horseshoe bend** around **Namcha Barwa (7,782 m)** — the easternmost peak of the Himalayas, in Arunachal Pradesh. This bend creates the **Dihang Gorge** — one of the world's deepest gorges (~5,000 m deep). The river then turns south, cuts through the mountains, and emerges into Assam as the Brahmaputra.

### The Brahmaputra in Assam

In Assam, the Brahmaputra is:
- Up to **18 km wide** during monsoon floods — the widest river in India during floods
- A **braided river** (splits into multiple channels separated by sand bars and islands)
- Home to **Majuli Island** — the world's largest inhabited river island (~880 sq km), near Jorhat; a centre of Vaishnavite culture (sattra monasteries); on UNESCO's tentative World Heritage list

### Major Tributaries of Brahmaputra (in Assam)

**Right bank (from south — Meghalaya, Nagaland hills):**
- **Subansiri** — "Golden River" (Arunachal Pradesh)
- **Dibang** — from Arunachal Pradesh
- **Lohit** — from Tibet through Arunachal; joins to form the "Brahmaputra" at the Lohit-Brahmaputra confluence; the state of Arunachal Pradesh is sometimes said to begin where the Dihang, Dibang, and Lohit join the Tsangpo system
- **Barak** — Manipur; flows west, eventually becomes Surma-Meghna in Bangladesh

**Left bank (from north — Bhutan and Arunachal Pradesh hills):**
- **Kameng (Jia Bhoreli)** — from Arunachal; Nameri National Park
- **Manas** — flows through Bhutan and Assam; Manas National Park and Tiger Reserve (UNESCO World Heritage, Project Tiger)
- **Sankosh** — Bhutan border area
- **Teesta (Tista)** — originates from Cho Lhamu Lake (Sikkim); flows through Sikkim and West Bengal; joins Brahmaputra near Fulchhari (Bangladesh); Teesta water-sharing is a longstanding India-Bangladesh diplomatic issue

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Gaumukh** = actual snout of Gangotri Glacier; where Bhagirathi (and hence Ganga) physically emerges.
2. **Devprayag** = where Ganga is formed (Bhagirathi + Alaknanda). NOT Haridwar (Haridwar = where Ganga enters the plains).
3. **Kosi = "Sorrow of Bihar"** — not "Sorrow of Bengal" (Damodar was called Sorrow of Bengal before DVC dams controlled it).
4. **Wular Lake (J&K)** = India's largest natural freshwater lake; formed on the **Jhelum River**. Do NOT confuse with Chilika (largest lagoon) or Vembanad (longest lake).
5. **Indus Water Treaty (1960):** India gets Ravi + Beas + Sutlej (eastern); Pakistan gets Indus + Jhelum + Chenab (western).
6. **Bhakra-Nangal Dam** = India's **largest** gravity dam; on the Sutlej; creates Gobind Sagar reservoir. NOT to be confused with Tehri Dam (highest dam in India, on Bhagirathi).
7. **Son River and Narmada** BOTH originate from **Amarkantak** — but Son flows northeast (into Ganga) and Narmada flows west (into Arabian Sea). Classic trick question.
8. **Yamuna** = longest tributary of the Ganga (1,376 km). NOT Chambal (which is a tributary of Yamuna).
9. **Chambal** = tributary of Yamuna (which is tributary of Ganga). Chambal → Yamuna → Ganga (two steps removed from Ganga).
10. **Tsangpo = Brahmaputra** in Tibet; **Dihang** in Arunachal Pradesh; **Jamuna** in Bangladesh.
11. **Namcha Barwa (7,782 m)** = easternmost Himalayan peak; around which Brahmaputra makes its U-turn (Dihang Gorge).
12. **Majuli** = world's largest river island; in **Brahmaputra** (Assam); NOT Ganga.
13. **Teesta (Tista)** = tributary of **Brahmaputra** (NOT Ganga). Flows through Sikkim and West Bengal before joining Brahmaputra in Bangladesh.
14. **Brahmaputra** is the widest river in India (during floods, up to 18 km); NOT the longest entirely within India (that's the Ganga at ~2,525 km).
15. **Sutlej** enters India via **Shipki La** pass (HP) — from Tibet/Rakas Lake. Useful because it links the river origin with the pass from the Himalayas lesson.
16. **Ken-Betwa Link** = India's first inter-river linking project; links Ken River to Betwa River — both tributaries of Yamuna, in MP/UP.
