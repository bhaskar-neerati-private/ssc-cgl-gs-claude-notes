# Minerals of India — Ferrous, Non-Ferrous & Coal

## The Hidden Treasure Under India's Soil

Every time a train runs, a steel bridge stands, or a power plant lights a city — minerals are at work. India sits atop one of the richest mineral endowments in the world, concentrated in the ancient hard rocks of the Peninsular Plateau (the Deccan Plateau and its northeastern extensions). Understanding where each mineral comes from — and who mines it — is exactly what SSC tests.

**Classification of minerals:**
- **Metallic:** Ferrous (iron-bearing: iron ore, manganese, chromite) + Non-Ferrous (copper, bauxite/aluminium, lead-zinc, gold)
- **Non-Metallic:** Mica, limestone, gypsum, rock salt
- **Energy/Fuel Minerals:** Coal, lignite (covered in this lesson); petroleum and gas in the Energy lesson

## Ferrous Minerals

### Iron Ore

India is among the **world's 4th–5th largest iron ore producers**, with vast reserves in the Peninsular plateau.

**Types of iron ore (highest to lowest quality):**

| Type | Iron Content | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Magnetite** | **~72%** | Highest iron; Fe₃O₄; magnetic; less abundant in India |
| **Hematite** | **60–70%** | **Most common in India**; Fe₂O₃; called "Red ore" |
| Limonite | 40–60% | Yellow-brown; lower grade |
| Siderite | ~48% | Least pure; FeCO₃ |

India is dominated by **Hematite** iron ore — found in Precambrian metamorphic and sedimentary rocks.

**Major iron ore belts:**

| Belt | States | Key Deposits |
|---|---|---|
| **Odisha–Jharkhand Belt** | Odisha + Jharkhand | Keonjhar, Sundargarh, Mayurbhanj (Odisha); Noamundi, Gua (Jharkhand, Singhbhum) |
| **Durg–Bastar–Chandrapur Belt** | Chhattisgarh + Maharashtra | **Bailadila (Bastar, CG)** — world's finest hematite; exported to Japan; NMDC |
| **Bellary–Hospet Belt** | Karnataka | Bellary (Ballari), Hospet, Kudremukh; large deposits, controversial mining history |
| **Maharashtra–Goa Belt** | Goa + Maharashtra | Goa was a major exporter (to Japan/Korea); Supreme Court ban 2012; Ratnagiri (MH) |

**State-wise ranking:**

| Rank | State |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Odisha** — largest iron ore producer |
| 2 | **Chhattisgarh** |
| 3 | **Jharkhand** |
| 4 | Karnataka |
| 5 | Goa (subject to court orders) |

**NMDC (National Mineral Development Corporation):**
- India's **largest iron ore producer** (public sector)
- HQ: **Hyderabad, Telangana**
- Operates: Bailadila mines (Chhattisgarh), Donimalai mine (Karnataka), Panna diamond mine (MP)

## Non-Ferrous Minerals

India is **deficient in copper** and imports a significant portion of its needs.

**Major deposits:**

| Region | Location | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Singhbhum** | Jharkhand (Mosabani, Rakha) | Major copper mining region |
| **Khetri** | Rajasthan (Jhunjhunu) | "**Copper City of India**" |
| **Malanjkhand** | Balaghat, Madhya Pradesh | **Largest copper deposit** in India |
| Agnigundala | Andhra Pradesh | — |

**Hindustan Copper Limited (HCL):**
- Only PSU engaged in copper mining in India
- HQ: **Kolkata, West Bengal**
- Operates at Khetri (Rajasthan), Malanjkhand (MP), Singhbhum (Jharkhand)

### Lead and Zinc

**India's status:** India has significant lead-zinc deposits; largely in Rajasthan.

- **Rajasthan = India's largest lead-zinc producer**
  - **Zawar mines** (near Udaipur) — one of the world's oldest continuously operated zinc mines
  - **Rampura Agucha** (Bhilwara district) — one of the world's largest zinc mines

**Hindustan Zinc Limited (HZL):**
- Largest zinc producer in India; now majority-owned by **Vedanta** (Anil Agarwal)
- HQ: **Udaipur, Rajasthan**

### Gold

India has limited domestic gold deposits; imports most of its gold.

| Region | Location | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Kolar Gold Fields (KGF)** | Kolar district, **Karnataka** | **Closed in 2001** (unviable depth; 3+ km deep); one of the world's deepest gold mines; historically richest in India |
| **Hutti Gold Mines** | Raichur, Karnataka | **Currently operating**; Karnataka government PSU |
| Ramallakota | Andhra Pradesh | Active; small |

- **Karnataka = largest gold-producing state** in India
- **KGF (Kolar Gold Fields)** — the inspiration for the movie franchise — operated for ~150 years before being shut; made Kolar famous

**Uses:** Electrical insulation (capacitors, transformers), cosmetics (eyeshadow, highlighters), paints, fireproofing

| Rank | State |

### Diamond

- **Panna (Madhya Pradesh)** = India's **only currently operating diamond mine**; operated by NMDC
- **Golconda (Andhra Pradesh / Telangana)** = India's historic diamond source — produced the **Koh-i-Noor**, **Hope Diamond**, **Regent Diamond**; mines mostly exhausted by the 18th century

## Coal — The Black Engine of Industrial India

Coal is India's most critical energy mineral and the primary fuel for thermal power plants (which generate ~70% of India's electricity).

**India's global position:**
- **4th largest coal reserves** in the world (USA → Russia → Australia → India)
- **2nd largest coal producer** in the world (China is 1st)
- **2nd largest coal consumer** (China is 1st)

Despite enormous reserves, **India still imports coal** because:
1. Domestic coal has **high ash content** (up to 40–45%) — not ideal for all uses
2. **Coking coal** (for steel blast furnaces) is scarce domestically — imports from Australia, USA
3. Coastal power plants find sea-borne coal economically competitive

### Types of Coal

| Type | Carbon % | Quality | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Anthracite** | **>80%** | Best | Hardest, most heat; very rare in India (only in J&K — Riasi) |
| **Bituminous** | **45–86%** | High | Most mined in India; includes coking coal (steel) + steam coal (power) |
| **Lignite** | **25–45%** | Low | "Brown coal"; soft; Neyveli (Tamil Nadu) — major deposit |
| **Peat** | **<25%** | Lowest | Partly decomposed plant matter; not commercially mined |

### Gondwana Coalfields (~98% of India's coal)

Gondwana coal is **200–300 million years old** — the best quality coal India has. Found in ancient river valley sedimentary basins.

| Valley Belt | States | Key Coalfields |
|---|---|---|
| **Damodar Valley Belt** | Jharkhand + West Bengal | **Jharia** (largest + best coking coal), **Raniganj** (oldest), Bokaro, Giridih, East Bokaro, Ramgarh |
| **Son Valley Belt** | MP + UP | **Singrauli** (largest in MP; "Energy Capital of India"), Sohagpur, Johilla |
| **Mahanadi Valley Belt** | Odisha + Chhattisgarh | **Talcher** (Odisha — largest in Odisha), IB River fields, **Korba** (Chhattisgarh — largest in CG) |
| **Wardha–Godavari Belt** | Maharashtra + AP/TS | Chandrapur, Yeotmal (MH); **Singareni** (Telangana — only major Gondwana coalfield in peninsular south) |

**Critical name facts:**
- **Jharia (Dhanbad, Jharkhand)** = **India's largest coalfield** AND best coking coal; notorious for underground coal fires burning for decades; "**Coal Capital of India**" = Dhanbad
- **Raniganj (Bardhhaman, West Bengal)** = **India's oldest coalfield** (discovered 1774 by East India Company); not the largest — often confused
- **Singareni (Telangana/AP)** = only major Gondwana coalfield in the Deccan/peninsular south; operated by SCCL (Singareni Collieries Company Limited, a Telangana state PSU)
- **Singrauli (MP/UP border)** = "**Energy Capital of India**" — massive coal + thermal power complex

### Tertiary Coalfields (~2% of reserves)

Younger (15–60 million years), lower quality, in Northeast India:
- **Meghalaya** (Garo, Khasi, Jaintia hills — "rat-hole mining" controversy), **Assam** (Makum, Ledo), **Arunachal Pradesh**, **Nagaland**, **Jammu (Riasi)** — **Anthracite** found here

### Coal India Limited (CIL)

- **World's largest coal mining company** by volume
- **Navratna PSU**
- HQ: **Kolkata, West Bengal**
- Produces ~80% of India's total coal output
- Subsidiaries: ECL (Raniganj), BCCL (Jharia), CCL (Jharkhand), NCL (Singrauli), WCL (Wardha), SECL (Chhattisgarh/MP), MCL (Odisha), NEC (northeast)

### Lignite — Brown Coal

- **Neyveli (Tamil Nadu)** = India's largest lignite deposit
- **NLC India Ltd (Neyveli Lignite Corporation)** — PSU; HQ **Neyveli, Tamil Nadu**; feeds the Neyveli Thermal Power Station
- Rajasthan (Barmer district — Kapurdi, Jalipa) — emerging lignite field; used for power in Rajasthan

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Hematite = most common iron ore in India** (Magnetite has higher iron content at 72%, but Hematite is what India mostly has — don't mix up quality and abundance).
2. **Odisha = India's largest iron ore producer** — NOT Jharkhand (both are in same belt; Odisha leads).
3. **Bailadila (Chhattisgarh) = world's finest hematite deposits** — operated by NMDC; exports to Japan.
4. **Jharia (Jharkhand) = India's largest coalfield + best coking coal**; Dhanbad district = "Coal Capital."
5. **Raniganj (West Bengal) = India's oldest coalfield (1774)** — NOT the largest; Jharia is largest.
6. **Coal India Limited = world's largest coal mining company**; HQ Kolkata.
7. **India's coal reserves = 4th largest globally** (USA → Russia → Australia → India).
8. **Khetri (Rajasthan) = "Copper City of India"**; Malanjkhand (MP, Balaghat) = largest copper deposit.
9. **Kolar Gold Fields (KGF, Karnataka) = historically richest gold mine, now CLOSED (2001)**; Hutti = currently operating gold mine in India.
10. **Koderma (Jharkhand) = "Mica Capital of India"**; Jharkhand = largest mica producer.
11. **Sukinda Valley (Odisha) = massive chromite deposits**; Odisha produces ~98% of India's chromite.
12. **Neyveli (Tamil Nadu) = India's main lignite deposit**; NLC India is the operating PSU.
13. **Singareni (Telangana) = only major Gondwana coalfield in peninsular south India**.
14. **Panna (MP) = India's only operating diamond mine** (operated by NMDC); Golconda = historical source of Koh-i-Noor (now exhausted).
15. **Rampura Agucha (Rajasthan) = one of the world's largest zinc mines**; HZL (Hindustan Zinc, Udaipur) operates it.
16. **NALCO HQ = Bhubaneswar; NMDC HQ = Hyderabad; HCL HQ = Kolkata; CIL HQ = Kolkata; MOIL HQ = Nagpur** — SSC loves testing PSU headquarters.
