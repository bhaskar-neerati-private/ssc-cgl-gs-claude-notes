# Major Industries of India

## The Workshop of Modern India

India's industrial map is not random — steel plants cluster near coal and iron ore, sugar mills grow within 50 km of sugarcane fields, and IT companies fill cities with reliable power and airports. Understanding why industries locate where they do is as important as memorising which city makes what.

---

## Iron and Steel

India is the **world's 2nd largest steel producer** (after China). Steel is the foundation of every other industry — railways, construction, automobiles, defence.

**SAIL (Steel Authority of India Limited):**
- India's largest **public sector** steel producer
- **Maharatna PSU**
- HQ: **New Delhi**

### The "Foreign Help" Story of SAIL's Integrated Plants

Each of India's post-independence public sector steel plants was built with foreign technical and financial help — and SSC tests exactly which country helped which plant:

| Plant | Location | State | Foreign Help | Year |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Rourkela Steel Plant** | Rourkela | **Odisha** | **West Germany** | 1959 — **first integrated steel plant of independent India** |
| **Bhilai Steel Plant** | Bhilai | **Chhattisgarh** | **Soviet Union (USSR)** | 1959 — uses Bailadila iron ore + Korba coal |
| **Durgapur Steel Plant** | Durgapur | **West Bengal** | **United Kingdom (British)** | 1956 |
| **Bokaro Steel Plant** | Bokaro | **Jharkhand** | **Soviet Union (USSR)** | 1964 |
| **IISCO (Indian Iron & Steel Co.)** | Burnpur | **West Bengal** | — | Oldest; later merged into SAIL |

**Memory trick:** B**o**karo + B**h**ilai = Both Soviet. **D**urgapur = **D**ifferent (British). **R**ourkela = **R**ight (German).

### Private Sector Steel

| Company | Plant Location | State | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tata Steel** | **Jamshedpur** | Jharkhand | **Oldest integrated steel plant in India (1907)**; founded by **Jamshedji Tata**; private sector |
| **RINL (Vizag Steel)** | **Visakhapatnam** | AP | PSU; **India's only coastal integrated steel plant** |
| **JSW Steel** | Toranagallu (Bellary) | Karnataka | One of India's largest private steel companies |
| Arcelor Mittal Nippon | Hazira | Gujarat | Global major |

**Jamshedpur (Jharkhand):** "**Steel City of India**"
- Tata Steel started here in 1907 — India's first integrated steel plant, privately built
- Confluence of rivers Subarnarekha and Kharkai
- A model company town: Jamshedji Tata built schools, hospitals, and recreational facilities for workers

**Vizag Steel / RINL:**
- **India's only coastal integrated steel plant** — a major locational advantage; imports coking coal from Australia directly by sea; exports steel easily
- Rashtriya Ispat Nigam Limited (RINL); Visakhapatnam, AP

## Cotton Textile Industry

India's **oldest and largest organised industry by employment** — predates the industrial revolution in the modern sector.

**Timeline:**
- 1818: First cotton textile mill near Kolkata (Fort Gloster)
- 1854: First successful cotton mill in **Mumbai** (Bombay Spinning & Weaving Company)
- 1861: American Civil War cut off US cotton → British demand drove Indian cotton boom

**Mumbai** = **"Manchester of India"** — historically India's cotton textile capital; mills lined the central business area (now redeveloped)

**Ahmedabad** also claims "Manchester of India" — Gujarat's textile powerhouse; **Calico Textile Museum** in Ahmedabad (world's finest collection of Indian textiles)

**Key centres:**

| City | State | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Ahmedabad | Gujarat | Cotton textiles; also synthetic; Mill district |
| Mumbai | Maharashtra | Historical mills; now finance & synthetic |
| **Coimbatore** | Tamil Nadu | **"Manchester of South India"** — cotton spinning |
| **Tirupur** | Tamil Nadu | **"T-Shirt City / Knitwear Capital"** — global knitwear export hub |
| Surat | Gujarat | **Synthetic textiles; man-made fibres**; now India's largest synthetic fabric centre |
| Kanpur, Agra | UP | Cotton + leather |
| Solapur | Maharashtra | Towels and bedsheets |
| Ludhiana | Punjab | Woollen hosiery, knitwear |

**Handloom clusters — famous fabrics:**
- **Varanasi (UP)** — Banarasi silk (GI tagged)
- **Kanchipuram (TN)** — Kanchipuram/Kanjivaram silk (GI tagged)
- **Pochampally (Telangana)** — Ikat weave
- **Sambalpuri (Odisha)** — Sambalpuri saree
- **Assam** — Muga silk (golden; GI tagged), Eri silk; Mekhela Chador
- **Pashmina (J&K/Ladakh)** — from Changthangi goat underfur; world's finest wool

## Sugar Industry

India = **world's largest or 2nd largest sugar producer** (trading with Brazil year to year).

**Critical location rule:** Sugar mills must be within **50 km of sugarcane fields** — sucrose content drops rapidly after harvest. This makes sugar a **resource-oriented industry**.

**Major sugar states:**

| Rank | State | Note |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **UP** | Largest sugarcane + sugar producer; Meerut-Muzaffarnagar-Gorakhpur belt |
| 2 | **Maharashtra** | Famous for **sugar cooperatives** (Kolhapur, Satara, Pune-Solapur belt); politically influential |
| 3 | Karnataka, TN | TN = highest sucrose yield per ha |

**Maharashtra Sugar Cooperatives** — a landmark model: farmer-owned sugar factories where growers share profits. Leaders like Sharad Pawar built their careers through these cooperatives.

## Cement Industry

India = **world's 2nd largest cement producer** (China is 1st).

**Raw material:** Limestone (essential) + Gypsum + Coal

**Rajasthan = India's largest cement-producing state** (most abundant limestone)

Other major states: Andhra Pradesh (Guntur), Karnataka, MP, Chhattisgarh

**Private leaders:**
- **UltraTech Cement** (Aditya Birla Group) — India's largest cement company
- Ambuja, ACC, Shree Cement

## Chemical and Petroleum Industry

**Jamnagar (Gujarat):** Site of **Reliance Industries' refinery complex** — the **world's largest single-location oil refinery** cluster

**Major oil refineries (PSU):**
- **IOCL (Indian Oil):** Panipat, Mathura, Barauni, Haldia, Paradip
- **BPCL:** Mumbai (Mahul)
- **HPCL:** Mumbai, Visakhapatnam
- **CPCL (Chennai Petroleum):** Manali, Chennai

**Fertiliser Plants:**
- Trombay (Mumbai) — **RCF (Rashtriya Chemicals & Fertilizers)**
- Sindri (Jharkhand) — once India's largest; coal-based; now closed
- Namrup (Assam) — Brahmaputra Valley Fertilizer Corporation
- Panipat (Haryana) — NFL (National Fertilisers Ltd)

**IFFCO (Indian Farmers Fertiliser Cooperative):**
- **World's largest fertiliser cooperative**
- HQ: **New Delhi**
- Plants at Phulpur (UP), Kandla (Gujarat), Paradeep (Odisha)

## Pharmaceutical Industry

India = **"Pharmacy of the World"** — world's **largest producer of generic medicines** by volume; supplies ~60% of global vaccine demand.

**Major pharmaceutical hubs:**
- **Hyderabad ("Pharma City of India")** — bulk drug manufacturing; largest pharma concentration in India
- **Ahmedabad** — Torrent, Zydus (Cadila), Sun Pharma's roots
- **Mumbai** — corporate HQ of most pharma companies
- **Baddi (Himachal Pradesh)** — tax-incentive hub; many manufacturing plants relocated here

**Major companies:** Sun Pharma, Dr. Reddy's, Cipla, Lupin, Aurobindo (Hyderabad), Wockhardt, Biocon (Bengaluru — biotech)

## Information Technology Industry

India = **world's 2nd largest IT exporter** (after the USA); IT-BPM sector is India's largest services export earner.

**The IT city nicknames:**
- **Bengaluru = "Silicon Valley of India"** — Infosys, Wipro, TCS campus, HCL Tech; India's largest IT hub
- **Hyderabad = "Cyberabad"** — HITEC City; Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Oracle, Infosys India hubs
- **Chennai** — Cognizant, Ford Tech, Zoho HQ, major IT hub
- **Pune** — Infosys, Wipro, Persistent, ThoughtWorks
- **Noida / Gurugram** — NCR; HCL Tech, TCS, Genpact, BPO hub

**STPI (Software Technology Parks of India):**
- Set up 1991 under the IT-enabling policy post-liberalisation
- Provided tax incentives, satellite connectivity, and bonded zones for software export
- Enabled the 1990s–2000s Indian IT boom (Infosys, Wipro, TCS all benefited)

## Defence Industries

**HAL (Hindustan Aeronautics Limited):**
- Maharatna PSU
- HQ: **Bengaluru**
- Manufactures: **Tejas** (India's indigenous Light Combat Aircraft / LCA), **Dhruv** (Advanced Light Helicopter / ALH), aircraft engines, Jaguar and Su-30 MKI maintenance

**BEL (Bharat Electronics Limited):**
- Navratna PSU
- HQ: **Bengaluru**
- Defence electronics: radar, sonar, electronic warfare, satellite communication

**BDL (Bharat Dynamics Limited):**
- Navratna PSU
- HQ: **Hyderabad**
- Missiles: anti-tank (Milan, Konkurs), surface-to-air (Akash supply)

**DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation):**
- HQ: **New Delhi**
- Develops missiles (Agni, Prithvi, BrahMos with Russia), drones, electronic systems

**Ordnance Factory Board → 7 Defence PSUs (2021):**
Major factories: Kanpur (small arms), Ambarnath/MH (ammunition), Jabalpur (gun carriages), Pune (armoured vehicles)

## Space Industry — ISRO

**ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation):**
- HQ: **Bengaluru** (Antariksh Bhavan)
- Founded: **1969** (replaced INCOSPAR)
- **Vikram Sarabhai** = **"Father of Indian Space Programme"**; founded INCOSPAR; ISRO was his vision; died 1971

**Key ISRO Centres:**

| Centre | Location | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **VSSC (Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre)** | **Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala** | Develops launch vehicles (PSLV, GSLV, LVM3) |
| **SHAR (Satish Dhawan Space Centre)** | **Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh** | India's **only operational spaceport / launch pad** |
| **SAC (Space Applications Centre)** | **Ahmedabad, Gujarat** | Communication + remote sensing satellites |
| **URSC / ISAC** | **Bengaluru, Karnataka** | Spacecraft systems and integration |
| **NRSC** | **Hyderabad, Telangana** | National Remote Sensing Centre; satellite data |
| **LPSC** | Thiruvananthapuram + Bengaluru | Liquid propulsion systems |

**Sriharikota (Andhra Pradesh):** A barrier island (between the Bay of Bengal and Pulicat Lake — India's 2nd largest lagoon) that houses the Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC-SHAR) — India's only rocket launch facility.

**Major ISRO milestones:**

| Mission | Year | Achievement |
|---|---|---|
| **Mangalyaan (MOM)** | 2014 | India's first Mars mission; **succeeded on first attempt** — the only country to do so |
| **Chandrayaan-1** | 2008 | Discovered water molecules on the Moon |
| **Chandrayaan-2** | 2019 | Orbiter succeeded; lander Vikram crash-landed |
| **Chandrayaan-3** | **2023** | **Successful soft landing at Moon's South Pole** — India became the **4th country to land on Moon** and **first to land at south pole** |
| **Aditya-L1** | 2023 | India's first solar observatory; placed at L1 Lagrange point |

## Industrial Policy Landmarks

- **1991 New Industrial Policy:** Liberalisation — licence raj dismantled; foreign investment opened; MNCs invited
- **Make in India (2014):** Promote domestic manufacturing; 25 focus sectors
- **PLI (Production Linked Incentive) Scheme (2020+):** Incentives to manufacture in India across 14 sectors (mobiles, pharma, textiles, food, EVs, etc.)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Bhilai and Bokaro = Soviet (USSR) help; Durgapur = British help; Rourkela = West German help** — one of SSC's favourite fill-in-the-blank setups.
2. **Rourkela = first integrated steel plant of independent India (1959, Odisha)** — NOT Bhilai or Jamshedpur.
3. **Tata Steel (Jamshedpur) = oldest integrated steel plant in India (1907)** — private sector; built before independence.
4. **Vizag Steel / RINL = India's only coastal integrated steel plant** — Visakhapatnam, AP.
5. **India = world's 2nd largest steel producer** (China is 1st); **India = world's 2nd largest cement producer** (China is 1st) — same ranking, different industry.
6. **Rajasthan = largest cement-producing state** (most limestone).
7. **"Manchester of India" = Ahmedabad or Mumbai (cotton textiles); "Manchester of South India" = Coimbatore; "Detroit of India" = Chennai** (automobiles).
8. **Tirupur (Tamil Nadu) = "T-Shirt City / Knitwear Capital"** — largest knitwear exporter.
9. **SAIL HQ = New Delhi; HAL HQ = Bengaluru; BEL HQ = Bengaluru; BDL HQ = Hyderabad; MDL HQ = Mumbai**.
10. **Cochin Shipyard (Kochi) = India's largest shipyard** — built INS Vikrant, India's first indigenous aircraft carrier (2022).
11. **ISRO HQ = Bengaluru; VSSC (launch vehicles) = Thiruvananthapuram; Sriharikota (AP) = only launch pad**.
12. **Vikram Sarabhai = Father of Indian Space Programme** (NOT Homi Bhabha — Bhabha = nuclear; Sarabhai = space).
13. **Chandrayaan-3 (2023) = first to land on Moon's South Pole**; India = 4th country to land on Moon (USA, USSR, China were first three).
14. **Mangalyaan (2014) = India reached Mars on FIRST ATTEMPT** — no other country managed this.
15. **India = "Pharmacy of the World"** — world's largest generic medicine producer; Hyderabad = India's pharma hub.
16. **IFFCO = world's largest fertiliser cooperative**; HQ New Delhi — PSU vs. cooperative distinction matters.
