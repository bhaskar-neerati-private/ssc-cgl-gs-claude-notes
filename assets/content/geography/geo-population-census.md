# Population, Census 2011 & Urbanisation

## Counting a Nation

Every ten years, India does something extraordinary: it counts every single person on its vast territory. The Census is the largest peacetime administrative exercise in the world. The 2011 Census — the 15th since 1872, the 7th since independence — revealed a nation of 1.21 billion people in transition: more literate, more urban, growing slower than ever, yet still deeply unequal across states.

---

## History of the Census

| Year | Significance |
|---|---|
| **1872** | First census ever conducted in India (non-synchronous; different provinces counted at different times); under **Lord Mayo** |
| **1881** | First **synchronous** census (all of India counted at the same time); under **Lord Ripon**; every 10 years since |
| 1947 onward | Post-independence censuses: 1951, 1961, 1971, 1981, 1991, 2001, 2011 |
| **2011** | 15th census since 1872; 7th since independence; Census Commissioner: C. Chandramauli |
| 2021 | Postponed due to COVID-19 pandemic; not completed as of 2025 |

**Who conducts it?** The **Registrar General and Census Commissioner of India** — under the Ministry of Home Affairs.

## Demographic Concepts

### Demographic Transition

India has moved through the classic **demographic transition**:

| Stage | Description | India's history |
|---|---|---|
| Stage 1 | High birth rate + High death rate = stable (low) population | Pre-colonial India |
| Stage 2 | High birth rate + Falling death rate = rapid growth | 1921–1980 (population explosion) |
| Stage 3 | Falling birth rate + Low death rate = slowing growth | 1980–present |
| Stage 4 | Low birth + Low death = stable | Goal — southern states already here |

**India (2011) is in Stage 3** — growth slowing but still positive. Kerala, Tamil Nadu, and Andhra Pradesh are in Stage 4 already.

### Demographic Dividend

When the **working-age population (15–59 years) is large relative to dependents** (children + elderly), economic output rises if productive employment is available.

- India's working-age share (2011): ~62.5% — a huge dividend window
- India's dividend window: approximately **2020–2040**
- If jobs match the workforce: economic boom
- If not: demographic disaster ("youth bulge" with unemployment)

### Key Fertility and Mortality Data

| Indicator | India Average (2011 approx.) | Best State | Worst State |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Total Fertility Rate (TFR)** | ~2.4 | Kerala (~1.8) | Bihar (~3.7) |
| **Infant Mortality Rate (IMR)** | ~44/1,000 live births | Kerala (~12) | MP (~59) |
| **Maternal Mortality Rate (MMR)** | ~167/1,00,000 | Kerala (~66) | Assam (~300+) |
| **Life Expectancy** | ~67 years | Kerala (~75) | — |

Kerala dominates every positive health indicator in India — the "Kerala Model" of development through education and healthcare investment is studied worldwide.

## Family Planning — A Historic First

**1952:** India became the **world's first country to launch a national family planning programme** — a remarkable policy decision just 5 years after independence.

The programme has evolved enormously:
- Early 1970s: Forced sterilisation campaigns (especially under Emergency, 1975–77) — deeply scarred public trust
- Post-1977: Shift to voluntary, incentive-based programmes
- **National Population Policy 2000:** Target TFR of 2.1 (replacement level) by 2010; gradually being approached nationally

## Urbanisation in India

### What Counts as "Urban"?

India uses **three criteria for Census Towns:**
1. Population **≥ 5,000**
2. **≥ 75%** of male workers in **non-agricultural** activities
3. Density **≥ 400 persons/km²**

**Statutory towns** (with municipal bodies) are automatically urban regardless of these numbers.

### Urban India by Numbers (2011)

- Urban share: **31.16%** (first time crossed 30%)
- Urban population: ~377 million — **3rd largest urban population in the world** (after China and USA)
- Number of cities with population ≥ 10 lakh (1 million): **53 cities** (Census 2011)
- Number of cities with population ≥ 50 lakh: **8 megacities**

## India 2023 — The World's Most Populous Nation

Census 2011 showed India as **2nd most populous (after China)**. In **April 2023**, the UN's World Population Prospects declared India had **surpassed China** — ~1.429 billion people vs China's ~1.426 billion.

India is now the **world's most populous country**.

Census 2021 was delayed by COVID-19 and had not been completed as of 2025.

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Bihar = most densely populated STATE (1,102/km²)**; Delhi UT = most densely populated unit (11,320/km²) — state vs. UT distinction matters.
2. **Arunachal Pradesh = least densely populated state (17/km²)** — vast area, small population.
3. **UP = most populous state; Sikkim = least populous state** (Census 2011).
4. **Kerala = highest sex ratio (1,084)** — more women than men; the only large state with this; also highest literacy, female literacy, life expectancy, lowest IMR.
5. **Haryana = lowest sex ratio (877) AND lowest child sex ratio (834)** — worst demographic indicators for women, both at once.
6. **Bihar = lowest literacy rate (63.82%)**; Kerala = highest (94.0%).
7. **Meghalaya = highest decadal growth rate (27.82%)**; Nagaland = only state with NEGATIVE growth (−0.47%) — unique distinction SSC loves.
8. **First synchronous census: 1881 (Lord Ripon)**; first ever census: 1872 (Lord Mayo) — two different "firsts."
9. **Census 2011 total population: 121.08 crore; sex ratio: 943; density: 382/km²; literacy: 74.04%; urban: 31.16%** — these five headline numbers together.
10. **Child sex ratio (919) is WORSE than adult sex ratio (943)** — meaning discrimination is intensifying, not improving, in younger generations.
11. **Mumbai = largest urban agglomeration (Census 2011, ~18.4 million)**; Delhi = 2nd; Kolkata = 3rd.
12. **Goa = most urbanised state (62.2%)**; Delhi UT = most urbanised unit (97.5%); HP = least urbanised state (10%).
13. **India was FIRST country to adopt national family planning programme (1952)** — a distinctive historical "first" that SSC tests.
14. **Mizoram = highest ST proportion (~94.4%)** among states; **Punjab = highest SC proportion (31.9%)**.
15. **India crossed 30% urban population for the FIRST TIME in Census 2011 (31.16%)** — milestone urbanisation fact.
16. **India became world's most populous country in 2023 (surpassing China)** — important update; Census 2011 showed India 2nd; now 1st.
