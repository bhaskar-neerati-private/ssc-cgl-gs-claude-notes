# Transport & Communication — Roads, Railways, Ports & Airways

## The Arteries of a Billion-Person Economy

Transport is where geography becomes economics. India's 3.3 million km road network, 67,000 km rail network, 12 major ports, and hundreds of airports move people, goods, coal, iron, and food across a subcontinent. This lesson covers the facts SSC tests relentlessly — the longest, the first, the deepest, the largest.

---

## Roads

India has the **world's 2nd largest road network** (after the USA) in total length.

**Road classification (by maintenance authority):**
- **National Highways (NH):** Central government (NHAI); 2% of road length but 40% of total traffic
- **State Highways (SH):** State PWDs
- **District Roads:** District panchayats / local bodies
- **Village/Rural Roads:** PMGSY scheme

**NHAI (National Highways Authority of India):**
- HQ: **New Delhi**
- Autonomous body under Ministry of Road Transport & Highways
- Implements the National Highway Development Project (NHDP)

### Key National Highways

| Highway | Length | Route | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NH-44** | **~3,745 km** | **Srinagar → Kanyakumari** | **India's longest National Highway**; runs north-south |
| **NH-27** | ~3,507 km | Porbandar (Gujarat) → Silchar (Assam) | East-west corridor |
| **NH-48** | ~2,807 km | Delhi → Chennai | Former NH-8 extended |

**Old NH numbering vs. new:** Many textbooks still cite NH-7 (old number) as the longest. Under the **new numbering (post-2010)**, NH-44 is the longest. SSC questions may use either — context tells you which era.

### Golden Quadrilateral

A 4-lane (later 6-lane in parts) highway connecting India's four metro cities:

**Delhi — Mumbai — Chennai — Kolkata**

- Total length: ~5,846 km
- Part of **NHDP (National Highway Development Project)**
- Launched in **2001 under PM Atal Bihari Vajpayee**; managed by **NHAI**
- The largest highway project in India at the time of launch

### North-South and East-West Corridors

- **North-South Corridor:** Srinagar (J&K) → Kanyakumari (Tamil Nadu)
- **East-West Corridor:** Porbandar (Gujarat) → Silchar (Assam)
- **Intersection point: Jhansi (Uttar Pradesh)** — both corridors cross here

### PMGSY

**Pradhan Mantri Gram Sadak Yojana (PMGSY):**
- Launched: **2000**, under **Atal Bihari Vajpayee**
- Goal: All-weather road connectivity to every unconnected habitation above a minimum population threshold
- Most transformative rural road scheme in India's history

## Railways

**Indian Railways facts:**
- **World's 4th largest rail network** by route length (USA → China → Russia → India)
- Also described as the **2nd largest under single management** in the world
- Total route: **~67,000 km**
- **Largest employer in India** — over 12 lakh employees (1.2 million)
- HQ: **Rail Bhavan, New Delhi**

### Rail Gauges

| Gauge | Width | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Broad Gauge (BG)** | **1,676 mm** | **~90% of India's network**; most trains run on this |
| **Metre Gauge (MG)** | 1,000 mm | Being progressively converted to BG under "Project Unigauge" |
| **Narrow Gauge (NG)** | 762 mm / 610 mm | Hill railways; heritage trains |

### The First Railway in India (and Asia)

**16 April 1853** — India's **first passenger train** ran from **Bori Bunder (Mumbai, now CST area) to Thane** — **34 km**; under British rule (Great Indian Peninsular Railway / GIPR). This was also **Asia's first railway**.

### Mountain Railways — UNESCO World Heritage Sites

All three are part of **"Mountain Railways of India"** — jointly inscribed on UNESCO's World Heritage List:

| Railway | State | Gauge | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Darjeeling Himalayan Railway (DHR)** | **West Bengal** | Narrow (610 mm) | "Toy Train"; opened 1881; loops and zig-zags |
| **Nilgiri Mountain Railway (NMR)** | **Tamil Nadu** | Metre gauge | Mettupalayam to Ooty; rack-and-pinion system; opened 1908 |
| **Kalka-Shimla Railway** | **Himachal Pradesh** | Narrow (762 mm) | Opened 1903; over 800 bridges, 100+ tunnels |

### Notable Trains

| Train | Note |
|---|---|
| **Vande Bharat Express** | Semi-high speed; manufactured at ICF Chennai; India's fastest indigenous train |
| **Gatimaan Express** | Currently fastest train in India (160 km/h); Delhi-Agra |
| **Rajdhani Express** | Delhi to state capitals; fully air-conditioned |
| **Shatabdi Express** | Daytime high-speed intercity |
| **Deccan Queen** | **Oldest superfast train** in India (since 1930); Mumbai–Pune |

### Konkan Railway

- Coastal railway along the **Konkan coast** — Roha (Maharashtra) to Mangaluru (Karnataka)
- One of India's most challenging railway projects: Western Ghats, 2,000+ bridges, 92 tunnels
- Operated by **Konkan Railway Corporation Limited**; inaugurated **1998**

### Metro Railways

- **First metro in India:** **Kolkata Metro (1984)** — East-West line under Esplanade
- **Largest metro network:** **Delhi Metro** — 350+ km; DMRC (Delhi Metro Rail Corporation)
- Other metros: Bengaluru (Namma Metro), Mumbai, Chennai (CMRL), Hyderabad (Metro Rail), Kochi, Lucknow

| **Mumbai Port** | Maharashtra | Natural harbour; oldest operating major port |
| **Jawaharlal Nehru Port (JNPT)** | Maharashtra (Navi Mumbai) | **India's largest container port**; handles most of India's container traffic |
| **Mormugao Port** | Goa | Major iron ore export port (to Japan, South Korea) |
| **New Mangalore Port** | Karnataka | — |

## Inland Waterways

India has **111 National Waterways** (declared under National Waterways Act 2016).

| Waterway | Route | Length | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NW-1** | Ganga-Bhagirathi-Hooghly (Allahabad/Prayagraj → Haldia) | **1,620 km** | **India's longest and most commercially used national waterway** |
| **NW-2** | Brahmaputra (Sadiya → Dhubri, Assam) | 891 km | Navigable stretch in Assam |
| **NW-3** | West Coast Canal, Kerala (Kottapuram → Kollam) | 205 km | Kerala backwaters |

**IWAI (Inland Waterways Authority of India):**
- HQ: **Noida, Uttar Pradesh**
- Develops and maintains national waterways

India = **world's 3rd largest domestic aviation market** (after USA and China).

**Major Airports:**

| Airport | City | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Indira Gandhi International (IGI)** | **New Delhi** | **India's largest and busiest airport** |
| **Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International** | Mumbai | 2nd busiest |
| **Kempegowda International** | Bengaluru | — |
| **Rajiv Gandhi International** | Hyderabad | — |

| **Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International** | Kolkata | — |
| **Cochin International Airport** | Kochi | **World's first fully solar-powered airport** |

**Aviation firsts:**
- **First commercial flight in India:** 18 February 1911 — **Allahabad to Naini** (8.8 km); mail carried; pilot **Henri Pequet**
- **Air India:** Founded as Tata Airlines (1932) by **J.R.D. Tata** (India's first commercial pilot); nationalised 1953; privatised back to Tata Group **2022**

**Regulatory bodies:**
- **DGCA (Directorate General of Civil Aviation):** Safety regulator; HQ **New Delhi**
- **AAI (Airports Authority of India):** Manages most airports; HQ **New Delhi**

## Pipelines

Pipelines efficiently transport crude oil, petroleum products, and natural gas across long distances.

**GAIL (Gas Authority of India Limited):**
- India's largest natural gas transmission company
- **Maharatna PSU**
- HQ: **New Delhi**
- Major pipeline: **HBJ Pipeline** (Hazira–Vijaipur–Jagdishpur) — first major cross-country gas pipeline

**Key oil pipelines:**
- Nahorkatiya (Assam) → Barauni (Bihar) → Kanpur (UP) — crude oil from northeast
- Paradip → Hyderabad — HPCL petroleum products
- Jamnagar → Loni (UP) — Reliance refinery products to North India

## Communication

**India Post:**
- **World's largest postal network** by number of post offices (~1.55 lakh post offices)
- Part of Department of Posts; HQ **New Delhi**
- Includes rural branches — the **Branch Post Office** is the most rural communication anchor

**Telecom:**
- **BSNL (Bharat Sanchar Nigam Ltd):** Largest government-owned telecom; HQ **New Delhi**
- **TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India):** Telecom regulator; HQ **New Delhi**
- India has one of the world's cheapest mobile data rates (post-Jio launch, 2016)
- Over 1.1 billion mobile subscribers — one of the world's largest networks

**Satellites (Communication):**
- **INSAT series** (Indian National Satellite System): Telecom, TV broadcasting, meteorology, disaster warning
- **GSAT series:** Dedicated communication satellites

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **NH-44 = India's longest National Highway** (~3,745 km; Srinagar to Kanyakumari). Old numbering called it NH-7 — if the exam uses old numbers, the answer is NH-7; if new numbers, NH-44.
2. **Golden Quadrilateral = Delhi–Mumbai–Chennai–Kolkata** (4 cities only; NOT 6 or 8 cities).
3. **NS-EW corridor intersection point = Jhansi, Uttar Pradesh** — a static fact SSC repeats.
4. **First railway in India AND Asia: 16 April 1853, Bori Bunder (Mumbai) → Thane, 34 km**.
5. **Indian Railways = 4th largest rail network globally** (USA, China, Russia, India) OR 2nd largest under a single management.
6. **Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, Nilgiri Mountain Railway, Kalka-Shimla Railway = all three are UNESCO World Heritage Sites** (Mountain Railways of India) — tested as a set.
7. **Kolkata Metro = first metro in India (1984)**; Delhi Metro = largest metro network — common mix-up.
8. **Kandla/Deendayal Port (Gujarat) = India's largest port by TOTAL CARGO** — largest overall.
9. **JNPT (Navi Mumbai) = India's largest CONTAINER port** — largest for containers specifically. Kandla ≠ JNPT. Both are in the same "largest" category but for different metrics.
10. **Visakhapatnam = India's deepest port** (natural deep-water harbour).
11. **Kolkata Port = India's only major RIVER port** (on Hooghly, not directly on sea).
12. **NW-1 (Ganga, Prayagraj to Haldia, 1,620 km) = India's longest national waterway**.
13. **Kochi Airport = world's first fully solar-powered airport** — this is a current-affairs-turned-static fact.
14. **Indira Gandhi International Airport, Delhi = India's largest and busiest airport**.
15. **India Post = world's largest postal network** by post office count (~1.55 lakh).
16. **Air India was founded as Tata Airlines (1932) by J.R.D. Tata** — privatised back to Tata Group in 2022 after 69 years of government ownership; first commercial flight in India was 1911 (Henri Pequet, Allahabad to Naini).
