## Freshwater Lakes

### Wular Lake — J&K
- **Largest natural freshwater lake in India**
- Located in the Kashmir Valley; formed on the **Jhelum River** (the river expands into a lake-like basin due to a tectonic depression)
- Size varies dramatically: ~30 sq km in dry season → ~260 sq km in flood season
- Ramsar site; supports willow cultivation and fresh water fisheries
- Do not confuse with Dal Lake (also J&K but smaller, and famous for houseboats)

### Dal Lake — Srinagar, J&K
- Srinagar's iconic lake, ringed by the Zabarwan range and the city
- Famous for: houseboats (shikaras), floating gardens (**Rad**), floating vegetable markets
- Also a Ramsar site; rapidly shrinking due to encroachment and silt
- Second largest lake in J&K (after Wular)

### Loktak Lake — Manipur
- **Largest freshwater lake in Northeast India**
- Famous for **phumdis** — unique floating masses of vegetation, soil, and organic matter that drift on the lake surface
- **Keibul Lamjao National Park** sits on the phumdis — the **world's only floating national park**
- Home to the **Sangai** (Manipur's state animal; brow-antlered deer / dancing deer), which also lives on the phumdis
- Ramsar site (1990)

### Pangong Tso — Ladakh
- High-altitude lake at ~4,350 m elevation; 134 km long, narrow (6 km wide)
- **Brackish** (saline), not freshwater — despite the "tso" meaning lake in Tibetan
- About 40% in India (Ladakh), 60% in China (Tibet) — an endorheic (landlocked, no outflow) basin
- Cannot be classified as "Indian" in its entirety
- Famous post-2020 Galwan standoff and from the Bollywood film *3 Idiots* (Rancho's school scenes)
- Does **not** flow into any river

### Tso Moriri — Ladakh
- Another high-altitude lake in Ladakh (4,522 m)
- Ramsar site; bar-headed geese and black-necked cranes breed here
- Entirely within Indian territory (unlike Pangong)

### Roopkund Lake — Uttarakhand
- "Skeleton Lake" — at 5,029 m altitude in the Chamoli district
- Famous for hundreds of ancient human skeletons found preserved in ice; thought to be pilgrims who died in a hailstorm ~9th century CE (DNA studies suggest multiple events across centuries)

### Nainital Lake — Uttarakhand
- Famous hill resort; eye-shaped (Nainital = "Eye Lake" in Hindi)
- A landslide-dammed lake

## Saline / Brackish Lakes

### Sambhar Lake — Rajasthan
- **Largest inland saline lake in India**
- Located near Jaipur, Rajasthan — tectonic (fault-controlled) origin
- India's largest producer of inland salt (exploited since ancient times — the Sambhar Salt Works)
- Ramsar site; flamingo habitat
- Fed by rivers Mendha, Rupangarh, Khari, Khandela — all seasonal

### Chilika Lake — Odisha
- **Largest coastal lagoon in India** (~1,100 sq km)
- Brackish (mix of freshwater from rivers and saltwater from the sea)
- India's **first Ramsar site (1981)** — along with Keoladeo Ghana National Park
- Famous for: migratory birds (bar-headed geese, flamingoes from Siberia/Central Asia), **Irrawaddy dolphins**, the Nalabana Bird Sanctuary within it
- Connected to the Bay of Bengal through a narrow mouth near Satapada
- Puri, the famous pilgrimage town, is close by

### Pulicat Lake — AP/TN border
- India's **second largest coastal lagoon** (~460 sq km)
- Separated from Bay of Bengal by a sand bar island — **Sriharikota** — where ISRO's Satish Dhawan Space Centre (satellite launch station) is located
- Mainly in Andhra Pradesh with a small portion in Tamil Nadu

### Lonar Lake — Maharashtra
- India's **only meteor-impact crater lake** — formed by a meteorite strike ~50,000 years ago
- Located in Buldhana district, Maharashtra
- Both **saline and alkaline** (pH around 10.5) — a rare chemical combination that supports unique microorganisms
- The crater itself is lined with basaltic rock and contains several ancient temples on its rim
- Became a **Ramsar site in 2020**

### Kolleru Lake — Andhra Pradesh
- Freshwater lake (technically, but shrinking) located between the Krishna and Godavari deltas
- Ramsar site; important for migratory birds; famous for aquaculture (fish farming)

## Islands of India

### Andaman & Nicobar Islands — Bay of Bengal

A chain of 572 islands, islets, rocks, and shoals in the Bay of Bengal. Only 36–38 are permanently inhabited.

- **Union Territory** with capital at **Port Blair** (South Andaman)
- Geologically: the islands are the exposed peaks of a submerged mountain range — a continuation of the **Arakan Yoma** (Myanmar mountains)
- Total area: ~8,249 sq km

**Key Islands:**

| Island | Key Facts |
|---|---|
| **North Andaman** | Saddle Peak (highest in A&N at 732 m); interviews with North Andaman are needed for Barren Island access |
| **South Andaman** | Port Blair; Ross Island (British colonial HQ); Cellular Jail (Kala Pani) |
| **Little Andaman** | Southernmost of the Andaman group; separated from South Andaman by Duncan Passage |
| **Barren Island** | India's **only active volcano**; eruption in 2017, 2020; located in North Andaman district |
| **Narcondam Island** | India's **only dormant volcano**; part of the Andaman group; bird sanctuary (Narcondam hornbill) |
| **North Sentinel Island** | Home to the **Sentinelese** — one of the world's last uncontacted peoples (PVTG); landing prohibited by law |
| **Great Nicobar Island** | Southernmost island; contains **Indira Point** = India's southernmost point |

**Cellular Jail (Kala Pani):** The famous British colonial prison in Port Blair where Indian freedom fighters were imprisoned and tortured — Veer Savarkar was its most famous prisoner.

**Indira Point** (formerly Pygmalion Point/Parson's Point) on Great Nicobar Island = **India's southernmost point** (6°45'N latitude, approximately).

**Channels within Andaman & Nicobar:**

| Channel | Separates |
|---|---|
| **Coco Channel** | North Andaman from Coco Islands (Myanmar) |
| **Duncan Passage** | South Andaman from Little Andaman |
| **Ten Degree Channel** | Andaman Islands (Little Andaman) from Nicobar Islands (Car Nicobar) |

### Lakshadweep — Arabian Sea

A group of coral atolls in the Arabian Sea, off the coast of Kerala.

- **36 islands**, only 11 inhabited; total area only ~32 sq km — **smallest Union Territory of India by area**
- **Capital:** Kavaratti
- Religion: **Predominantly Muslim** — only Muslim-majority UT of India
- Geology: Coral atolls (ring-shaped coral reefs surrounding a lagoon)
- Economy: fishing, tourism, copra (dried coconut)

**Key Islands and Channels:**

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Minicoy Island** | Southernmost island of Lakshadweep; culturally distinct (Mahl language, not Malayalam) |
| **Agatti Island** | Has the only airport in Lakshadweep |
| **Nine Degree Channel** | Separates **Minicoy** from the main Lakshadweep group |
| **Eight Degree Channel** | Separates Lakshadweep from the **Maldives** |
| **Pitti Island** | Uninhabited; bird sanctuary; nesting site for sea turtles |

## Straits and Channels

### Between India and Sri Lanka

| Water Body | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Palk Strait** | Separates Tamil Nadu (India) from Jaffna/Northern Sri Lanka; very shallow (7–9 m); allows fishing boats but not cargo ships |
| **Palk Bay** | The bay-like extension of the Palk Strait, between the Coromandel Coast and Sri Lanka's northern shores |
| **Gulf of Mannar** | The broader, deeper body of water south of the Palk Strait, between southern Tamil Nadu and southern Sri Lanka; **Gulf of Mannar Marine Biosphere Reserve** (UNESCO); historically famous for pearl fishing |
| **Pamban Channel** | Narrow channel between Pamban Island (Rameswaram) and the mainland; crossed by the Pamban Bridge |

**Adam's Bridge (Ram Setu):** A chain of limestone shoals/sandbanks stretching ~50 km between Pamban Island (India) and Mannar Island (Sri Lanka), visible from satellite. Hindu tradition identifies it as the bridge built by Lord Rama's army; geological evidence suggests it was a land connection above sea level about 7,000 years ago. The proposed **Sethusamudram Shipping Canal Project** would dredge through this to create a navigable channel (controversial and currently shelved).

## Exam Traps — 18 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Wular Lake = largest NATURAL FRESHWATER lake** (J&K; Jhelum River). Gobind Sagar = largest ARTIFICIAL lake.
2. **Chilika = largest COASTAL LAGOON** (Odisha; brackish, NOT freshwater). India's FIRST Ramsar site (1981).
3. **Sambhar = largest INLAND SALINE lake** (Rajasthan) — inland means NOT connected to the sea (unlike Chilika/Pulicat).
4. **Loktak Lake = largest freshwater lake in NORTHEAST INDIA** (Manipur). Keibul Lamjao = world's ONLY floating national park.
5. **Lonar Lake = only meteor-impact crater lake** in India; both saline AND alkaline; Maharashtra (Buldhana).
6. **Barren Island = India's only ACTIVE volcano** (Andaman). Narcondam = India's only DORMANT volcano (Andaman).
7. **Indira Point (Great Nicobar)** = India's southernmost point. Do NOT say Kanyakumari (that's the southernmost point of the MAINLAND).
8. **Ten Degree Channel** = separates Andaman from Nicobar. (10°N latitude passes through it.)
9. **Nine Degree Channel** = separates Minicoy from main Lakshadweep group.
10. **Eight Degree Channel** = separates Lakshadweep from Maldives.
11. **Lakshadweep = smallest UT of India by area** (~32 sq km); only Muslim-majority UT; in Arabian Sea (NOT Bay of Bengal).
12. **Andaman & Nicobar** = in Bay of Bengal (NOT Arabian Sea — Lakshadweep is Arabian Sea).
13. **Sriharikota (ISRO)** is on the sand bar of **Pulicat Lake** — second largest lagoon (AP-TN border).
14. **Abdul Kalam Island** (formerly Wheeler Island) = DRDO missile test range; Odisha. Renamed 2015.
15. **Pamban Bridge** = India's first sea bridge; connects Rameswaram (Pamban Island) to mainland Tamil Nadu.
16. **Palk Strait = too shallow for cargo ships** — hence the Sethusamudram Canal Project controversy (to deepen a channel for shipping).
17. **North Sentinel Island = uncontacted Sentinelese tribe** — protected from contact by Indian law. Landing is illegal.
18. **Keoladeo Ghana NP + Chilika Lake** = India's first TWO Ramsar sites, both designated simultaneously in **1981**.
