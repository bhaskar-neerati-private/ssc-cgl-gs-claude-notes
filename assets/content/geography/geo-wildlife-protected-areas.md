# Wildlife, National Parks, Tiger Reserves & Biosphere Reserves

## The Legal Foundation — Wildlife Protection Act, 1972

India's entire protected area network rests on the **Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972** — passed under PM Indira Gandhi, who was a passionate wildlife conservationist. The Act defines:

- **National Parks** — highest level of protection; no human habitation; no resource extraction; no grazing; boundaries fixed by law
- **Wildlife Sanctuaries** — some human activities permitted; buffers around NPs
- **Conservation Reserves / Community Reserves** — newer categories; local communities involved

**Species Protection Schedules:**

| Schedule | Level | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **I & II** | Absolute protection — cannot be hunted under any circumstances; maximum penalties | Tiger, lion, rhinoceros, snow leopard, gharial, elephant |
| III & IV | Protected but lesser penalties | Porcupine, some deer |
| **V** | Vermin — can be killed (pests) | Rats, mice, common crow |
| **VI** | Protected plants | Cycas (sago palm), Blue Vanda orchid, Red Vanda |

## Project Tiger — India's Flagship Conservation Programme

**Launched:** **1973** by PM **Indira Gandhi**; first reserve: **Jim Corbett, Uttarakhand**

**Why 1972-1973?** The 1972 census showed fewer than 1,800 tigers left in India. Indira Gandhi personally championed the programme; banned tiger hunting and skin trade; launched Project Tiger on **April 1, 1973**.

**Current status:**
- **53 Tiger Reserves** across India (as of 2023)
- India holds **~70% of the world's wild tiger population**
- Tiger population: ~3,167 (2022 census — most recent All India Tiger Estimation)
- **Administered by:** National Tiger Conservation Authority (NTCA) — set up under the WPA amendment 2006
- Tiger census method: camera traps + pugmark identification + DNA from scat

**First Tiger Reserve:** Jim Corbett (1973)
**Largest Tiger Reserve (by area):** Nagarjunasagar-Srisailam (AP/Telangana) — ~3,728 sq km

### Critically Important Wildlife

**One-horned Indian Rhinoceros:**
- Found: Kaziranga NP (Assam) — 75%+ of world population; Pobitora WS (Assam) — highest rhino density in the world; Manas NP; Dudhwa NP (UP, reintroduced); Chitwan (Nepal)
- Kaziranga is the stronghold — about 2,600+ rhinos

**Asiatic Lion (Gir Lion):**
- Found **exclusively** in Gir NP and surrounding areas (Junagadh, Gujarat)
- ~600+ individuals — only wild population in Asia outside Africa for all lion species
- A proposed second habitat: Kuno National Park (MP) was approved by the Supreme Court for lion relocation — but the Gujarat government has resisted
- Smaller, shaggier mane than African lion; fold of skin on belly

**Great Indian Bustard (GIB):**
- Critically endangered — fewer than 200 remaining
- Found in: Desert NP (Rajasthan), Kutch (Gujarat), Sorasan GS (Rajasthan)
- Major threat: overhead power transmission lines (they fly into wires — have poor frontal vision)
- Rajasthan state bird

**Gharial (*Gavialis gangeticus*):**
- Critically endangered; long, narrow snout with a distinctive bulbous growth (ghara) at the tip of males
- Found only in the Chambal River (National Chambal Sanctuary), Girwa River, and upper Ganga
- Project Crocodile (1975) — National Chambal Sanctuary is prime gharial habitat

**Golden Langur (*Trachypithecus geei*):**
- Found only in Assam (Bodoland area, Chakrashila WS) and western Bhutan
- One of the rarest primates; Schedule I; golden-cream fur

**Hangul / Kashmir Stag (*Cervus hanglu hanglu*):**
- Critically endangered; only found in Dachigam NP, J&K
- India's rarest deer; about 200 individuals remaining

## Three-Layer Protected Area Hierarchy

India's protected areas work in concentric zones:

```
NATIONAL PARK (strictest — no human activities at all)
  |
WILDLIFE SANCTUARY (buffer — limited human use permitted)
  |
BIOSPHERE RESERVE (largest — three zones: Core/NP, Buffer/WS, Transition zone)
```

**Example — Nilgiri Biosphere Reserve:**
- Core zone: National Parks and WS (Mudumalai NP, Nagarhole NP, Bandipur NP, Wayanad WS, Silent Valley NP, etc.)
- Buffer zone: managed forests around the cores
- Transition zone: where human habitation and sustainable use are allowed

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Jim Corbett = oldest NP in India (1936)** AND **first Tiger Reserve (1973)** — two different firsts in the same park.
2. **Gir NP (Gujarat) = ONLY wild Asiatic Lions in the world** — not Africa (African lions), not anywhere else in India. If examiners say "Kuno NP" — that's the proposed second habitat, not yet operational for lions.
3. **Kaziranga = highest ONE-HORNED RHINO population** (not "largest NP" — Hemis is largest by area). Kaziranga is in Assam, not Odisha or anywhere else.
4. **Hemis NP = largest NP in India** (by area) — in Ladakh; Snow Leopard habitat. NOT Jim Corbett.
5. **Keibul Lamjao = world's ONLY floating national park** — in Manipur (Loktak Lake phumdis); Sangai deer.
6. **Namdapha NP = only NP in India with all four big cats** (tiger, leopard, clouded leopard, snow leopard).
7. **National Animal = Tiger** (NOT lion — lion was replaced by tiger in 1973 when Project Tiger launched).
8. **National Aquatic Animal = Gangetic/River Dolphin (Susu)** — NOT fish, NOT whale.
9. **Project Tiger started 1973; Project Elephant 1992; Project Crocodile 1975** — dates are tested.
10. **Sariska NP** had all its tigers poached; tigers were **reintroduced from Ranthambore** — both are in Rajasthan.
11. **Keoladeo Ghana NP (Bharatpur)** = famous for birds, NOT tigers; UNESCO WHS; Ramsar site.
12. **White tigers were first seen in the wild in Bandhavgarh/Rewa area (MP)** — not Sundarbans, not Assam.
13. **Golden Langur** = found only in Assam + western Bhutan; associated with Manas NP and Chakrashila WS.
14. **Hangul (Kashmir Stag)** = only in Dachigam NP, J&K — critically endangered; ~200 individuals.
15. **Great Indian Bustard** = state bird of Rajasthan; found in Desert NP (Jaisalmer); critically endangered; power lines are a major threat (they collide with overhead wires).
16. **Silent Valley NP (Kerala)** = saved from a hydroelectric dam in 1980s; last intact tropical rainforest in India; Lion-tailed Macaque; part of Nilgiri BR.
