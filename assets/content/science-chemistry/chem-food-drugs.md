# Food Chemistry, Drugs & Common Medicines — Antiseptics, Disinfectants, Anaesthetics & Food Additives

## The Chemistry on Your Dinner Table (and in Your Medicine Cabinet)

In 1928, Alexander Fleming returned from holiday to find a mould contaminating one of his Petri dishes — and killing the bacteria around it. The mould was *Penicillium notatum*. The substance it produced was **penicillin** — the first antibiotic, which would go on to save an estimated 200 million lives.

In 1899, a Bayer chemist synthesised **acetylsalicylic acid** — aspirin — from a chemical in willow bark that humans had been chewing for fever relief since antiquity. It became the most widely sold drug in history.

Both stories share a common thread: the intersection of chemistry and medicine. This lesson covers the chemistry behind what you eat and what you take when you're sick — two domains that SSC tests with surprising regularity.

## Part 1 — Food Chemistry: What's in Food?

### Macronutrients

| Nutrient | Chemical Nature | Caloric Value | Key Function |
|----------|---------------|---------------|-------------|
| **Carbohydrates** | Sugars, starches, fibre (C, H, O) | 4 kcal/g | Primary energy source |
| **Proteins** | Amino acid chains (C, H, O, N, S) | 4 kcal/g | Growth, repair, enzymes, hormones |
| **Fats (Lipids)** | Fatty acids + glycerol (C, H, O) | **9 kcal/g** — highest | Energy store, cell membranes, fat-soluble vitamins |
| **Water** | H₂O | 0 kcal | Solvent, metabolic reactions, temperature regulation |
| **Dietary Fibre** | Cellulose, pectin | ~2 kcal/g (not absorbed) | Gut health, prevents constipation |

### Carbohydrates — The Energy Architects

- **Monosaccharides** (single sugars): Glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆), Fructose, Galactose
- **Disaccharides** (two sugars joined): 
  - **Sucrose** = Glucose + Fructose (table sugar)
  - **Lactose** = Glucose + Galactose (milk sugar)
  - **Maltose** = Glucose + Glucose (malt sugar; from starch digestion)
- **Polysaccharides** (many sugars):
  - **Starch** = storage carbohydrate in plants (amylose + amylopectin)
  - **Glycogen** = storage carbohydrate in animals (liver, muscle)
  - **Cellulose** = structural carbohydrate in plant cell walls (humans cannot digest — dietary fibre)

**Glucose is the body's primary fuel** — brain depends almost entirely on glucose. Blood glucose normal range: 70–110 mg/dL (fasting).

### Fats — Saturated vs Unsaturated

| Type | Structure | Example | Health Note |
|------|-----------|---------|------------|
| **Saturated fat** | Single bonds only; solid at room temperature | Butter, ghee, coconut oil, lard | Associated with raised LDL cholesterol |
| **Unsaturated fat (mono)** | One double bond | Olive oil, mustard oil | Associated with heart health |
| **Unsaturated fat (poly)** | Multiple double bonds | Sunflower oil, fish oil, flaxseed | Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids |
| **Trans fat** | Artificially hydrogenated unsaturated fat | Vanaspati, margarine, baked goods | Most harmful; raises LDL, lowers HDL; banned in India (2022 limit) |

**Hydrogenation**: Adding H₂ across C=C double bonds (using nickel catalyst) converts liquid oils to solid fats (vanaspati, margarine). The process creates some trans fats.

### Proteins — Building Blocks

- Made of **amino acids** (20 types in humans)
- **Essential amino acids** (cannot be synthesised by body; must come from diet): 9 amino acids including leucine, lysine, tryptophan, methionine, valine
- **Complete proteins** (contain all essential AAs): meat, fish, eggs, milk, soybean
- **Incomplete proteins** (lacking one or more essential AAs): most plant proteins (legumes are a good source but may lack methionine)

### Vitamins — The Micro-Managers

**Fat-soluble vitamins** (stored in body fat; can accumulate to toxic levels): A, D, E, K  
**Water-soluble vitamins** (not stored; excess excreted in urine): B complex, C

| Vitamin | Chemical Name | Deficiency Disease | Source |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| A | Retinol | Night blindness, Xerophthalmia | Liver, egg, carrot, green leafy vegetables |
| B1 | Thiamine | Beriberi | Whole grains, legumes |
| B2 | Riboflavin | Ariboflavinosis (skin, eye) | Milk, eggs, meat |
| B3 | Niacin | Pellagra (4 D's: Dermatitis, Diarrhoea, Dementia, Death) | Meat, peanuts |
| B6 | Pyridoxine | Anaemia, peripheral neuropathy | Poultry, fish, bananas |
| B9 | Folic Acid | Neural tube defects (during pregnancy), anaemia | Green leafy vegetables, citrus |
| B12 | Cobalamin | Pernicious anaemia, neurological damage | Only in animal products (meat, dairy, eggs) — deficiency common in vegans |
| C | Ascorbic Acid | **Scurvy** (bleeding gums, joint pain) | Citrus, guava, amla |
| D | Calciferol | **Rickets** (children), **Osteomalacia** (adults) | Sunlight, fish liver oil |
| E | Tocopherol | Muscle weakness, nerve damage | Nuts, seeds, plant oils |
| K | Phylloquinone | Excessive bleeding (clotting factor synthesis) | Green leafy vegetables |

## Part 2 — Food Additives

**Food additives** are substances added to food to preserve, colour, flavour, or improve texture.

### Types of Food Additives

| Type | Function | Examples | Notes |
|------|---------|---------|-------|
| **Preservatives** | Prevent microbial growth | Sodium benzoate (E211), Potassium sorbate, Salt, Sugar, Vinegar | Sodium benzoate most common in soft drinks |
| **Antioxidants** | Prevent rancidity (fat oxidation) | BHA, BHT, Vitamin C, Vitamin E, EDTA | Rancidity = oxidation of unsaturated fats → bad smell/taste |
| **Colours/Dyes** | Improve appearance | Tartrazine (yellow, E102), Sunset yellow, Erythrosine, Caramel | Some linked to hyperactivity in children |
| **Flavours** | Enhance taste/aroma | Vanilla extract, MSG (monosodium glutamate) | MSG is the sodium salt of glutamic acid (umami taste) |
| **Emulsifiers** | Keep oil and water mixed | Lecithin, Monoglycerides | Used in bread, ice cream, margarine |
| **Sweeteners** | Low-calorie sugar substitute | Saccharin, Aspartame, Sucralose, Steviol glycosides | |
| **Thickeners** | Improve texture/viscosity | Pectin, Guar gum, Xanthan gum, Agar | |
| **Leavening agents** | Make baked goods rise | Baking soda (NaHCO₃), Baking powder, Yeast | |

### Common Artificial Sweeteners

| Sweetener | Sweetness vs Sucrose | Notes |
|-----------|---------------------|-------|
| **Saccharin** | ~300× sweeter | First artificial sweetener (1879); no calories; controversial cancer link (later cleared) |
| **Aspartame** | ~200× sweeter | Contains phenylalanine — dangerous for PKU patients |
| **Sucralose** | ~600× sweeter | Made from sugar; passes through body unchanged; popular in baking |
| **Steviol glycosides** (Stevia) | ~200-400× sweeter | Natural (from Stevia plant); approved in India |

### Rancidity
When fats and oils undergo oxidation or hydrolysis → **rancid smell and taste**.
- **Oxidative rancidity**: unsaturated fats react with O₂; prevented by antioxidants (BHA, BHT, Vitamin C, E)
- **Hydrolytic rancidity**: fat hydrolysis by water/enzymes → free fatty acids
- **Prevention**: vacuum packaging, inert gas flushing (N₂), refrigeration, antioxidants, airtight containers

## Part 3 — Antiseptics and Disinfectants

| Term | Definition | Used On |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Antiseptic** | Kills/inhibits microorganisms on living tissue without damaging it | Wounds, skin, mucous membranes |
| **Disinfectant** | Kills microorganisms on non-living surfaces; too harsh for skin | Floors, instruments, toilets, water tanks |
| **Sterilisation** | Complete destruction of all microorganisms including spores | Surgical instruments, food canning |

### Important Antiseptics

| Antiseptic | Chemical | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Dettol** | Chloroxylenol (in pine oil + isopropanol) | Common household antiseptic |
| **Savlon** | Chlorhexidine + Cetrimide | Wound cleaning |
| **Iodine solution** | I₂ in KI/alcohol (tincture of iodine) | 2.5% solution on wounds |
| **Betadine** | Povidone-Iodine | Broad spectrum; pre/post-surgery |
| **Hydrogen Peroxide** | H₂O₂ (3%) | Releases nascent oxygen [O]; kills anaerobic bacteria; wound cleaning |
| **Phenol (Carbolic acid)** | C₆H₅OH | Lister's original antiseptic (1867); too irritating for skin now |
| **Boric acid** | H₃BO₃ | Eye wash, mild antiseptic |
| **Gentian violet** | Crystal violet dye | Fungal infections, wounds |

### Important Disinfectants

| Disinfectant | Chemical | Use |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| **Chlorine** | Cl₂ | Water treatment (0.2-0.5 ppm); kills bacteria, viruses |
| **Bleaching powder** | Ca(OCl)Cl | Toilet, water, floor disinfection |
| **Phenol** | C₆H₅OH | Disinfectant for floors, instruments (original Lysol) |
| **Formaldehyde** | HCHO | Sterilising surgical instruments; preserving specimens |
| **Lysol** | Cresol in soap solution | Floor and surface disinfection |
| **Potassium Permanganate** | KMnO₄ | Water purification, wounds (mild oxidiser) |
| **Ozone** | O₃ | Water disinfection (no chlorine taste/smell) |
| **UV radiation** | — | Water purifiers, air sterilisation; kills bacteria/viruses |

## Part 4 — Drugs and Medicines

**Drug**: any chemical that alters the body's normal physiological or psychological state.

### Analgesics (Pain Killers)

| Drug | Type | Mechanism/Notes |
|------|------|----------------|
| **Aspirin** (Acetylsalicylic acid) | NSAID | Inhibits COX enzymes → reduces prostaglandins; anti-inflammatory, antipyretic, antiplatelet |
| **Paracetamol** (Acetaminophen) | Analgesic/antipyretic | Safe at normal doses; liver damage in overdose; widely used in India |
| **Ibuprofen** | NSAID | Anti-inflammatory; COX inhibitor |
| **Morphine** | Opioid narcotic | From opium poppy; powerful; highly addictive; used for severe pain |
| **Codeine** | Opioid | Weaker than morphine; used in cough syrups |

**NSAIDs** (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs) = Aspirin, Ibuprofen, Naproxen — reduce fever, pain, inflammation.

### Antibiotics

| Antibiotic | Discovered By | Year | Target |
|-----------|-------------|------|--------|
| **Penicillin** | Alexander Fleming | 1928 (discovery), 1943 (clinical use) | Gram-positive bacteria; inhibits cell wall synthesis |
| **Streptomycin** | Selman Waksman | 1943 | First antibiotic for tuberculosis |
| **Tetracycline** | — | 1945 | Broad spectrum |
| **Chloramphenicol** | — | 1947 | Broad spectrum; causes aplastic anaemia |
| **Erythromycin** | — | 1952 | For penicillin-allergic patients |
| **Ciprofloxacin** | — | 1987 | Fluoroquinolone; UTI, respiratory infections |

**Antibiotics work only against bacteria — NOT viruses**. Colds, flu, COVID-19 are viral — antibiotics do nothing. Misuse leads to antibiotic resistance.

### Antiseptic vs Antibiotic
- **Antiseptic**: used externally on living tissue; kills surface microorganisms
- **Antibiotic**: taken internally; targets specific bacteria using selective toxicity (harms bacteria without harming human cells)

### Antimalarials

| Drug | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **Quinine** | From Cinchona bark; first antimalarial; bitter taste |
| **Chloroquine** | Synthetic; widely used; now resistant strains common |
| **Artemisinin** | From Artemisia plant; Nobel Prize 2015 (Tu Youyou) — discovered from ancient Chinese herb |
| **Primaquine** | Kills liver stage; used to prevent relapse |

### Anaesthetics

| Type | Examples | Use |
|------|---------|-----|
| **General anaesthesia** (whole body) | Chloroform (CHCl₃), Ether (C₂H₅OC₂H₅), Halothane, Thiopental | Surgery; patient unconscious |
| **Local anaesthesia** (specific area) | Lignocaine (Lidocaine), Procaine (Novocaine), Benzocaine | Dental surgery, minor procedures; patient conscious |

**Chloroform (CHCl₃)**: First general anaesthetic (used clinically from 1847); historically used during surgeries; toxic and now replaced by modern agents.

**Ether (Diethyl ether)**: First ether anaesthetic (1846); flammable; still used as reference.

### Sedatives and Tranquillisers

| Drug | Class | Use |
|------|-------|-----|
| **Diazepam (Valium)** | Benzodiazepine | Anxiety, muscle relaxant; addictive |
| **Phenobarbitone** | Barbiturate | Epilepsy; older class; addictive |
| **Meprobamate** | Minor tranquilliser | Anxiety |

### Antihistamines (Allergy Drugs)

Block histamine receptors; used for allergies, hay fever, motion sickness:
- **Diphenhydramine** (Benadryl) — first generation; causes drowsiness
- **Cetrizine, Loratadine** — second generation; non-drowsy

### Antacids

Neutralise excess stomach acid (HCl):

| Antacid | Formula | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| **Magnesium hydroxide** | Mg(OH)₂ | Milk of magnesia; also mild laxative |
| **Aluminium hydroxide** | Al(OH)₃ | Long-acting; causes constipation |
| **Sodium bicarbonate** | NaHCO₃ | Fast-acting; produces CO₂ (burping) |
| **Calcium carbonate** | CaCO₃ | Tums; also calcium supplement |
| **Omeprazole, Pantoprazole** | Proton pump inhibitors | Reduce acid production; not neutralise |

## Part 5 — Food Safety & Testing

### Common Adulterants and Tests

| Food | Common Adulterant | Test |
|------|-----------------|------|
| Milk | Water (check density), starch (iodine → blue), urea | Lactometer for density |
| **Edible oil** | Argemone oil (toxic) | Nitric acid test → orange/red colour |
| Spices (turmeric) | Lead chromate (yellow) | HCl test; dissolves and turns yellow |
| Honey | Sugar syrup | Fiehe's test (resorcinol in HCl → cherry red) |
| **Ghee** | Vanaspati | Baudouin test: furfural in HCl → red (detects sesame/vanaspati) |
| Green vegetables | Copper sulphate / malachite green | Wash with water test |
| Tea | Used tea leaves, colour | Smell, water solubility |

### Food Poisoning

| Pathogen | Food | Toxin/Condition |
|---------|------|----------------|
| **Salmonella typhi** | Contaminated water/food | Typhoid fever |
| **Clostridium botulinum** | Canned food | Botulinum toxin — most lethal biological toxin |
| **Staphylococcus aureus** | Dairy, meat | Enterotoxin — nausea, vomiting within 1–6 hours |
| **E. coli (pathogenic)** | Contaminated water/meat | Diarrhoea; haemolytic uraemic syndrome (O157:H7) |
| **Aflatoxin** | Mouldy groundnuts, maize | From *Aspergillus* fungi; highly carcinogenic (liver cancer) |

**Botulinum toxin** (produced by *Clostridium botulinum*) is the most toxic substance known — lethal dose ~1 ng/kg body weight. It blocks acetylcholine release at neuromuscular junctions → paralysis. In dilute cosmetic form: **Botox** (temporarily paralyses facial muscles to reduce wrinkles).

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Fats provide 9 kcal/g** — the most energy per gram among macronutrients. Carbohydrates and proteins both provide 4 kcal/g. This is why dietary fat is calorie-dense.

2. **Trans fats** are the most harmful dietary fat — they raise LDL (bad cholesterol) AND lower HDL (good cholesterol). FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) reduced permissible trans fat in food to 2% in 2022.

3. **Penicillin was discovered by Alexander Fleming in 1928** (accident with mould in his lab). He won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine 1945 (with Chain and Florey who developed it as a drug).

4. **Antibiotics are ineffective against viruses** — common cold, flu, COVID-19 are viral infections. Misuse of antibiotics causes **antibiotic resistance** — a major global health crisis.

5. **Aspirin (acetylsalicylic acid)** inhibits COX (cyclooxygenase) enzymes → reduces prostaglandin synthesis → reduces pain, fever, inflammation. At low doses (75-100 mg), it prevents platelet aggregation → used to prevent heart attacks (antiplatelet action).

6. **Paracetamol** (acetaminophen) is safe at normal doses but causes **liver damage in overdose** — this is why it says "do not exceed recommended dose." It does NOT have significant anti-inflammatory action (unlike aspirin/ibuprofen).

7. **Chloroform (CHCl₃)** was the first widely used general anaesthetic (from 1847). It is a trihalomethane (not the same as chlorine). Modern anaesthetics (halothane, isoflurane) have replaced it.

8. **Quinine** from Cinchona bark was the original antimalarial. **Artemisinin** from the Artemisia plant (artemisinin) won the Nobel Prize 2015 for Tu Youyou (China). Both are plant-derived antimalarials.

9. **Antiseptics vs Disinfectants**: Antiseptics are used on living tissue (wounds, skin). Disinfectants are too harsh for living tissue and are used on surfaces, instruments, water. Phenol/carbolic acid is used as both (diluted as antiseptic by Lister in 1867, concentrated as disinfectant for floors).

10. **Sodium benzoate** is the most widely used chemical preservative in soft drinks and acidic foods. It is antimicrobial in acidic conditions. Some concern about conversion to benzene in the presence of Vitamin C.

11. **Monosodium Glutamate (MSG)** is a flavour enhancer (umami taste) — the sodium salt of glutamic acid. It is widely used in Chinese cooking and processed foods. Some people report headaches ("Chinese restaurant syndrome"), though clinical evidence is controversial.

12. **Botulinum toxin** from *Clostridium botulinum* is the world's most toxic substance. It causes botulism (food poisoning from improperly canned food) — muscle paralysis. In dilute medical form, it is **Botox** — injected to temporarily paralyse facial muscles for cosmetic or medical use.

13. **Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)** is found ONLY in animal products. Pure vegetarians (vegans) MUST supplement B12 or risk deficiency — leading to pernicious anaemia and neurological damage. This is the only vitamin completely absent from all plant foods.

14. **Aflatoxin** (from *Aspergillus flavus* mould on groundnuts, maize, cottonseed) is a highly potent carcinogen — particularly for liver cancer. FSSAI has set limits for aflatoxin in food. Visibly mouldy peanuts or grain should not be consumed.

15. **Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)** can accumulate in body fat to toxic levels with excess supplementation. Water-soluble vitamins (B complex, C) are generally non-toxic in excess as the body excretes surplus in urine.

16. **Argemone oil in mustard oil** is a common and dangerous adulterant — it causes epidemic dropsy (oedema, paralysis, death). Tested with nitric acid (develops orange/red colour). Argemone mexicana is a weed whose seeds resemble mustard seeds.
