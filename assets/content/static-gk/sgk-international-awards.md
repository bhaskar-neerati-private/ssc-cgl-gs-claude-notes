# International Awards — Nobel, Booker, Oscar & Sports Honours

## The Telegram That Changed Everything

On October 10, 1913, Rabindranath Tagore was in a country house in England when a telegram arrived from Stockholm. The Swedish Academy had awarded him the Nobel Prize in Literature — the first Asian to receive any Nobel Prize. He was 52 years old. The telegram was so unexpected that Tagore initially doubted it was real. By the time he returned to Calcutta, enormous crowds lined the streets. The British Viceroy formally congratulated him; the King of Sweden invited him to Stockholm. Tagore, a Bengali poet barely known outside India, had become the most celebrated Indian in the world. When SSC asks about Nobel Prize winners from India, it is asking about a chain of recognition that began with this one telegram in 1913.

## Nobel Prize — Key Facts

- **Established**: 1901 (Alfred Nobel's will, 1895; first awarded 1901)
- **Fields**: Physics, Chemistry, Medicine/Physiology, Literature, Peace, Economic Sciences (Economics added 1968)
- **No Nobel for Mathematics**: Fields Medal is the mathematics equivalent
- **Peace Nobel**: Given by Norwegian Nobel Committee (others by Swedish)
- **Economics Nobel**: Not in Nobel's original will; added by Sweden's Riksbank in 1968
- **Prize amount**: ~10 million Swedish kronor (each); gold medal + diploma

### Most Nobel Prizes by Country (context)
USA leads overall; Sweden, UK, Germany follow. India ranks low in total but Tagore remains Asia's first.

## Oscar (Academy Awards) — India Connections

- **Bhanu Athaiya**: First Indian to win Oscar; Best Costume Design for Gandhi (1983)
- **A. R. Rahman**: Two Oscars for Slumdog Millionaire (2009) — Best Original Score + Best Original Song (Jai Ho)
- **Resul Pookutty**: Oscar for Best Sound Mixing — Slumdog Millionaire (2009)
- **Gulzar**: Oscar for Best Original Song — Jai Ho (shared with Rahman)
- **RRR (Naatu Naatu)**: Oscar for Best Original Song (2023) — composed by M. M. Keeravani; first Indian film song to win Oscar
- **The Elephant Whisperers**: Oscar for Best Documentary Short (2023) — directed by Kartiki Gonsalves; Netflix India production

### India's Official Entry for Oscars
- India submits one film annually for Best International Feature Film consideration
- Not always nominated/won — submission ≠ nomination

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Rabindranath Tagore** = first Asian Nobel laureate (Literature, 1913); **C. V. Raman** = first Indian citizen to win Nobel in Sciences (Physics, 1930).
2. **Nobel Economics** = NOT in Alfred Nobel's original will; added in **1968** by Sweden's Riksbank — it's formally the "Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel."
3. **No Nobel Prize for Mathematics** — the equivalent is the **Fields Medal** (under 40) or **Abel Prize**.
4. **Mother Teresa** was Albanian by nationality, not Indian — but she did much of her work in India and is considered Indian by association. She received Bharat Ratna (1980) too.
5. **Kailash Satyarthi** (2014 Peace Nobel) shared the prize with **Malala Yousafzai** — only time a Pakistani and Indian shared it.
6. **Amartya Sen** = Economics Nobel 1998 = about poverty and famines (NOT about globalisation or trade).
7. **Arundhati Roy** = **Booker Prize 1997** for "The God of Small Things" — her only novel until "The Ministry of Utmost Happiness" (2017).
8. **Midnight's Children** (Salman Rushdie) = Booker 1981 AND "Booker of Bookers" — both facts tested.
9. **Bhanu Athaiya** = first Indian to win Oscar (Best Costume Design, Gandhi, 1983).
10. **A. R. Rahman** won TWO Oscars for Slumdog Millionaire (2009) — Best Score + Best Song.
11. **Naatu Naatu (RRR)** won Oscar 2023 = first Indian SONG to win Oscar = Best Original Song.
12. **Ramon Magsaysay Award** = "Asian Nobel Prize"; based in Manila, Philippines.
13. **Pulitzer Prize** = USA; journalism + literature; awarded by Columbia University.
14. **Abel Prize** (2002) and **Fields Medal** = both for mathematics; Fields Medal is for under-40 mathematicians only.
15. **Turing Award** = "Nobel of Computing" = given by ACM (Association for Computing Machinery).
16. **Nobel Peace Prize** is given by the **Norwegian Nobel Committee** (Oslo), while all other Nobels are awarded by Swedish institutions in Stockholm.
