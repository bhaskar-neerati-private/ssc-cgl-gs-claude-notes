# Defence — Missiles, Organisations, Exercises & Operations

## The Missile That Changed India's Position

On May 11, 1998 — code name "Operation Shakti" — India detonated five nuclear devices at the Pokhran Test Range in Rajasthan. The first test was called "Smiling Buddha" in 1974; this was "Shakti." Within days, the United States, Japan, and European nations imposed economic sanctions. But something else happened too: India was suddenly a de facto nuclear state. The architects of the tests — including DRDO scientists, the Army, and A. P. J. Abdul Kalam who had built the missiles — had done in 24 hours what diplomacy could not have done in decades. May 11 became National Technology Day. That is the context in which SSC asks about DRDO, India's missiles, and defence exercises. These are not just administrative facts — they represent one of India's most significant strategic transformations.

## India's Missile Programme (IGMDP & Beyond)

The **Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP)** was launched in 1983 under Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam. It produced five missiles remembered by the mnemonic **"PANT + NB"**:

| Missile | Type | Range/Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Prithvi** | Surface-to-surface; ballistic | 150–750 km; first IGMDP missile inducted; three variants (Prithvi-I/II/III) |
| **Agni** | Long-range; ballistic | Agni-I (700 km) to Agni-V (5000+ km intercontinental); nuclear capable |
| **Nag** | Anti-tank; fire-and-forget | Helicopter-launched variant = Helina; tank destroyer |
| **Trishul** | Surface-to-air; short range | Anti-aircraft; low-level; decommissioned |
| **Akash** | Surface-to-air; medium range | 25–30 km; India's own SAM system; export to Philippines |
| **Brahmos** | Cruise missile (joint with Russia) | Russia (NPO Mashinostroyeniya) + India (DRDO); supersonic; Mach 2.8; 290 km+ range; world's fastest cruise missile |
| **Nirbhay** | Cruise missile (subsonic) | Long-range; 1,000 km; stealth; for deep strikes |
| **Astra** | Air-to-air; beyond visual range | DRDO; for Tejas, Sukhoi; 100+ km range |

## Armed Forces

### Indian Army
- **Headquarters**: New Delhi
- **Chief of Army Staff (COAS)**: 4-star general
- **Commands**: 7 commands (Northern, Southern, Eastern, Western, Central, Southwestern, South Western)
- **Largest regiment**: Rajput Regiment (one of oldest)

### Indian Navy
- **Headquarters**: New Delhi
- **Chief of Naval Staff (CNS)**
- **Commands**: Western (Mumbai), Eastern (Visakhapatnam), Southern (Kochi)
- **INS Vikrant**: India's first indigenous aircraft carrier; commissioned 2022
- **INS Vikramaditya**: Refitted Russian carrier; commissioned 2013
- **INS Arihant**: India's first nuclear submarine (SSBN); commissioned 2016

### Indian Air Force
- **Headquarters**: New Delhi
- **Chief of Air Staff (CAS)**
- **Raised**: October 8, 1932 (Royal Indian Air Force; "Royal" dropped 1950)
- **Tejas**: Indigenous light combat aircraft; IOC in 2016, FOC 2023
- **Rafale**: French multi-role jet; India inducted 2020 (36 aircraft)

### Chief of Defence Staff (CDS)
- Created in **2019** following Kargil Review
- First CDS: **General Bipin Rawat** (2020–2021; died in helicopter crash December 2021)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **IGMDP** missiles = Prithvi, Agni, Nag, Trishul, Akash — mnemonic **PANT + A** (or PANTA); launched **1983** under A. P. J. Abdul Kalam.
2. **BrahMos** = joint venture between **India and Russia** (not India alone); fastest cruise missile in world; Mach 2.8.
3. **Agni-V** = ICBM class; **5,000–8,000 km** range; capable of reaching most of China and Europe.
4. **Mission Shakti** (March 27, 2019) = India destroyed its satellite; India became **4th country** to demonstrate A-SAT capability (after USA, Russia, China).
5. **National Technology Day** = May 11 = Pokhran II nuclear tests (1998) under **Operation Shakti**.
6. **Malabar** exercise = India + USA + Japan (Australia joined from 2020) = Navy; one of India's most strategically significant exercises.
7. **INS Vikrant** = India's first **indigenous aircraft carrier** (commissioned September 2022); NOT INS Vikramaditya (which was refitted from Russia).
8. **INS Arihant** = India's first **nuclear-powered ballistic missile submarine** (SSBN); commissioned 2016.
9. **IAF raised**: **October 8, 1932** — this date is Indian Air Force Day.
10. **Chief of Defence Staff (CDS)** created **2019**; first CDS = **General Bipin Rawat** (died December 2021).
11. **Operation Polo** = Hyderabad integration 1948; **Operation Vijay** = BOTH Goa liberation (1961) AND Kargil victory (1999) — two different operations with the same name.
12. **Vijay Diwas** = December 16 = 1971 war victory; **Kargil Vijay Diwas** = July 26 — two different days.
13. **Tejas** = Light Combat Aircraft; **HAL** made; FOC (Final Operational Clearance) in 2023; **Rafale** is FRENCH (not domestic).
14. **DRDO** established **1958**; HQ = **New Delhi** (not Bengaluru — ISRO HQ is Bengaluru).
15. **NDA** = National Defence Academy = **Khadakwasla, Pune** (not Dehradun — IMA is Dehradun).
16. **Yudh Abhyas** = India-**USA** Army exercise; **Indra** = India-**Russia** tri-service exercise; **Shakti** = India-**France** army exercise.
