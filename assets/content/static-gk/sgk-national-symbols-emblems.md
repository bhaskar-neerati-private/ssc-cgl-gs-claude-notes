# National Symbols, Emblems & State Symbols of India

## The Lion That Wasn't Always the Lion

When India needed a national emblem in 1950, the choice was not obvious. The Ashoka Chakra had already been chosen for the flag, but India needed a full state seal — an image that would appear on official documents, currency, and correspondence. The decision was made to adopt the Lion Capital of Ashoka, discovered at Sarnath in 1905 and kept in the Sarnath Museum. The original capital has four lions standing back-to-back atop a drum; the state emblem shows three lions visible from the front, with the Ashoka Chakra in the centre of the abacus, a bull on the right, a horse on the left, and the motto "Satyameva Jayate" (Truth Alone Triumphs) from the Mundaka Upanishad in Devanagari below. Every element was deliberate — an ancient emperor's pillar became a modern republic's face. This is what national symbols do: they tell the world who a country believes itself to be.

| **National Flag** | Tiranga (Tricolour) | Adopted July 22, 1947; saffron (top) + white (middle) + dark green (bottom); Ashoka Chakra (navy blue; 24 spokes) in centre of white band; ratio 3:2; designed by Pingali Venkaiah; Khadi only |

## About the Ashoka Chakra

- 24 spokes — represents 24 hours of the day; perpetual motion; progress
- Navy blue colour in national flag
- Derived from the Lion Capital of Ashoka (Sarnath, 250 BCE)
- Appears in centre of the white band of the national flag
- Also present in the national emblem (on the abacus between lions)
- Dhamma Chakra — wheel of righteousness in Buddhism

| Feature | Detail |

## Satyameva Jayate

- Sanskrit phrase; meaning: "Truth Alone Triumphs"
- Source: **Mundaka Upanishad** (one of the 108 Upanishads)
- Part of the national emblem
- Written in **Devanagari script** below the emblem
- Adopted with the emblem on January 26, 1950

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **National Flag adopted July 22, 1947** — NOT August 15 (Independence Day) and NOT January 26 (Republic Day).
2. **Pingali Venkaiah** designed the national flag; from **Andhra Pradesh**; also designed the flag predecessor for INC.
3. **Ashoka Chakra has 24 spokes** (not 12, not 32); navy blue colour (not dark blue or black).
4. **"Satyameva Jayate"** = from **Mundaka Upanishad** (NOT Rigveda or Manusmriti).
5. **National Emblem adopted January 26, 1950** (Republic Day); national anthem adopted January 24, 1950.
6. **Bengal Tiger replaced the lion** as national animal in **1973** (Project Tiger year) — the lion was national animal before 1973.
7. **National Aquatic Animal** = Ganges River Dolphin (2009); NOT the whale shark or gharial.
8. **National Heritage Animal** = Elephant (2010); this is IN ADDITION TO national animal (Bengal Tiger) — both exist.
9. **Vande Mataram** source = novel **Anand Math** (Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay, 1882); first publicly sung at **1896 INC session**.
10. **Jana Gana Mana** first sung = **December 27, 1911** (Calcutta INC session); composed by **Rabindranath Tagore**.
11. **₹ symbol** designed by **D. Udaya Kumar** (NOT the RBI or Finance Ministry officials); adopted **July 15, 2010**.
12. **National River = Ganga**; declared **November 4, 2008** (NOT 1950 or some other year).
13. **King Cobra** = national reptile (2010); longest venomous snake in world; different from the common cobra.
14. **National Calendar = Saka Calendar**; Chaitra = first month (starts ~March 22); adopted 1957 alongside Gregorian.
15. **Only Khadi** (hand-spun/hand-woven) is legally permitted for the national flag material — BIS standard.
16. **Gujarat's state animal = Asiatic Lion** (not tiger) — Gir Forest is the only wild habitat; SSC asks this because India's national animal is tiger but Gujarat has lion.
