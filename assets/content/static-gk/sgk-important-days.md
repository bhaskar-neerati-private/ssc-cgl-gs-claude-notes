# National & International Days, Weeks & Themes

## The Day That Almost Wasn't

On June 21, 2015, an estimated 192 countries observed the first International Day of Yoga simultaneously — the largest coordinated mass yoga event in history. At Delhi's Rajpath, 35,985 people performed yoga together, setting a Guinness World Record. But barely six months earlier, the idea had not existed. PM Narendra Modi had proposed it at the UN General Assembly only in September 2014. The UN adopted the resolution co-sponsored by 177 nations in a matter of weeks — a record for the fastest adoption of a resolution with that many co-sponsors. The fact that June 21 (summer solstice) was chosen was intentional — the longest day of the year, when the sun is at maximum strength, symbolically connected to yoga's solar salutation practice. This is the story behind one International Day; every day on this list has a story. SSC tests the date, the theme-setter (usually UN or WHO), and the specific day assigned.

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **National Science Day** = February 28 (Raman Effect announced 1928); NOT C. V. Raman's birthday.
2. **Children's Day** (India) = November 14 (Nehru's birthday); **Universal Children's Day** (UN) = November 20 — different dates.
3. **World Environment Day** = June 5; **World Earth Day** = April 22 — frequently confused.
4. **World Health Day** = April 7 (WHO founded 1948 on April 7).
5. **World Population Day** = July 11 ("Day of Five Billion" 1987).
6. **International Women's Day** = March 8; **National Girl Child Day** = January 24 — both are different.
7. **Teachers' Day** (India) = September 5; **World Teachers' Day** (UNESCO) = October 5 — different months.
8. **National Unity Day** = October 31 = Sardar Patel's birthday — NOT Indira Gandhi's assassination date.
9. **National Technology Day** = May 11 = Pokhran II nuclear tests (1998), NOT invention of any technology.
10. **International Day of Yoga** = June 21 (summer solstice); proposed by India at UNGA September 2014; first observed 2015.
11. **World Blood Donor Day** = June 14 = Karl Landsteiner's birthday (discovered ABO blood groups).
12. **World Food Day** = October 16 = FAO founded (1945); NOT World Hunger Day (May 28).
13. **Human Rights Day** = December 10 = UDHR adopted (1948) — Universal Declaration of Human Rights.
14. **International Mother Language Day** = February 21 = Bangladesh Language Movement (1952); UNESCO since 1999.
15. **Kargil Vijay Diwas** = July 26; **Vijay Diwas** (1971 war) = December 16 — two different Vijay Diwases.
16. **Hindi Diwas** (National) = September 14; **World Hindi Day** = January 10 — same language, different dates.
