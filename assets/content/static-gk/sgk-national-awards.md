# National Awards — Bharat Ratna, Padma, Gallantry & Literary

## The Award That Arrived by Post

In 1954, when the Government of India established the Bharat Ratna, the first three recipients were C. Rajagopalachari (India's last Governor-General), S. Radhakrishnan (philosopher and future President), and C. V. Raman (Nobel Prize-winning physicist). The government sent the awards by registered post. Raman, receiving his medal in Bangalore, reportedly held the small circular gold medallion up to the light and said nothing for a long moment. There was no ceremony that year — the government was still figuring out how to celebrate itself. Over seven decades later, the Bharat Ratna remains the highest honour the Republic of India can bestow on any person, and SSC has asked about it in virtually every exam cycle since 2010.

## Bharat Ratna — Key Facts

- **Established**: 1954
- **Form**: Peepal-leaf shaped medallion (not circular — changed from circular medallion to peepal leaf in 1955)
- **Criteria**: Exceptional service to nation; originally only arts/literature/science — later amended to include any field (1955 amendment extended to public service)
- **Announced by**: President of India (recommended by PM)
- **Maximum per year**: Up to 3 per year (no formal cap, but convention)
- **Can be awarded posthumously**: Yes (since 1966; first posthumous: Lal Bahadur Shastri, 1966)

## Padma Awards

- **Announced**: Republic Day eve (January 25)
- **Presented by**: President of India (March/April ceremony)
- **No caste, occupation, or position bar** — any citizen (including NRIs and foreigners)
- **Three tiers**: Padma Vibhushan > Padma Bhushan > Padma Shri
- Discontinued 1977–1980 and 1992–1995 (controversies)

## Gallantry Awards — Military

### Wartime (in presence of enemy)

| Award | Rank | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Param Vir Chakra (PVC)** | Highest | Most conspicuous bravery in face of enemy; India's highest military honour |
| **Maha Vir Chakra (MVC)** | 2nd | Acts of conspicuous gallantry in presence of enemy |
| **Vir Chakra (VC)** | 3rd | Acts of gallantry in presence of enemy |

### Peacetime

| Award | Rank | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Ashoka Chakra (AC)** | Highest peacetime | Most conspicuous bravery or self-sacrifice in peacetime; equivalent of PVC for peacetime |
| **Kirti Chakra (KC)** | 2nd | Conspicuous gallantry in peacetime |
| **Shaurya Chakra (SC)** | 3rd | Gallantry in peacetime |

> **Key fact**: Param Vir Chakra = highest wartime military honour; Ashoka Chakra = highest peacetime military honour.

### Param Vir Chakra Facts
- 21 PVCs awarded to date (majority posthumously)
- First PVC: Major Somnath Sharma (1947, Kashmir operations; posthumous)
- Only living PVC holders: very few (most are posthumous)
- Designed by: Savitri Khanolkar (Swiss-born wife of Indian army officer)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Bharat Ratna** established **1954**; changed from circular to **peepal-leaf shape in 1955**.
2. **First posthumous Bharat Ratna**: Lal Bahadur Shastri (**1966**) — not Ambedkar or Vinoba Bhave.
3. **First non-Indian Bharat Ratna**: Mother Teresa (1980); **Nelson Mandela** also non-Indian recipient (1990).
4. **Sachin Tendulkar** = youngest Bharat Ratna (age 40, 2014) + only sportsperson to receive it while alive at that age.
5. **M. S. Subbulakshmi** = first musician to receive Bharat Ratna (1998); first woman to receive Sangeet Natak Akademi's highest award.
6. **Param Vir Chakra** = highest WARTIME military honour; **Ashoka Chakra** = highest PEACETIME military honour.
7. **First Param Vir Chakra**: Major Somnath Sharma (posthumous, 1947 Kashmir operations).
8. **Dada Saheb Phalke Award**: established **1969**; for **lifetime achievement** in cinema; NOT the same as National Film Award.
9. **National Film Award for Best Film** = **Swarna Kamal** (Golden Lotus) — not Golden Globe or National Award simply.
10. **Dronacharya Award** = for **coaches** (not athletes); Arjuna = for athletes.
11. **Maulana Abul Kalam Azad (MAKA) Trophy** = **oldest sports award** in India (for universities).
12. **Padma awards announced on Republic Day eve (January 25)** — given by President in ceremony weeks later.
13. **A. P. J. Abdul Kalam** received Bharat Ratna in **1997** (as a scientist, BEFORE he became President in 2002).
14. **Khel Ratna renamed** from Rajiv Gandhi to Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna in **2021**.
15. **M. G. Ramachandran** = first Chief Minister to receive Bharat Ratna (1988); actor + politician.
16. **2024 Bharat Ratna**: 5 recipients — Karpoori Thakur, L. K. Advani, M. S. Swaminathan, Chaudhary Charan Singh, P. V. Narasimha Rao.
