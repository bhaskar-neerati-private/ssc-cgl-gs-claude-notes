# Famous Books & Authors — Leaders, Scientists & Literary Works

## The Book That Was Written in Prison

In 1930, Jawaharlal Nehru was arrested for civil disobedience and put in Naini Prison, Allahabad. He had nothing to do except think and write. Over the next years, he wrote hundreds of letters to his daughter Indira — a girl then in her early teens — describing the entire history of the world from ancient civilisations to the rise of nationalism. When compiled, those letters became "Glimpses of World History" — a book that Indira Gandhi later said shaped her entire understanding of politics and history. Nehru wrote two of India's most important political books from prison cells. When SSC asks you who wrote "The Discovery of India," it is asking about a man who had enough time to think in Ahmednagar Fort Prison. Know your books — they are among the most predictably tested facts in Static GK.

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **"My Experiments with Truth"** = Gandhi's autobiography; originally written in **Gujarati** (Satya ke Prayog), translated to English.
2. **"Discovery of India"** = Nehru; written in **Ahmednagar Fort** (1944). "Glimpses of World History" = written in **Naini Prison** earlier.
3. **"Hind Swaraj"** = Gandhi, **1909** — written on a ship returning from England; critique of modernity.
4. **"Anand Math"** = Bankim Chandra Chattopadhyay — the novel that contains **Vande Mataram** (India's national song).
5. **"Wings of Fire"** = A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's autobiography — NOT "India 2020" (that is a future vision book, not autobiography).
6. **"The God of Small Things"** = Arundhati Roy = Booker **1997**; NOT 1981 (that's Rushdie's Midnight's Children).
7. **"The White Tiger"** = Aravind Adiga = Booker **2008** — set in contemporary India; SSC confuses with "Slumdog Millionaire" (film, same era).
8. **"Poverty and Un-British Rule in India"** = Dadabhai Naoroji = **first articulation of drain of wealth theory**.
9. **"India Wins Freedom"** = Maulana Abul Kalam Azad — not Nehru or Gandhi; about Partition.
10. **"Godan"** = Munshi Premchand = Hindi novel about farmer life = his most famous work.
11. **"Argumentative Indian"** = Amartya Sen — about India's intellectual tradition, NOT economics (his economics books are "Development as Freedom" and "Inequality Reexamined").
12. **"Das Kapital"** = Karl Marx; "The Communist Manifesto" = Marx AND Engels jointly.
13. **"A Passage to India"** = E. M. Forster = BRITISH author writing about British India — not an Indian author's book.
14. **"Ain-i-Akbari"** and **"Akbarnama"** = both by **Abul Fazl** (Akbar's court historian). Baburnama = Babur's own memoir.
15. **"Tahqiq-i-Hind" / "Kitab-ul-Hind"** = **Al-Biruni** = 11th century Arab scholar who came with Mahmud of Ghazni.
16. **Playing It My Way** = Sachin Tendulkar's autobiography. **Race of My Life** = Milkha Singh. SSC tests sportsperson-autobiography pairs frequently.
