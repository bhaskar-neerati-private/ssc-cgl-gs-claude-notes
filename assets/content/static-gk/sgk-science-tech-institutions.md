# Science & Technology — ISRO, Institutions, Labs & Missions

## The Night the Moon Turned Indian

On August 23, 2023 — at 6:04 PM IST — a spacecraft called Vikram touched down in the south polar region of the Moon. India became the fourth country in history to land on the Moon, and the first to land at the lunar south pole. Scientists at ISRO's Mission Control in Bengaluru erupted. PM Modi, watching from Johannesburg where he was attending a BRICS summit, waved an Indian flag. But what most people don't know is that this was a redemption mission — Chandrayaan-2's Vikram lander had crashed in 2019, just 2.1 km from the surface. For four years, ISRO redesigned the lander's legs, reprogrammed the descent algorithm, and tried again. The date of landing — August 23 — is now **National Space Day** in India. This is the spirit behind every institution and mission on this list. They represent India's cumulative scientific ambition, built over decades.

## ISRO — Indian Space Research Organisation

| Detail | Fact |
|---|---|
| **Founded** | 1969 |
| **Headquarters** | Bengaluru (Bangalore), Karnataka |
| **Predecessor** | INCOSPAR (Indian National Committee for Space Research, 1962) |
| **Founded by** | Vikram Sarabhai ("Father of Indian Space Programme") |
| **Current launch site** | Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC), Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh |
| **First launch site** | Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS), Kerala (1963) |
| **First Indian satellite** | Aryabhata (1975); launched by Soviet Union |
| **First ISRO launch vehicle** | SLV-3 (1980); placed Rohini satellite in orbit |

### Launch Vehicles

| Vehicle | Full Name | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SLV** | Satellite Launch Vehicle | Retired | SLV-3 placed Rohini in orbit (1980); A. P. J. Abdul Kalam project director |
| **ASLV** | Augmented Satellite Launch Vehicle | Retired | 4 attempts; mostly failed |
| **PSLV** | Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle | Operational | Workhorse; 50+ successful launches; used for Chandrayaan-1, MOM |
| **GSLV** | Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle | Operational | Cryogenic upper stage; GSLV Mk II, Mk III |
| **LVM3 (GSLV Mk III)** | Launch Vehicle Mark 3 | Operational | India's heaviest rocket; used for Chandrayaan-3, OneWeb launches |
| **SSLV** | Small Satellite Launch Vehicle | New | For small satellites; rapid deployment |
| **Gaganyaan** | Crewed vehicle | Under development | India's first human spaceflight programme |

## Major ISRO Missions

| Mission | Year | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Aryabhata** | 1975 | First Indian satellite; built by ISRO; launched by Soviet Union |
| **Rohini (RS-1)** | 1980 | First satellite launched by Indian rocket (SLV-3) |
| **INSAT series** | 1982– | Indian National Satellite System; telecom + weather |
| **IRS series** | 1988– | Indian Remote Sensing satellites; earth observation |
| **Chandrayaan-1** | 2008 | First lunar mission; discovered water molecules on Moon; PSLV-C11 |
| **Chandrayaan-2** | 2019 | Orbiter (operational) + Vikram lander (crashed) + Pragyan rover |
| **Mangalyaan (MOM)** | 2013–2022 | Mars Orbiter Mission; first Asian nation to reach Mars; first attempt succeeded; PSLV-C25 |
| **Chandrayaan-3** | 2023 | Vikram lander + Pragyan rover; first soft landing at south pole; August 23 = National Space Day |
| **Aditya-L1** | 2023 | India's first solar mission; studies Sun from Lagrange Point 1 |
| **ASTROSAT** | 2015 | India's first dedicated astronomy satellite; multi-wavelength |
| **NavIC (IRNSS)** | 2013–2016 | Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System; 7-satellite constellation; India's own GPS |
| **Gaganyaan** | 2025+ | Crewed mission; 3 astronauts (Gagannauts); LVM3 rocket |
| **Shukrayaan** | Proposed | Venus mission under development |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **ISRO founded 1969**; predecessor = INCOSPAR (1962); **Vikram Sarabhai** = Father of Indian Space Programme.
2. **Aryabhata (1975)** = first Indian satellite; but **Rohini** = first satellite launched by **Indian rocket** (SLV-3, 1980).
3. **Chandrayaan-1** = 2008; **discovered water on Moon**; orbiter + impact probe; PSLV-C11.
4. **MOM / Mangalyaan** = first Asian country to reach Mars; first attempt succeeded (no country had succeeded on first attempt); launched 2013.
5. **Chandrayaan-3**: Vikram lander + Pragyan rover; landed **August 23, 2023**; South Pole; **India = 4th country** to soft-land on Moon; **first to land at south pole**.
6. **Aditya-L1** = India's first **solar mission**; studies Sun from **L1 Lagrange point** (not the Sun's surface).
7. **PSLV** = India's workhorse launch vehicle; **GSLV Mk III / LVM3** = heaviest rocket; used for Chandrayaan-3.
8. **DRDO HQ = New Delhi**; **ISRO HQ = Bengaluru** — SSC tests this reversal trick.
9. **BARC** = Bhabha Atomic Research Centre = **Trombay, Mumbai** (not Bengaluru or New Delhi); under DAE.
10. **Homi J. Bhabha** = Father of Indian Nuclear Programme; **BARC** was originally AEET (Atomic Energy Establishment Trombay), renamed after his death.
11. **IIT Kharagpur** = first IIT (1951); **IIM Calcutta and IIM Ahmedabad** = both first IIMs (1961 simultaneously).
12. **AIIMS New Delhi** = first AIIMS (**1956**); now 23 AIIMS across India.
13. **NavIC** = India's own navigation system; 7 satellites; **regional** (not global like GPS) — covers India + 1,500 km neighbourhood.
14. **First rocket launch in India** = **1963** at Thumba, Kerala; a US-supplied Nike-Apache rocket; sounding rocket (not orbital).
15. **Kudankulam** = India-Russia nuclear plant (Tamil Nadu); **Tarapur** = India's oldest nuclear plant (Maharashtra; US-designed).
16. **National Space Day** = **August 23** (Chandrayaan-3 landing date, 2023); NOT any other date.
