# Sports — Trophies, Tournaments, Venues & Governing Bodies

## The Match That Made a Nation Cry

On June 25, 1983, India — 66-to-1 underdogs — walked onto the Lord's Cricket Ground in London for the Prudential World Cup Final against the mighty West Indies, who had won the previous two World Cups. No one gave India a chance. West Indies had made 183. India made 183 chasing… no, they were bowled out for 183 in the batting innings. Then their bowlers did the unthinkable — they dismissed West Indies for just 140. India had won. In Bombay, Delhi, Madras, people who had gathered around the one television set in the neighbourhood reportedly wept. Kapil Dev lifted the trophy at Lord's, and within a decade India had become the commercial centre of world cricket. That tournament win didn't just change cricket; it changed the economics of an entire sport. Know your trophies — SSC asks about them in every exam, and the story behind each one is why it matters.

| Trophy/Tournament | Type | Key Details |
|---|---|---|
| **ICC Cricket World Cup** | ODI; Men's | Every 4 years; India won: 1983 (Kapil Dev), 2011 (M. S. Dhoni) |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **India's Cricket World Cup wins**: 1983 (Kapil Dev; Prudential Cup) and 2011 (M. S. Dhoni; ICC CWC); T20 World Cup 2007 (inaugural) and 2024.
2. **Ranji Trophy** = oldest domestic cricket tournament (1934); named after **Ranjitsinhji** (K. S. Ranjitsinhji).
3. **Durand Cup** = India's **oldest football tournament** (1888); not Santosh Trophy (which is national championship).
4. **Murugappa Gold Cup** = **oldest hockey tournament in Asia** (1895, Chennai); not to be confused with the oldest football trophy.
5. **National Sports Day** = **August 29** = Major Dhyan Chand's birthday.
6. **Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna** = renamed from Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna in **2021**.
7. **All England** badminton = world's oldest badminton tournament; India's **Prakash Padukone** won in 1980, **Gopichand** in 2001.
8. **P. V. Sindhu** = **first Indian woman** to win BWF World Championship (2019, Basel).
9. **Viswanathan Anand** = India's first chess World Champion; won **5 times** (2000, 2007, 2008, 2010, 2012).
10. **First Asian Games** = 1951, **New Delhi** — India hosted the inaugural Asian Games.
11. **India's best Asian Games**: 2023 **Hangzhou** — 107 medals including 28 gold.
12. **Mary Kom** = **6-time** World Amateur Boxing Champion (not 5 or 7).
13. **Leander Paes** = 18 Grand Slam doubles titles; **NOT Grand Slam singles** titles.
14. **Olympic rings meaning**: 5 rings = 5 **continents** (not 5 founding nations or 5 sports).
15. **India's first Olympic gold overall** = Men's Hockey 1928 (Amsterdam) — **team sport**; first **individual** gold = Abhinav Bindra 2008.
16. **ICC** (Cricket) = Dubai, UAE; **FIH** (Hockey) = Lausanne; **FIFA** (Football) = Zurich; **BWF** (Badminton) = Kuala Lumpur.
