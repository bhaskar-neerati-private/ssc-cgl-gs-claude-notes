# Human Circulatory System — Heart, Blood, Blood Groups & Lymphatic System

## The River That Never Stops

In 1628, English physician William Harvey published a small but explosive book: *Exercitatio Anatomica de Motu Cordis et Sanguinis* — "An Anatomical Study of the Motion of the Heart and of the Blood." It proved, against centuries of wrong assumptions, that blood circulates in a single closed loop driven by the heart. He was mocked. He was right.

The circulatory system is a closed highway network: 100,000 km of blood vessels through which ~5 litres of blood make a complete circuit every minute. Every cell in the body — from neurons in the brain to osteocytes in bone — depends on this continuous delivery of oxygen and removal of waste.

---

## Types of Circulatory Systems

| Type | Description | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Open** | Blood not always in vessels; bathes organs directly | Arthropods, most molluscs |
| **Closed** | Blood always confined to vessels; exchanges via capillaries | Annelids, vertebrates (including humans) |

**Double circulation in humans:** Blood passes through the heart **twice** in one complete circuit:
- **Pulmonary circulation:** Right heart → Lungs (oxygenation) → Left heart
- **Systemic circulation:** Left heart → Body tissues (O₂ delivery) → Right heart

**Discovery:** William Harvey, 1628

## The Heart

**Location:** Thoracic cavity, between the two lungs, slightly left of midline (in the mediastinum)
**Size/Weight:** Roughly the size of a closed fist; ~300 g
**Covering:** Double-walled sac — **Pericardium**
- Outer fibrous pericardium + inner visceral pericardium (epicardium)
- **Pericardial fluid** between layers reduces friction during heartbeat

### Four Chambers

| Chamber | Receives blood from | Sends blood to |
|---|---|---|
| **Right Atrium (RA)** | Body (via SVC + IVC) — deoxygenated | Right Ventricle |
| **Right Ventricle (RV)** | Right Atrium | Lungs (via pulmonary artery) |
| **Left Atrium (LA)** | Lungs (via pulmonary veins) — oxygenated | Left Ventricle |
| **Left Ventricle (LV)** | Left Atrium | Body (via Aorta) |

**Left ventricle has the thickest wall** — it must pump blood against the highest pressure (to the whole body).

### Heart Valves (Prevent backflow)

| Valve | Location | Cusps |
|---|---|---|
| **Tricuspid** | Between RA and RV | 3 |
| **Mitral (Bicuspid)** | Between LA and LV | 2 |
| **Pulmonary semilunar** | Between RV and Pulmonary artery | 3 |
| **Aortic semilunar** | Between LV and Aorta | 3 |

**Mnemonic:** **T**ry **B**efore **P**umping **A**orta → Tricuspid, Bicuspid, Pulmonary, Aortic

**Heart sounds "Lub-Dub":**
- **"Lub" (S1):** Closure of AV valves (Tricuspid + Mitral) at start of ventricular contraction
- **"Dub" (S2):** Closure of semilunar valves (Aortic + Pulmonary) at end of ventricular contraction

---

## Major Blood Vessels

| Vessel | Carries | From → To |
|---|---|---|
| Superior Vena Cava (SVC) | Deoxygenated blood | Upper body → RA |
| Inferior Vena Cava (IVC) | Deoxygenated blood | Lower body → RA |
| **Pulmonary artery** | **Deoxygenated blood** | RV → Lungs *(only artery carrying deoxygenated blood)* |
| **Pulmonary veins (×4)** | **Oxygenated blood** | Lungs → LA *(only veins carrying oxygenated blood)* |
| Aorta (largest artery) | Oxygenated blood | LV → Body |
| Coronary arteries | Oxygenated blood | Aorta → Heart muscle |

**Coronary arteries** supply the heart muscle itself. Blockage → **Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack)**.

## Cardiac Cycle and Rhythm

**Normal heart rate:** ~72 beats/minute (adult); ~120 bpm (newborn)
**Cardiac output:** Heart rate × Stroke volume = 72 × 70 mL = ~5 litres/minute at rest

### Conducting System (Natural Pacemaker)
| Structure | Location | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **SA Node (Sinoatrial node)** | RA wall | **"Pacemaker"** — initiates electrical impulse; sets rhythm (60–100 bpm) |
| **AV Node (Atrioventricular node)** | Floor of RA, near tricuspid valve | Delays impulse slightly (allows ventricles to fill) |
| **Bundle of His** | Interventricular septum | Conducts impulse toward ventricles |
| **Purkinje fibres** | Ventricular walls | Distributes impulse for coordinated ventricular contraction |

**ECG (Electrocardiogram):** Records electrical activity of the heart — detects arrhythmias, heart attacks.

**Artificial pacemaker:** Electronic device implanted when SA node fails.

## Blood Pressure

| State | Value (mmHg) |
|---|---|
| Normal | **120/80** (systolic/diastolic) |
| Hypertension | ≥140/90 |
| Hypotension | ≤90/60 |

- **Systolic pressure:** Pressure during ventricular contraction (heart pumping)
- **Diastolic pressure:** Pressure during ventricular relaxation (heart at rest)
- **Measured by:** Sphygmomanometer
- **Hypertension:** "Silent killer" — no symptoms; major risk for stroke, heart attack, kidney failure

## Blood — Composition and Functions

**pH:** 7.35–7.45 (slightly alkaline)
**Volume:** ~5–6 litres in adult

| Component | Percentage | Contents |
|---|---|---|
| **Plasma** | ~55% | Water (90%), plasma proteins, nutrients, hormones, waste |
| **Formed elements** | ~45% | RBCs, WBCs, Platelets |

**Serum = Plasma minus clotting factors (fibrinogen removed after clot forms)**

---

### Red Blood Cells (RBCs / Erythrocytes)

- Count: **4.5–5.5 million/mm³** (males); 4–5 million (females)
- Shape: **Biconcave disc** — increases surface area for gas exchange
- **No nucleus; no mitochondria** (in mature mammalian RBCs — not in birds/reptiles)
- Contains: **Haemoglobin (Hb)** — iron-containing protein that carries O₂
- Lifespan: **~120 days**
- Produced in: **Red bone marrow** (adults); liver and spleen (foetal life)
- Destroyed in: **Spleen** ("**graveyard of RBCs**") and liver (Kupffer cells)

**Haemoglobin:**
- 4 globin chains + 4 haem groups; each haem carries **1 Fe²⁺** (iron) atom → 4 O₂ molecules per Hb
- Oxyhaemoglobin (HbO₂): Bright red; formed in lungs
- Deoxyhaemoglobin (HHb): Dark red/maroon; in tissues
- Carbaminohaemoglobin: Carries CO₂ (from tissues to lungs)
- **CO poisoning:** Carbon monoxide binds Hb with **200–250× higher affinity** than O₂ → displaces O₂ → asphyxia; cherry-red skin/lips (carboxyhaemoglobin is bright red)

**Anaemia:** Reduced haemoglobin or RBC count → fatigue, pallor, breathlessness
- Iron-deficiency anaemia: Most common; low Fe²⁺ → less Hb
- Pernicious anaemia: B12 deficiency (no intrinsic factor)
- Sickle cell anaemia: Genetic (HbS) — covered in Genetics lesson
- Thalassaemia: Genetic defect in globin chain synthesis

---

### White Blood Cells (WBCs / Leukocytes)

- Count: **4,000–11,000/mm³** (much fewer than RBCs)
- **Have nucleus**; no haemoglobin; larger than RBCs
- Lifespan: Hours to years (varies greatly)

**Types of WBCs:**

**Granulocytes** (granules in cytoplasm):
| Cell | % | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Neutrophils** | 60–70% | Phagocytosis; first line against bacteria — most common WBC |
| **Eosinophils** | 2–4% | Allergic reactions; fight parasitic infections |
| **Basophils** | 0.5–1% | Release histamine (allergic responses, inflammation) |

**Agranulocytes** (no granules):
| Cell | % | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Lymphocytes** | 20–25% | Adaptive immunity — B cells (antibody production), T cells (kill infected cells) |
| **Monocytes** | 2–8% | Phagocytosis; become **macrophages** when entering tissues |

**Leukaemia:** Cancer of WBCs (uncontrolled WBC proliferation)

---

### Platelets (Thrombocytes)

- Count: **1.5–4 lakh/mm³** (150,000–400,000)
- Smallest formed element; **no nucleus**; are cell fragments (from megakaryocytes in bone marrow)
- Lifespan: **~10 days**
- Function: **Blood clotting (haemostasis)**
- **Thrombocytopenia:** Abnormally low platelet count → bleeding tendency (characteristic feature of **dengue fever**)

**Blood Clotting Cascade:**
1. Damaged vessel → **platelet plug** forms (primary haemostasis)
2. Coagulation cascade activated → **Prothrombin** (inactive) → **Thrombin** (active)
   - Requires: **Calcium (Ca²⁺)** and **Vitamin K**
3. Thrombin converts **Fibrinogen** (soluble) → **Fibrin** (insoluble threads)
4. Fibrin mesh traps RBCs → **blood clot (thrombus)**

**Anticoagulants:** Heparin (natural; produced by basophils/mast cells); Warfarin (drug)
**Haemophilia A:** Factor VIII absent; Haemophilia B: Factor IX absent (X-linked recessive)

## Lymphatic System

The lymphatic system is the circulatory system's silent partner — it runs alongside blood vessels but carries a different fluid.

**Lymph:** Fluid similar to blood plasma (less protein); no RBCs; contains lymphocytes
- Formed from tissue fluid (interstitial fluid) that leaks from blood capillaries
- Lymph vessels collect this fluid and return it to the bloodstream

**Functions:**
1. Return excess tissue fluid to blood (prevents oedema/swelling)
2. Absorb digested fats from small intestine (via lacteals)
3. Immune defence (lymph nodes filter lymph; lymphocytes attack pathogens)

**Lymph nodes:**
- Small bean-shaped structures along lymph vessels
- Filter lymph; contain lymphocytes and macrophages
- **Swell during infection** (swollen glands = enlarged lymph nodes)

**Key lymphoid organs:**
| Organ | Role |
|---|---|
| **Bone marrow** | Origin of all blood cells (B cells mature here) |
| **Thymus** | T cell maturation (most active in childhood; shrinks with age) |
| **Spleen** | Largest lymphoid organ; filters blood; removes old RBCs; reservoir |
| **Tonsils** | First line of defence in throat; trap pathogens |

**Thoracic duct:** Largest lymph vessel; collects lymph from lower body and left side; empties into **left subclavian vein**
**Right lymphatic duct:** Drains right side of head, neck, right arm; empties into right subclavian vein

## Common Circulatory Disorders

| Disorder | Cause | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Myocardial Infarction** (Heart Attack) | Coronary artery blockage → heart muscle death | Severe chest pain radiating to left arm; ECG changes |
| **Angina pectoris** | Reduced coronary flow without infarction | Chest pain on exertion, relieved by rest |
| **Atherosclerosis** | Plaque (cholesterol, calcium) build-up in arteries | Narrowed arteries → reduced blood flow |
| **Hypertension** | Chronic high BP (>140/90) | Silent; increases risk of heart attack, stroke, kidney disease |
| **Stroke** | Blood supply to brain interrupted (clot or haemorrhage) | Sudden paralysis, speech difficulty |
| **Anaemia** | Low Hb/RBCs | Fatigue, pallor, breathlessness |
| **Thalassaemia** | Genetic globin chain defect | Chronic haemolytic anaemia |
| **Dengue** | Dengue virus → thrombocytopenia | Platelet count drops; bleeding risk |
| **Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)** | Blood clot in deep vein (usually leg) | Swelling, pain; clot can travel to lungs (pulmonary embolism) |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Pulmonary artery carries DEOXYGENATED blood; pulmonary veins carry OXYGENATED blood** — the only artery-vein pair that reverses the usual oxygen convention.
2. **SA node = "Pacemaker of the heart"** — located in the right atrium wall; sets the rhythm; artificial pacemaker replaces it when it fails.
3. **Normal BP = 120/80 mmHg; measured by sphygmomanometer** — hypertension ≥140/90.
4. **Heart sounds: "Lub" = AV valve closure (tricuspid + mitral); "Dub" = semilunar valve closure (aortic + pulmonary)** — which valves cause which sound.
5. **Left ventricle has the thickest wall** — pumps blood to the entire body; right ventricle only to nearby lungs.
6. **Tricuspid valve = 3 cusps (right side); Mitral/Bicuspid = 2 cusps (left side)** — "tri" vs "bi" — don't swap the sides.
7. **RBCs: no nucleus, no mitochondria, ~120-day lifespan; destroyed in spleen** — all four facts.
8. **Spleen = "graveyard of RBCs" and largest lymphoid organ** — both roles tested separately.
9. **Neutrophils are most abundant WBC (60–70%); Lymphocytes second (20–25%)** — exact percentages and rankings.
10. **Platelets are not cells — they are cell fragments from megakaryocytes; no nucleus** — "smallest formed element."
11. **Blood clotting requires Vitamin K and Calcium (Ca²⁺)** — both cofactors; Vitamin K deficiency → impaired clotting.
12. **Fibrinogen (soluble) → Fibrin (insoluble) by thrombin** — serum = plasma minus fibrinogen.
13. **CO binds haemoglobin with 200–250× greater affinity than O₂** — CO poisoning causes cherry-red skin (carboxyhaemoglobin).
14. **Thymus: T cell maturation; most active in childhood; shrinks (involutes) with age** — adults have minimal thymic tissue.
15. **Thoracic duct empties into left subclavian vein** — largest lymph vessel; drains lower body and left upper body.
16. **Dengue fever causes thrombocytopenia (low platelet count)** — not low RBC or WBC count; platelet drop is the hallmark.
