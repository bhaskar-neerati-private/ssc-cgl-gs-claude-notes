# Nervous System & Endocrine Glands — Brain, Spinal Cord & Hormones

## Two Languages the Body Uses

The body has two systems for coordinating its 37 trillion cells — and they couldn't be more different in style.

The **nervous system** is instant messaging: electrical signals travelling at up to 120 m/s, reaching their target in milliseconds, causing immediate but short-lived responses. Touch a hot stove — your hand withdraws before your conscious brain has even registered the pain.

The **endocrine system** is the postal service: chemical messengers (hormones) released into the bloodstream, travelling slowly but affecting every cell they reach, producing sustained changes that can last hours, days, or even years.

Together they govern everything — movement, growth, reproduction, metabolism, mood, sleep, stress, and survival.

---

## The Nervous System — Overview

**Two major divisions:**

| Division | Components | Controls |
|---|---|---|
| **Central Nervous System (CNS)** | Brain + Spinal Cord | Integration, processing |
| **Peripheral Nervous System (PNS)** | All nerves outside CNS | Carries signals to/from CNS |

**PNS subdivisions:**
- **Somatic NS:** Voluntary control (skeletal muscles; conscious movement)
- **Autonomic NS:** Involuntary (heart, glands, smooth muscle)
  - **Sympathetic:** "Fight or flight" — speeds heart, dilates pupils, releases glucose, increases alertness
  - **Parasympathetic:** "Rest and digest" — slows heart, promotes digestion, conservation

---

## The Neuron — Functional Unit

Every thought, sensation, and movement is transmitted by a nerve cell (neuron):

**Parts of a neuron:**
| Part | Function |
|---|---|
| **Cell body (Soma)** | Contains nucleus; metabolic centre |
| **Dendrites** | Receive incoming signals (from other neurons or receptors) |
| **Axon** | Carries nerve impulse AWAY from cell body |
| **Myelin sheath** | Fatty insulation around axon; speeds conduction dramatically |
| **Nodes of Ranvier** | Gaps in myelin where impulse "jumps" (saltatory conduction) |
| **Axon terminal (Synaptic knob)** | Releases neurotransmitters into the synapse |

**Types of neurons:**
- **Sensory (Afferent):** Carry impulses from sense organs/receptors → CNS
- **Motor (Efferent):** Carry impulses from CNS → muscles/glands
- **Interneurons (Association):** Connect sensory and motor neurons within CNS

**Synapse:** The gap between two neurons (or neuron and muscle). Electrical impulse triggers release of **neurotransmitters** into the synaptic cleft → bind receptors on next neuron.

**Key neurotransmitters:**
| Neurotransmitter | Role | Disease link |
|---|---|---|
| **Acetylcholine (ACh)** | Neuromuscular junction; memory; parasympathetic | Alzheimer's (ACh deficit) |
| **Dopamine** | Reward, motivation, smooth movement | Parkinson's (substantia nigra neurons die) |
| **Serotonin** | Mood, sleep, appetite regulation | Depression (treated with SSRIs) |
| **Noradrenaline** | Alertness, fight-or-flight arousal | — |
| **GABA** | Main inhibitory neurotransmitter (calming) | Alcohol, benzodiazepines enhance GABA |

## The Brain

**Weight:** ~1.4 kg (adult)
**Neurons:** ~86 billion neurons; ~100 trillion synaptic connections
**Protection:** Skull (bone) + Meninges (3 membranes: dura mater, arachnoid mater, pia mater) + CSF (cerebrospinal fluid cushion)

**Three major divisions:**

---

### 1. Forebrain

**Cerebrum (Cerebral cortex):**
- Largest part (~85% of brain weight)
- Divided into **2 hemispheres** (left and right) connected by **corpus callosum**
- Left hemisphere: Language, logic, analysis (dominant in most right-handed people)
- Right hemisphere: Creativity, spatial awareness, music

**Four lobes per hemisphere:**
| Lobe | Location | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Frontal lobe** | Front | Motor cortex (voluntary movement); personality; reasoning; Broca's area (speech production) |
| **Parietal lobe** | Top/side | Somatosensory cortex (touch, pain, temperature, pressure) |
| **Temporal lobe** | Side | Hearing; memory; Wernicke's area (speech comprehension) |
| **Occipital lobe** | Back | Vision; visual processing |

**Broca's aphasia:** Damage to Broca's area → can understand speech but cannot produce fluent speech
**Wernicke's aphasia:** Damage to Wernicke's area → can produce speech but comprehension is impaired

**Thalamus:**
- "Relay station" — all sensory information (except smell) passes through thalamus before reaching cerebral cortex
- Filters and routes sensory data

**Hypothalamus:**
- Master of homeostasis — controls the internal environment
- Functions: Hunger, thirst, body temperature, sleep-wake cycles, circadian rhythm
- Controls the **pituitary gland** (via releasing hormones)
- Generates emotions (rage, fear, pleasure)

---

### 2. Midbrain

- Relay centre for **visual and auditory reflexes**
- Controls eye movements (oculomotor reflexes)
- Contains **substantia nigra**: rich in dopamine neurons; degeneration here → **Parkinson's disease**

---

### 3. Hindbrain

**Cerebellum ("Little brain"):**
- Located at back, below cerebrum
- Functions: **Coordination of voluntary movements, balance and posture, fine motor skills**
- Damage → ataxia (loss of coordination, staggering gait)

**Pons:**
- Connects brain to spinal cord and cerebellum
- Contains **pneumotaxic centre** (regulates breathing rate and depth)
- Controls some facial reflexes

**Medulla Oblongata:**
- The lowest part of the brain; merges into spinal cord
- Controls **vital involuntary functions** (autonomic reflexes):
  - **Cardiac centre:** Heart rate regulation
  - **Respiratory centre:** Breathing rhythm
  - **Vasomotor centre:** Blood pressure
  - **Vomiting, coughing, swallowing, sneezing reflexes**
- Damage to medulla = **instantly fatal**

---

## Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)

- Specialised tight junctions in brain capillary walls
- **Allows:** Glucose, O₂, CO₂, lipid-soluble substances, some drugs (anaesthetics, alcohol)
- **Blocks:** Most large molecules, many drugs, pathogens, proteins
- Protects brain from toxins and infections
- **Challenge in medicine:** Many drugs cannot reach the brain; brain tumours and infections are harder to treat

---

## The Spinal Cord

- ~**45 cm long**; runs through vertebral canal
- Protected by: **Vertebral column** (bones) + Meninges + CSF
- **Structure:** Grey matter (butterfly-shaped) in centre; White matter on outside
  - (Opposite of cerebrum — in brain, grey matter is on outside)
- **Grey matter:** Cell bodies of neurons; reflex integration
- **White matter:** Myelinated axons conducting signals up and down
- Functions: Conducts impulses between brain and body; mediates **reflex actions**

---

## Reflex Action

A **reflex** is a rapid, automatic, involuntary response to a stimulus — it does NOT wait for conscious brain input.

**Reflex arc (pathway):**
**Receptor → Afferent neuron → Spinal cord → Efferent neuron → Effector (muscle/gland)**

**Examples:**
- **Knee-jerk (patellar) reflex:** Tap on patellar tendon → quadriceps contracts; monosynaptic (only one synapse)
- **Withdrawal reflex:** Touching hot object → hand pulls back (pain signal reaches brain after withdrawal)
- **Pupillary reflex:** Light in eye → pupil constricts

**Cranial nerves:** 12 pairs from brain
**Spinal nerves:** 31 pairs from spinal cord (Cervical 8, Thoracic 12, Lumbar 5, Sacral 5, Coccygeal 1)

## The Endocrine System — Overview

**Endocrine glands = ductless glands** — secrete hormones directly into blood
**Hormones:** Chemical messengers that act on specific target organs (with receptors for that hormone)

---

## Pituitary Gland — "The Master Gland"

- Size of a pea; located at base of brain in a bony cavity (sella turcica)
- Connected to hypothalamus by pituitary stalk
- Controlled by hypothalamic releasing/inhibiting hormones

**Anterior Pituitary (Adenohypophysis):**
| Hormone | Action | Excess | Deficiency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Growth Hormone (GH/Somatotropin)** | Bone, muscle growth; cell division | Gigantism (children), Acromegaly (adults) | Dwarfism |
| **TSH** (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone) | Stimulates thyroid → T3/T4 | — | Hypothyroidism |
| **ACTH** (Adrenocorticotropic Hormone) | Stimulates adrenal cortex → cortisol | Cushing's | Addison's |
| **FSH** (Follicle Stimulating Hormone) | Ovarian follicle/sperm development | — | Infertility |
| **LH** (Luteinizing Hormone) | Ovulation (F); testosterone (M) | — | — |
| **Prolactin** | Milk production (lactation) | Galactorrhoea | Lactation failure |

**Posterior Pituitary (Neurohypophysis):** Stores and releases hypothalamic hormones:
| Hormone | Action | Deficiency |
|---|---|---|
| **ADH (Vasopressin)** | Water reabsorption in kidney tubules | **Diabetes insipidus** (polyuria — excessive urination; not to be confused with diabetes mellitus) |
| **Oxytocin** | Uterine contractions during labour; milk let-down reflex; "love hormone" | Prolonged labour |

---

## Thyroid Gland

- **Location:** Front of neck, below larynx
- **Shape:** Butterfly-shaped; two lobes connected by isthmus
- **Largest purely endocrine gland**
- Requires **iodine** to produce hormones

| Hormone | Action |
|---|---|
| **Thyroxine (T4) and Triiodothyronine (T3)** | Regulate basal metabolic rate (BMR), growth, development, protein synthesis |
| **Calcitonin** | Lowers blood calcium (opposes parathyroid hormone) |

**Thyroid Disorders:**
| Condition | Cause | Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Goitre** | Iodine deficiency → low T3/T4 → high TSH → thyroid enlarges | Swollen neck; common in iodine-poor inland areas |
| **Hypothyroidism (Myxoedema in adults)** | Low thyroid hormones | Low BMR, weight gain, fatigue, cold intolerance, slow pulse |
| **Cretinism** | Hypothyroidism during fetal/infant life | Stunted physical and intellectual development; preventable with iodine |
| **Hyperthyroidism (Graves' disease)** | Autoimmune — excess T3/T4 | High BMR, weight loss, rapid heart rate, bulging eyes (exophthalmos) |

---

## Parathyroid Glands

- **4 tiny glands** embedded in the posterior surface of the thyroid
- **Parathyroid Hormone (PTH):** **Raises blood calcium**
  - Increases bone resorption (releases Ca²⁺ from bone)
  - Increases Ca²⁺ reabsorption in kidney
  - Activates Vitamin D → more Ca²⁺ absorbed from gut
- **Hypoparathyroidism:** Low Ca²⁺ → **tetany** (involuntary muscle spasms, cramps)
- **Calcitonin (thyroid) opposes PTH** — classic antagonist pair

---

## Adrenal Glands

- 2 glands sitting on top of each kidney ("suprarenal glands")
- Two completely different layers:

**Adrenal Cortex (outer; steroid hormones):**
| Type | Hormone | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Glucocorticoids** | **Cortisol** | Anti-inflammatory; raises blood glucose; stress response ("stress hormone") |
| **Mineralocorticoids** | **Aldosterone** | Na⁺ and water reabsorption in kidney → raises blood pressure |
| **Androgens** | DHEA | Weak male sex hormones (both sexes) |

**Adrenal Medulla (inner; derived from neural tissue):**
| Hormone | Action |
|---|---|
| **Adrenaline (Epinephrine)** | "Fight or flight" — raises heart rate, BP, blood glucose; dilates pupils and airways |
| **Noradrenaline (Norepinephrine)** | Similar; primarily raises BP; also a neurotransmitter |

**Adrenal Disorders:**
| Condition | Cause | Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Addison's disease** | Adrenal cortex failure | Low cortisol + aldosterone → low BP, fatigue, weight loss, skin darkening (hyperpigmentation) |
| **Cushing's syndrome** | Excess cortisol | Weight gain, "moon face," "buffalo hump," hypertension, diabetes |

---

## Other Important Glands

### Thymus
- Location: Upper chest (mediastinum), behind sternum
- Hormone: **Thymosin** — promotes T-lymphocyte (T cell) maturation
- Most active in childhood; gradually **involutes (shrinks) with age**
- Crucial for immune system development in early life

### Pineal Gland
- Located in the brain (between cerebral hemispheres); tiny, pinecone-shaped
- Hormone: **Melatonin** — regulates **circadian rhythm** (sleep-wake cycle)
- Secreted in **darkness**; suppressed by **light**
- Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) linked to disrupted melatonin rhythms in winter

### Gonads
| Organ | Hormone | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Testes** | **Testosterone** | Secondary male sexual characteristics; sperm production; anabolic effects |
| **Ovaries** | **Oestrogen, Progesterone** | Menstrual cycle; secondary female sexual characteristics; pregnancy |

| Gland | Hormone | Excess Disorder | Deficiency Disorder |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ant. Pituitary | GH | Gigantism (child), Acromegaly (adult) | Dwarfism |
| Post. Pituitary | ADH | Water retention (SIADH) | **Diabetes insipidus** |

| Pancreas β cells | Insulin | Hypoglycaemia | **Diabetes mellitus** |
| Pineal | Melatonin | — | Sleep disruption, SAD |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Cerebellum = coordination, balance, posture** — NOT thinking or emotion; damage → ataxia (uncoordinated movement).
2. **Medulla oblongata = vital reflex centre (heart rate, breathing, BP, vomiting)** — damage here is instantly fatal.
3. **Hypothalamus controls the pituitary** — hypothalamus is the "boss of the boss"; regulates hunger, thirst, temperature, circadian rhythm.
4. **Broca's area = speech PRODUCTION; Wernicke's area = speech COMPREHENSION** — both in left hemisphere (usually); exact lobe matters: Broca's in frontal, Wernicke's in temporal.
5. **Thalamus = sensory relay station** — all senses except smell relay through thalamus; olfaction (smell) goes directly to cortex.
6. **Pituitary = "Master Gland"; Hypothalamus = "Master of the Master Gland"** — hypothalamus controls pituitary.
7. **GH excess: Gigantism (child — bones still growing); Acromegaly (adult — bones thicken, especially hands, feet, face)** — same hormone, different age = different disease.
8. **ADH deficiency → Diabetes insipidus (excessive water loss in urine)** — NOT diabetes mellitus; completely different disease despite the name.
9. **Thyroid is the largest purely endocrine gland** — pituitary is master but tiny; pancreas is larger but mixed (not purely endocrine).
10. **Goitre = iodine deficiency → low T3/T4 → TSH rises → thyroid enlarges trying to make more hormone** — the goitre is the thyroid's futile overwork.
11. **Calcitonin (thyroid) LOWERS calcium; PTH (parathyroid) RAISES calcium** — antagonists; calcitonin vs PTH.
12. **Adrenal cortex (outer) = steroid hormones (cortisol, aldosterone); Adrenal medulla (inner) = catecholamines (adrenaline, noradrenaline)** — different hormones from different layers.
13. **Addison's = adrenal cortex FAILURE (low cortisol + aldosterone); Cushing's = EXCESS cortisol** — opposite conditions from the same gland.
14. **Thymus secretes Thymosin for T-cell maturation; most active in childhood; shrinks with age** — do not confuse thymus (immune) with thyroid (metabolism).
15. **Melatonin from pineal gland; secreted in darkness; regulates sleep-wake cycle** — light suppresses melatonin; darkness triggers it.
16. **Knee-jerk reflex is monosynaptic** — only one synapse (between sensory neuron and motor neuron directly); fastest reflex; used clinically to test spinal cord integrity at L2–L4.
