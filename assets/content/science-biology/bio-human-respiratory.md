# Human Respiratory System — Lungs, Breathing & Gas Exchange

## Every Breath You Take

Right now, without thinking about it, you are breathing approximately 15 times a minute. Over a lifetime, that adds up to about 600 million breaths. Each one draws air through a precisely engineered pathway, delivers oxygen to 37 trillion cells, and expels the carbon dioxide those cells produce.

The respiratory system is deceptively simple at first glance — air in, air out. But the underlying mechanics, chemistry, and control systems are anything but simple. And SSC tests all of them.

---

## Two Levels of Respiration

The word "respiration" means two different things — you must know both:

| Level | What it is | Where it happens |
|---|---|---|
| **External respiration (Breathing/Ventilation)** | Mechanical exchange of gases between body and atmosphere | Lungs |
| **Internal (Cellular) respiration** | Chemical process — food + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + Energy (ATP) | Inside every cell |

**Aerobic cellular respiration:**
C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + **38 ATP**

**Anaerobic respiration (in muscles during intense exercise):**
C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2 Lactic acid + **2 ATP** (causes muscle fatigue and burn)

**Fermentation (in yeast):**
C₆H₁₂O₆ → 2 Ethanol + 2CO₂ + **2 ATP** (basis of bread-making and alcohol)

## The Respiratory Pathway

**Nose → Nasal cavity → Pharynx → Larynx → Trachea → Primary bronchi (2) → Bronchioles → Alveoli**

---

### 1. Nose and Nasal Cavity

The nose is not just an entry point — it is an air conditioning unit:
- **Nasal hairs:** Trap large particles (dust, pollen)
- **Mucous membrane:** Warms and humidifies incoming air; mucus traps fine particles
- **Cilia:** Sweep debris-laden mucus backward to the throat (mucociliary escalator)
- **Sinuses:** Air-filled spaces in skull bones (frontal, maxillary, ethmoid, sphenoid); infected → sinusitis

---

### 2. Pharynx and Larynx

**Pharynx (Throat):** Common passage for air and food; where epiglottis diverts food to oesophagus

**Larynx (Voice Box):**
- Cartilaginous structure containing vocal cords (vocal folds)
- **Thyroid cartilage = "Adam's apple"** (largest cartilage; more prominent in males)
- **Epiglottis:** Folds over the glottis (larynx opening) during swallowing
- **Sound production:** Exhaled air vibrates vocal cords; pitch controlled by cord tension; loudness by air volume

---

### 3. Trachea (Windpipe)

- ~10–12 cm long; ~2 cm diameter
- **C-shaped cartilage rings** (16–20 rings): Prevent trachea from collapsing during inhalation; open side faces oesophagus (allows food to pass)
- Lined with **ciliated pseudostratified columnar epithelium + goblet cells** (mucus secretors)
- Mucus + cilia = "mucociliary escalator" — sweeps debris upward to be swallowed or expelled
- **Carina:** Ridge at the bottom where trachea branches into two primary bronchi

---

### 4. Bronchi and Bronchioles

| Structure | Key Feature |
|---|---|
| **Primary bronchi (2)** | Right bronchus: shorter, wider, more vertical → foreign objects more likely to enter right lung |
| **Secondary bronchi** | One per lobe (3 right, 2 left) |
| **Bronchioles** | No cartilage; only smooth muscle — spasm of this muscle causes **asthma** |
| **Terminal bronchioles** | Last conducting (non-gas-exchange) airways |
| **Respiratory bronchioles** | First airways with alveoli; gas exchange begins |

---

### 5. Lungs

**Two lungs — not mirror images:**
| | Right Lung | Left Lung |
|---|---|---|
| Lobes | 3 (upper, middle, lower) | 2 (upper, lower) |
| Size | Larger | Smaller (has cardiac notch for heart) |

**Pleural membranes (double-walled covering):**
- **Visceral pleura:** Sticks to lung surface
- **Parietal pleura:** Lines the inner thoracic cavity wall
- **Pleural fluid** between layers: Reduces friction during breathing; maintains surface tension that keeps lungs expanded
- **Pneumothorax:** Air enters pleural cavity (stab wound, rib fracture) → lung collapses

---

### 6. Alveoli — The Exchange Units

- ~**300–600 million alveoli** in both lungs
- Total surface area: **~70–80 m²** (size of a tennis court/badminton court)
- Wall is only **one cell thick** — extremely short diffusion distance
- Surrounded by a dense network of capillaries

**Cells in alveoli:**
| Cell | Function |
|---|---|
| **Type I pneumocytes** | Squamous cells; gas exchange (most of alveolar surface) |
| **Type II pneumocytes** | Secrete **surfactant** — reduces surface tension; prevents alveolar collapse |
| **Alveolar macrophages (Dust cells)** | Phagocytose particles, bacteria, debris reaching alveoli |

**Surfactant:** Phospholipid–protein mixture coating alveolar surface.
- Without it, alveoli would collapse (very high surface tension)
- **Deficient in premature babies → NRDS** (Neonatal Respiratory Distress Syndrome / Hyaline Membrane Disease)

## Mechanism of Breathing

Breathing is a mechanical process driven by **pressure differences** between the atmosphere and lungs.

### Inspiration (Inhalation) — Active Process
1. **Diaphragm contracts** → moves downward (flattens from dome shape)
2. **External intercostal muscles contract** → ribs swing upward and outward
3. Thoracic volume **increases** → intrapulmonary pressure **falls below** atmospheric pressure
4. Air rushes **in** (from high → low pressure)

### Expiration (Exhalation) — Passive in normal breathing
1. **Diaphragm relaxes** → returns to dome shape
2. Elastic recoil of lung tissue
3. Thoracic volume **decreases** → pressure **rises above** atmospheric
4. Air moves **out**
5. **Forced expiration** (coughing, sneezing, singing): abdominal muscles + internal intercostal muscles actively contract

**Diaphragm:** The most important muscle of breathing; innervated by the **phrenic nerve** (C3, C4, C5 spinal nerves — "C3, 4, 5 keeps the diaphragm alive")

## Lung Volumes (Spirometry)

| Volume / Capacity | Approx. Value | Definition |
|---|---|---|
| **Tidal Volume (TV)** | 500 mL | Air inhaled/exhaled in one normal quiet breath |
| **Inspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV)** | ~3000 mL | Extra air that can be forcibly inhaled after normal inspiration |
| **Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV)** | ~1100 mL | Extra air that can be forcibly exhaled after normal expiration |
| **Residual Volume (RV)** | ~1200 mL | Air remaining after maximum expiration — can NEVER be expelled |
| **Vital Capacity (VC)** | ~4600 mL | TV + IRV + ERV — maximum breathable range |
| **Total Lung Capacity (TLC)** | ~6000 mL | VC + RV — total lung volume |

**Normal respiratory rate:** 12–20 breaths/min (adult); 30–60 (newborn)

## Gas Exchange

Exchange is driven by **diffusion** — gases move from high partial pressure to low partial pressure.

### At the Alveoli (External respiration)
| Gas | In alveolar air | In deoxygenated blood (arriving) | Movement |
|---|---|---|---|
| O₂ | High (PO₂ ~104 mmHg) | Low (PO₂ ~40 mmHg) | Alveoli → Blood |
| CO₂ | Low (PCO₂ ~40 mmHg) | High (PCO₂ ~45 mmHg) | Blood → Alveoli |

### At the Tissues (Internal respiration)
| Gas | In arterial blood | In tissues | Movement |
|---|---|---|---|
| O₂ | High (PO₂ ~95 mmHg) | Very low (PO₂ ~40 mmHg) | Blood → Cells |
| CO₂ | Low (PCO₂ ~40 mmHg) | High (PCO₂ ~45 mmHg) | Cells → Blood |

---

## Transport of Gases in Blood

### Oxygen Transport
- **97%:** As **oxyhaemoglobin (HbO₂)** — O₂ bound to haemoglobin inside RBCs
- **3%:** Dissolved in plasma

### Carbon Dioxide Transport
- **70%:** As **bicarbonate ions (HCO₃⁻)** in plasma (Chloride shift mechanism — CO₂ + H₂O → H₂CO₃ → H⁺ + HCO₃⁻; catalysed by carbonic anhydrase in RBCs)
- **23%:** As **carbaminohaemoglobin** (CO₂ bound to globin proteins of Hb)
- **7%:** Dissolved in plasma

## Control of Breathing

Breathing is **involuntary** — controlled automatically by the brain:

| Centre | Location | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Respiratory rhythm centre** | Medulla oblongata | Sets basic breathing rhythm (inspiration/expiration cycle) |
| **Pneumotaxic centre** | Pons | Fine-tunes rate and depth of breathing |

**Chemical regulation (most important):**
- **Rising blood CO₂ (hypercapnia)** → increased blood H⁺ (falling pH) → medullary chemoreceptors detect → **increased respiratory rate**
- O₂ level alone is NOT the primary driver of normal breathing — CO₂/pH is
- **Holding your breath:** CO₂ builds up until it overrides the voluntary hold

## Common Respiratory Disorders

| Disorder | Cause | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Asthma** | Allergic bronchospasm; smooth muscle contraction; excess mucus | Episodic breathlessness, wheezing; bronchodilators (salbutamol) treat |
| **Bronchitis** | Viral/bacterial; smoking | Inflamed bronchi; persistent cough, sputum |
| **Emphysema** | Alveolar wall destruction (primarily smoking) | Reduced gas exchange surface; barrel chest; irreversible |
| **COPD** | Combination of emphysema + chronic bronchitis | Irreversible airflow obstruction; major cause of death globally |
| **Pneumonia** | *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (most common); viruses | Alveoli fill with fluid (exudate); fever, breathlessness, cough |
| **Tuberculosis (TB)** | *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* (acid-fast bacillus) | Chronic cough, blood in sputum; granulomas (tubercles) in lungs |
| **Silicosis** | Silica dust inhalation (quarry/mine workers) | Lung fibrosis; occupational disease |
| **Asbestosis** | Asbestos fibres | Lung fibrosis; mesothelioma (cancer of pleura) |
| **Pleurisy** | Inflammation of pleura | Sharp, stabbing chest pain on breathing |
| **NRDS** | Surfactant deficiency in premature babies | Alveoli collapse; breathing difficulty at birth |

### Tuberculosis (TB) — Special Focus
- **Causative agent:** *Mycobacterium tuberculosis* — gram-positive, acid-fast bacillus (AFB)
- **Transmission:** Airborne droplets (coughing, sneezing)
- **India:** Highest TB burden globally
- **Mantoux test (Tuberculin skin test):** Tests for prior TB exposure/infection
- **BCG vaccine** (Bacillus Calmette-Guérin): Prevents severe childhood TB; part of India's UIP
- **Treatment:** DOTS (Directly Observed Treatment Short-course) — combination of antibiotics (rifampicin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide, ethambutol) for 6 months
- **MDR-TB:** Multi-drug resistant TB — major public health challenge

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Tidal Volume = ~500 mL** — the normal breath in quiet breathing; Vital Capacity = ~4600 mL (TV + IRV + ERV).
2. **Residual Volume (~1200 mL) CANNOT be expelled** — even after maximum expiration; keeps alveoli from collapsing.
3. **Right lung = 3 lobes; Left lung = 2 lobes** — cardiac notch on left lung accommodates the heart.
4. **Right primary bronchus is wider, shorter, more vertical** → foreign bodies inhaled are more likely to enter the right lung.
5. **Diaphragm contracts → moves DOWN during inspiration** — counterintuitive; contraction flattens it (not elevates it).
6. **Normal expiration is PASSIVE** — no muscle energy needed; driven by elastic recoil of lungs.
7. **Alveolar surface area = ~70–80 m²** — size of a tennis/badminton court; frequently tested as "tennis court" comparison.
8. **Type II pneumocytes secrete surfactant** — not Type I; deficiency in premature babies → NRDS.
9. **CO₂ is transported mainly as bicarbonate ions (HCO₃⁻) — ~70%** — not as carbaminohaemoglobin (23%) or dissolved (7%).
10. **CO₂ level (not O₂) is the primary stimulus for breathing** — rising CO₂ → falling pH → medullary chemoreceptors stimulated.
11. **Respiratory centre is in the medulla oblongata** — not in the cerebrum (which controls voluntary breathing during speech).
12. **TB caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis (acid-fast bacillus)** — BCG vaccine prevents it; DOTS is the treatment protocol.
13. **Pneumonia: Streptococcus pneumoniae is most common bacterial cause** — COVID-19 pneumonia is viral.
14. **Emphysema = alveolar wall destruction** — irreversible; causes "barrel chest"; mainly from smoking.
15. **Asthma = bronchospasm (smooth muscle contraction) in bronchioles** — no cartilage in bronchioles so they can constrict; treated with bronchodilators.
16. **Phrenic nerve innervates the diaphragm** — damage to phrenic nerve (e.g., neck injury) can paralyse diaphragm and stop breathing.
