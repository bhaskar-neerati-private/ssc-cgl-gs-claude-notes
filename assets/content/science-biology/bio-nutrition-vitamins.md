# Nutrition — Vitamins, Minerals, Deficiency Diseases & BMI

## The Price of Ignorance

Between the 16th and 18th centuries, more British sailors died of **scurvy** than in all naval battles combined. They were dying from a missing nutrient — Vitamin C — even while surrounded by water. The remedy (citrus fruit) was discovered in 1747 by James Lind, yet it took the British Navy another 50 years to adopt it. Meanwhile, sailors' teeth fell out, their gums bled, their old wounds re-opened, and they died of infections that a healthy body would have beaten easily.

This story captures something essential: nutrients are not food — they are the specific chemical compounds your body needs and cannot always make itself. Without them, not starvation but a precise chemical failure kills you.

## The Six Classes of Nutrients

| Class | Energy (kcal/g) | Role | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Carbohydrates** | 4 | Primary energy fuel; stored as glycogen | Glucose, starch, sucrose, glycogen |
| **Proteins** | 4 | Structure, enzymes, hormones, immunity | Amino acids; essential + non-essential |
| **Fats (Lipids)** | 9 | Energy reserve; fat-soluble vitamin absorption; cell membranes; hormones | Saturated, unsaturated, essential fatty acids |
| **Vitamins** | 0 | Coenzymes, antioxidants, specific functions | A, B, C, D, E, K |
| **Minerals** | 0 | Structural (bones, teeth); enzyme cofactors; electrolytes | Ca, Fe, I, Zn, Na, K |
| **Water** | 0 | Transport, temperature regulation, solvent; ~60-70% of body weight | — |

> **Caloric mnemonic:** Carbs = 4, Protein = 4, Fat = 9, Alcohol = 7 (not a nutrient, but provides calories)

## Macronutrients in Detail

### Carbohydrates
- Simple: monosaccharides (glucose, fructose, galactose), disaccharides (sucrose, lactose, maltose)
- Complex: polysaccharides — starch (plant storage), glycogen (animal/human storage in liver+muscle), cellulose (dietary fibre, not digestible by humans)
- **Dietary fibre** (cellulose): not digested but essential for gut motility; prevents constipation, reduces colon cancer risk

### Proteins
- Built from amino acids; 20 amino acids total
- **Essential amino acids (9):** cannot be synthesised by the human body; must come from diet
  - HILL MVT + Phenylalanine: **H**istidine, **I**soleucine, **L**eucine, **L**ysine, **M**ethionine, **V**aline, **T**hreonine, Tryptophan, Phenylalanine
- Complete proteins: contain all 9 essential amino acids (meat, eggs, dairy, soy)
- Incomplete proteins: missing one or more (most plant proteins — rice + dal together complement each other)
- **Kwashiorkor:** severe protein deficiency in children (belly swelling from fluid retention, depigmented hair); contrast with **Marasmus** (overall calorie deficiency → wasting, severe underweight)

### Fats
- Saturated (solid at room temperature): animal fats, butter, coconut oil — excess raises LDL cholesterol
- Unsaturated: monounsaturated (olive oil), polyunsaturated (omega-3 fish oil, omega-6 sunflower oil)
- **Essential fatty acids (cannot be synthesised):** Linoleic acid (omega-6) and Alpha-linolenic acid (omega-3) — must come from diet
- Trans fats (partially hydrogenated oils): raise LDL, lower HDL — most harmful; linked to heart disease

## Vitamins — The Micro-Regulators

Vitamins are organic compounds needed in tiny amounts — yet their absence causes distinctive diseases. Divided into two groups based on solubility:

### Fat-Soluble Vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Stored in liver and adipose tissue; can accumulate → **toxicity possible** with excess
- Do not need to be consumed daily
- Absorbed with dietary fat; deficiency in fat malabsorption conditions (celiac disease, cystic fibrosis)

### Water-Soluble Vitamins (B complex + C)
- Not stored in body (except B12 briefly in liver); excreted in urine
- Must be consumed regularly
- Cooking destroys many (especially Vit C and folate) — raw/lightly cooked better

## Vitamin A (Retinol)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Chemical name** | Retinol (plant form: beta-carotene — provitamin A, converted in intestine) |
| **Sources** | Liver, egg yolk, dairy, green leafy vegetables (spinach, methi), orange/yellow vegetables (carrot, pumpkin — beta-carotene) |
| **Functions** | Formation of **rhodopsin** (visual pigment in rod cells of retina → dim light vision); skin and mucous membrane integrity; immune function; normal reproduction and growth |
| **Deficiency** | **Night blindness (Nyctalopia)** — earliest sign; inability to see in dim light. Prolonged deficiency → **Xerophthalmia** (dry eye) → **Bitot's spots** on conjunctiva → **keratomalacia** (corneal ulceration) → permanent blindness. Xerophthalmia is leading cause of preventable childhood blindness worldwide |
| **Toxicity** | Excess (hypervitaminosis A): liver damage, headache, bone pain, teratogenic (harmful to foetus) |

## Vitamin B Complex

Eight B vitamins, all water-soluble, most function as coenzymes in metabolism:

### B1 — Thiamine
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Whole grains (especially husk of rice), peas, pork, legumes |
| **Function** | Coenzyme in carbohydrate metabolism (pyruvate dehydrogenase); nerve function |
| **Deficiency** | **Beriberi** — historically common in polished-rice-eating Asian populations: **Dry beriberi** (peripheral neuropathy: numbness, weakness, wasting of limbs); **Wet beriberi** (heart failure with oedema/fluid retention). **Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome** in alcoholics (thiamine malabsorption) |

### B2 — Riboflavin
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Milk, eggs, organ meats, green leafy, almonds |
| **Function** | Component of FAD and FMN (electron carriers in energy metabolism) |
| **Deficiency** | **Ariboflavinosis**: Cheilosis (cracks and sores at corners of mouth), glossitis (red, inflamed tongue), angular stomatitis, corneal vascularisation |

### B3 — Niacin (Nicotinic acid)
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Meat, fish, groundnuts, whole grains; can also be synthesised from tryptophan (amino acid) |
| **Function** | Component of NAD and NADP (electron carriers; essential for hundreds of reactions) |
| **Deficiency** | **Pellagra** — the "4 Ds": **D**ermatitis (sun-exposed skin: rough, darkened, peeling), **D**iarrhea, **D**ementia, **D**eath (if untreated). Historically linked to maize-dependent diets (maize has niacin in bound, unabsorbable form unless treated with lime = "nixtamalization") |

### B5 — Pantothenic Acid
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Widespread (virtually all foods); meat, avocado, broccoli |
| **Function** | Component of Coenzyme A (CoA) — essential in fatty acid synthesis and breakdown; Krebs cycle |
| **Deficiency** | Rare; **Burning feet syndrome** (paraesthesias of feet) |

### B6 — Pyridoxine
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Poultry, fish, potatoes, bananas, fortified cereals |
| **Function** | Transamination and decarboxylation of amino acids; neurotransmitter synthesis |
| **Deficiency** | Peripheral neuropathy, anaemia, skin lesions, confusion |

### B7 — Biotin
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Egg yolk, nuts, liver; also produced by gut bacteria |
| **Function** | Carboxylation reactions (fatty acid synthesis, gluconeogenesis) |
| **Deficiency** | Rare; dermatitis, alopecia (hair loss), neurological symptoms. Raw egg whites contain **avidin** — a protein that binds biotin and prevents absorption (cooking denatures avidin) |

### B9 — Folic Acid (Folate)
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Green leafy vegetables (folate = "foliage"), legumes, fortified cereals, liver |
| **Function** | DNA synthesis; cell division; amino acid metabolism |
| **Deficiency** | **Megaloblastic anaemia** (large, immature RBCs; impaired DNA synthesis in rapidly dividing cells). **Neural tube defects (NTDs)** in foetus — spina bifida, anencephaly — if mother is deficient in early pregnancy (first trimester critical). Folic acid supplementation before and during pregnancy is standard medical advice worldwide |

### B12 — Cobalamin
| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | **ONLY in animal products** — meat, fish, eggs, dairy; fortified foods for vegans |
| **Function** | Myelin sheath synthesis; DNA synthesis; folate metabolism |
| **Deficiency** | **Pernicious anaemia** — requires **intrinsic factor** (glycoprotein secreted by parietal cells of stomach) for absorption; autoimmune gastritis destroys parietal cells → no intrinsic factor → B12 malabsorption despite adequate dietary intake. Also: **Subacute combined degeneration of spinal cord** (posterior and lateral column degeneration). Strict vegans at high risk; may take 3–6 years to develop deficiency (stores in liver) |

## Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Chemical name** | Ascorbic acid; L-ascorbic acid |
| **Sources** | Citrus fruits, **Amla/Indian gooseberry** (richest source — 600 mg/100g), guava, capsicum, kiwi, broccoli. Destroyed by cooking, heat, storage |
| **Functions** | Essential for **collagen synthesis** (hydroxylation of proline and lysine in collagen fibres); antioxidant; enhances non-haem iron absorption; immune function; wound healing |
| **Deficiency** | **Scurvy** — collagen synthesis fails → blood vessel fragility: bleeding gums, loose teeth, perifollicular haemorrhages (bleeding around hair follicles), poor wound healing, old wounds re-open, fatigue, joint pain. Historically a disease of sailors on long voyages (no fresh fruit for months) |
| **Toxicity** | High doses → kidney stones (oxalate), diarrhoea |

---

## Vitamin D (Calciferol)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Chemical name** | Cholecalciferol (D3, from animal sources/skin synthesis); Ergocalciferol (D2, from plant sources) |
| **Synthesis** | **Skin converts 7-dehydrocholesterol → Cholecalciferol (D3) under UV-B radiation** — the "sunshine vitamin" |
| **Sources** | Sunlight (primary); fatty fish, egg yolk, liver, fortified milk |
| **Activation** | Liver: D3 → 25-OH-D3 → Kidney: → 1,25-(OH)₂-D3 (calcitriol, active form) |
| **Functions** | Promotes calcium and phosphorus absorption in intestine; bone mineralisation; immune modulation |
| **Deficiency** | **Rickets** (in children): soft, weak bones → bowed legs, swollen joints, delayed teething, skull softening. **Osteomalacia** (in adults): bone pain, soft bones, muscle weakness, fractures. India has widespread Vitamin D deficiency despite abundant sunshine (skin coverage, indoor lifestyle) |
| **Toxicity** | Hypercalcaemia, calcium deposits in soft tissues (fat-soluble; can accumulate) |

---

## Vitamin E (Tocopherol)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Sources** | Vegetable oils (sunflower, wheat germ), nuts, seeds, green leafy vegetables |
| **Functions** | **Antioxidant** — protects cell membranes from oxidative damage; protects Vitamin A from oxidation; immune function |
| **Deficiency** | **Haemolytic anaemia** (especially in premature infants — premature = low Vit E stores); peripheral neuropathy; reproductive problems |

---

## Vitamin K (Koagulation — German)

| Aspect | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Forms** | K1 (Phylloquinone) — from green leafy vegetables; K2 (Menaquinone) — synthesised by gut bacteria |
| **Sources** | Dark green leafy vegetables (spinach, kale, broccoli); K2 from gut bacteria and fermented foods |
| **Functions** | **Blood clotting** — activates clotting factors II (prothrombin), VII, IX, X (and Proteins C and S); bone metabolism |
| **Deficiency** | **Haemorrhagic disease** — prolonged bleeding; spontaneous bruising. Newborns are at risk (gut not colonised by bacteria yet; low Vit K across placenta) → **Vitamin K injection given at birth** as standard prophylaxis |
| **Drug interaction** | **Warfarin** (blood thinner / anticoagulant) works by **blocking Vitamin K activity** → reduces clotting factor production → prevents clots (used in DVT, atrial fibrillation, mechanical heart valves). Foods rich in Vit K can reduce warfarin's effect |

## Key Minerals

| Mineral | Function | Deficiency | Excess | Sources |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Calcium (Ca)** | Bones, teeth, muscle contraction, nerve signals, blood clotting | Rickets, Osteoporosis, Tetany | Hypercalcaemia | Dairy, green leafy, sesame |
| **Iron (Fe)** | Haemoglobin (oxygen transport), myoglobin, enzyme cofactor | **Iron-deficiency anaemia** (most common nutritional deficiency worldwide) | Haemochromatosis | Red meat, spinach, jaggery, lentils |
| **Iodine (I)** | Thyroid hormone synthesis (T3/T4) | **Goitre** (thyroid enlargement), **Cretinism** (hypothyroidism in infancy) | Iodine toxicity | Iodised salt, seafood |
| **Zinc (Zn)** | Enzyme cofactor (>300 enzymes), immune function, wound healing, taste and smell | Growth retardation, hypogonadism, poor wound healing, taste loss | — | Meat, shellfish, nuts, seeds |
| **Fluoride (F)** | Dental enamel hardening; bone strength | Dental caries (tooth decay) | **Dental fluorosis** (white mottling of teeth); **Skeletal fluorosis** (joint pain, bone deformity) — excess fluoride in groundwater (endemic in Rajasthan, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh) | Fluoridated water, tea |
| **Phosphorus (P)** | Bones, teeth (with Ca), ATP, DNA/RNA structure | Rare; weakness, bone pain | — | Dairy, meat, whole grains |
| **Sodium (Na)** | Extracellular fluid balance, nerve impulses, muscle contraction | Hyponatraemia (muscle cramps, confusion) | Hypertension | Table salt, processed food |
| **Potassium (K)** | Intracellular fluid, heart rhythm, nerve function | Hypokalaemia (muscle weakness, arrhythmia) | Hyperkalaemia (cardiac arrest risk) | Banana, potato, legumes |
| **Magnesium (Mg)** | Enzyme cofactor, muscle relaxation, energy metabolism | Muscle cramps, arrhythmia, anxiety | — | Nuts, seeds, green leafy, whole grains |

## BMI — Body Mass Index

**Formula:** BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m)²

| Classification | BMI (kg/m²) |
|---|---|
| Underweight | < 18.5 |
| Normal weight | 18.5 – 24.9 |
| Overweight | 25.0 – 29.9 |
| Obese Class I | 30.0 – 34.9 |
| Obese Class II | 35.0 – 39.9 |
| Morbidly Obese | ≥ 40.0 |

**Limitations of BMI:**
- Does not distinguish fat from muscle (a muscular athlete may have BMI > 25 but low body fat)
- Does not indicate fat distribution (abdominal fat = greater health risk than same BMI with fat elsewhere)
- **Waist-Hip Ratio (WHR)** or **Waist Circumference** better reflect cardiovascular risk

**BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate):** Energy needed to maintain basic bodily functions at rest (breathing, circulation, temperature). Influenced by body size, muscle mass, age, sex, thyroid function.

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Vitamins A, D, E, K = fat-soluble** (stored in body → can cause toxicity); **B complex and C = water-soluble** (not stored → need regular intake; excess excreted in urine).
2. **Night blindness = Vitamin A deficiency** — rhodopsin (the visual pigment in rod cells) requires Vitamin A; rod cells are for dim-light vision.
3. **Scurvy = Vitamin C deficiency** (collagen fails → bleeding gums, loose teeth); **Beriberi = Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)**; **Pellagra = Vitamin B3 (Niacin)** — the "4 Ds: Dermatitis, Diarrhoea, Dementia, Death."
4. **Rickets = Vitamin D deficiency in children** (bowed legs, soft bones); **Osteomalacia = same deficiency in adults** (bone pain, fractures).
5. **Pernicious anaemia = Vitamin B12 deficiency** requiring intrinsic factor from the stomach; **Megaloblastic anaemia = Vitamin B9 (folic acid) deficiency** — both cause large, abnormal RBCs.
6. **Vitamin K is needed for blood clotting** (activates factors II, VII, IX, X); warfarin works by blocking Vitamin K's action.
7. **Richest source of Vitamin C = Amla (Indian gooseberry)** — NOT orange or lemon. Guava is also very rich. Many candidates incorrectly choose citrus fruits.
8. **Vitamin B12 is found ONLY in animal products** — strict vegans and vegetarians must supplement B12 to avoid deficiency and neurological damage.
9. **Essential amino acids = 9** (cannot be made by the human body; must come from diet). All 20 amino acids are needed; 11 are non-essential (made by the body).
10. **Essential fatty acids = Linoleic (omega-6) and Alpha-Linolenic acid (omega-3)** — cannot be synthesised; needed for cell membranes, hormone synthesis, and inflammation control.
11. **Caloric values: Carbohydrates = 4 kcal/g; Proteins = 4 kcal/g; Fats = 9 kcal/g; Alcohol = 7 kcal/g** — fats are the most energy-dense nutrient.
12. **Iron-deficiency anaemia is the most common nutritional deficiency worldwide** — affects ~2 billion people. Symptoms: fatigue, pallor, breathlessness (haemoglobin cannot carry enough oxygen).
13. **Iodine deficiency causes goitre** (thyroid enlargement); **fluoride excess causes fluorosis** (dental mottling, skeletal damage) — both affect teeth but by different elements.
14. **Vitamin D is synthesised in skin under UV-B radiation** — but excessive sunscreen use, indoor lifestyle, and dark skin pigmentation (melanin absorbs UV) all reduce synthesis.
15. **Folic acid (B9) must be taken before conception and in the first trimester** to prevent neural tube defects (spina bifida) in the foetus — not just "during" pregnancy.
16. **BMI = weight(kg)/height(m)²; Normal = 18.5–24.9; Obese ≥ 30** — BMI does not distinguish fat from muscle; waist circumference is a better predictor of metabolic disease risk.
