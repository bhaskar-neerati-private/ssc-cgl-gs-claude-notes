# Flagship Economic Schemes — Jan Dhan, Ujjwala, PMAY, MUDRA & Others

## The Art of the Scheme

Every Indian government runs on schemes. But since 2014 — and the tradition predates even that — the Central Government has launched a series of flagship programmes designed to solve India's most stubborn structural problems: financial exclusion, energy poverty, homelessness, lack of health insurance, and agricultural vulnerability.

SSC loves these schemes. Not the high concepts — the precise numbers: exact dates, exact amounts, exact premiums, exact targets. This lesson is a master reference for every scheme the exam tests.

Think of it this way: India had a "missing bottom" problem. The poorest 40% had no bank accounts, no LPG, no pucca homes, no crop insurance, no health cover. These schemes are India's effort — systematically — to push every service to that missing bottom.

---

## 1. PM Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) — Financial Inclusion

**Full name:** Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana
**Launched:** 28 August 2014
**PM:** Narendra Modi

**What it does:** Opens a basic zero-balance bank account for every unbanked household in India.

**Key Features:**
| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Account type | Zero-balance savings account |
| Debit card | RuPay debit card free |
| Overdraft facility | ₹10,000 overdraft (after 6 months satisfactory use) |
| Accident insurance | ₹2 lakh (via RuPay card — revised from ₹1 lakh) |
| Life insurance | ₹30,000 (Jeevan Jyoti cover) |
| Direct Benefit Transfer | All government subsidies flow directly into Jan Dhan accounts |

**Achievement:** 500+ million accounts opened (by 2023) — making it the world's largest financial inclusion initiative by number. More women account holders than men.

**Why this matters:** Before PMJDY, only ~40% of Indians had bank accounts. The scheme enabled Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) — cutting out middlemen and reducing leakages in subsidy delivery.

## 2. PM Ujjwala Yojana (PMUY) — Clean Cooking Fuel

**Full name:** Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana
**Launched:** 1 May 2016, from Ballia, Uttar Pradesh (a symbolically backward district)
**PM:** Narendra Modi
**Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas

**What it does:** Provides free LPG connections to BPL (Below Poverty Line) women — ending their dependence on firewood and biomass-burning chulhas.

**Why it matters:** Indoor air pollution from biomass cooking caused ~5 lakh deaths per year in India (WHO data). Women and children were the primary victims.

**Key Numbers:**
| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Initial target | 5 crore connections (BPL via ration card) |
| Extended target | 8 crore connections |
| Ujjwala 2.0 | August 2021 — extended to migrants without ration card; added first refill free |
| Achievement | 10 crore+ connections as of 2023 |

**Beneficiary criteria:** Women from BPL households identified via SECC-2011 data; ration card holders.

## 3. PM Awas Yojana (PMAY) — Housing for All

Two parallel schemes:

### PMAY-Urban (PMAY-U)
**Launched:** 25 June 2015
**Mission:** "Housing for All" in urban areas
**Four Verticals (implementation modes):**
1. **In-situ Slum Redevelopment (ISSR):** Redevelop slums using land as resource
2. **Affordable Housing in Partnership (AHP):** Government + private/public agencies build affordable units
3. **Beneficiary-Led Construction (BLC):** Individual house construction/enhancement subsidy
4. **Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS):** Interest subsidy on home loans for EWS/LIG/MIG categories

### PMAY-Gramin (PMAY-G)
**Launched:** 20 November 2016 (renamed from Indira Awas Yojana, which ran 1985–2016)
**Target beneficiaries:** Homeless rural families identified via SECC-2011 (Socio-Economic and Caste Census)

**Financial assistance per unit:**
- Plain areas: ₹1.20 lakh per house
- North East, hilly, difficult areas: ₹1.30 lakh per house
- MGNREGS convergence: 90–95 days of unskilled labour wages for each house built

**Target:** 2.95 crore houses under PMAY-G by 2024

## 4. MUDRA / PM Mudra Yojana — Micro-Enterprise Finance

**Full name:** Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency
**MUDRA established:** 2015 (as a subsidiary of SIDBI)
**PM Mudra Yojana (PMMY):** Umbrella scheme for MUDRA loans

**Three loan tiers:**
| Category | Loan Amount | Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Shishu** (infant) | Up to ₹50,000 | Starting/very early-stage micro-enterprises |
| **Kishore** (adolescent) | ₹50,001 – ₹5 lakh | Growing micro-enterprises |
| **Tarun** (youth) | ₹5 lakh – ₹10 lakh | Established micro-enterprises needing expansion |

**Key Features:**
- No collateral required for any tier
- Available from all scheduled commercial banks, MFIs, RRBs, NBFCs
- Target: Non-farm micro-enterprises (artisans, shopkeepers, hawkers, small manufacturers)
- Since 2022: Tarun Plus category introduced — ₹10 lakh to ₹20 lakh for graduating Tarun borrowers (credit guarantee still available)

## 5. Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) — Clean India

**Launched:** 2 October 2014 (Gandhi Jayanti)
**PM:** Narendra Modi, with massive public participation drive
**Two arms:**
- **SBM (Gramin/Rural):** Under Ministry of Jal Shakti (formerly Ministry of Drinking Water and Sanitation)
- **SBM (Urban):** Under Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs

**Phase 1 Goal (2014–2019):** Make India Open Defecation Free (ODF) by 2 October 2019 (Gandhi's 150th birth anniversary)

**Achievement Phase 1:**
- 10 crore+ individual household toilets (IHHLs) constructed
- 6 lakh+ villages declared ODF
- India declared ODF on 2 October 2019 (self-declared; progress varied by state)

**SBM Phase 2 (ODF Plus), 2020–2025:**
- Beyond toilets: Focus on solid waste management, liquid waste management, maintaining ODF status
- Plastic-free villages, clean drainage, biodegradable waste processing

## 6. Digital India Mission

**Launched:** 1 July 2015
**PM:** Narendra Modi
**Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY)

**Three Core Pillars:**
1. Digital Infrastructure as a Core Utility
2. Governance and Services on Demand
3. Digital Empowerment of Citizens

**Key Initiatives under Digital India:**
| Initiative | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **BharatNet** | Optical fibre connectivity to all gram panchayats |
| **UPI** (Unified Payments Interface) | Real-time instant digital payments (NPCI, 2016) |
| **DigiLocker** | Cloud storage for official documents (Aadhaar, PAN, certificates) |
| **Common Service Centres (CSC)** | Rural digital service delivery points (1 per gram panchayat) |
| **UMANG App** | Unified Mobile Application for New-age Governance — 200+ central/state services |
| **e-RUPI** | Prepaid digital voucher for targeted subsidy delivery (2021) |

## 7. Skill India Mission

**Launched:** 15 July 2015 (World Youth Skills Day)
**Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship

**Key Programmes:**
| Programme | Detail |
|---|---|
| **PMKVY** (Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana) | Short-term skill training; Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) for existing skills |
| **NSDC** (National Skill Development Corporation) | PPP body promoting skill training institutes |
| **Jan Shikshan Sansthan** | Vocational training for non-literate/neo-literate adults |
| **National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme** | Industry-based apprenticeship training |

**Target:** Skill 40 crore people by 2022 (revised targets ongoing)

## 8. PM-KISAN — Farmer Income Support

**Full name:** Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi
**Launched:** 24 February 2019 (announced in Interim Budget 2019-20)
**Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers' Welfare

**How it works:**
- ₹6,000 per year per farmer family
- Paid in **3 instalments of ₹2,000 each** (every 4 months)
- Directly into farmers' bank accounts (DBT)
- Eligibility: All landholder farmer families (small, marginal, and now all farmers)

**Scale:** 11 crore+ farmer families benefiting (2023)

## 9. Ayushman Bharat — Health Scheme

**Launched:** September 2018 (PM-JAY component)
**Two components:**

### PM-JAY (Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana)
- **World's largest government health insurance scheme** by coverage
- ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary hospitalisation
- Covers ~55 crore people (bottom 40% of population)
- Cashless treatment at empanelled public and private hospitals
- Implemented by: National Health Authority (NHA) — apex body
- No premium from beneficiaries — fully funded by Centre (60%) and states (40%)
- **AB-PMJAY + Ayushman Vaya Vandana:** Extended to all citizens aged 70+ regardless of income (2024)

### Health and Wellness Centres (HWCs)
- 1.5 lakh sub-health centres upgraded to comprehensive primary care centres
- Services: Non-communicable diseases (diabetes, hypertension), maternal-child health, telemedicine

## 10. Jal Jeevan Mission

**Launched:** 15 August 2019 (Independence Day)
**PM:** Narendra Modi
**Full name:** Jal Jeevan Mission (Har Ghar Jal)
**Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Jal Shakti

**Goal:** Functional Household Tap Connection (FHTC) to every rural household by 2024
- Potable piped water at 55 litres per person per day
- Coverage: 19 crore rural households
- 6 crore+ FHTCs provided as of 2022 (rapid scale-up ongoing)

## 11. Insurance Schemes (PMSBY, PMJJBY) — Jan Suraksha

Both launched: **9 May 2015** as part of Jan Suraksha schemes

### PMSBY — Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana
| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Type | Accidental death and disability insurance |
| Cover | ₹2 lakh (accidental death or permanent total disability); ₹1 lakh partial disability |
| Annual premium | ₹20 (revised to ₹20 per year — extremely low) |
| Age eligibility | 18–70 years |
| Administered by | General insurance companies (through bank accounts) |

### PMJJBY — Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana
| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Type | Life insurance (death from any cause) |
| Cover | ₹2 lakh |
| Annual premium | ₹436 (revised from ₹330) |
| Age eligibility | 18–50 years (coverage continues to age 55) |
| Administered by | Life insurance companies (LIC and others) |

## 12. Atal Pension Yojana (APY) — Pension for Unorganised Sector

**Launched:** 9 May 2015 (same day as Jan Suraksha schemes)
**Target:** Unorganised sector workers
**Age of entry:** 18–40 years
**Regulated by:** PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority)

**How it works:**
- Subscriber contributes a monthly amount based on age of entry and desired pension
- At age 60, receives a guaranteed monthly pension of ₹1,000, ₹2,000, ₹3,000, ₹4,000, or ₹5,000
- Younger you join = smaller monthly contribution for same pension
- Government co-contributed 50% (up to ₹1,000/year) for eligible subscribers till 2015–2020 (scheme closed for government employees and income taxpayers from Oct 2022)

---

## 13. PM Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) — Crop Insurance

**Launched:** 13 January 2016
**Purpose:** Comprehensive crop insurance against natural calamities, pests, diseases

**Premium structure (extremely low for farmers):**
| Crop Season | Farmer's Premium |
|---|---|
| Kharif crops | 2% of sum insured |
| Rabi crops | 1.5% of sum insured |
| Horticultural/commercial crops | 5% of sum insured |

**Balance of premium:** Paid equally by Centre and state governments
**Implemented by:** Agriculture Insurance Company of India (AIC) and private insurers

---

## 14. Stand-Up India — SC/ST and Women Entrepreneurs

**Launched:** 5 April 2016
**Purpose:** Promote entrepreneurship among SC/ST communities and women by ensuring access to institutional credit

**Key feature:**
- At least **1 SC/ST borrower** and **1 woman borrower** per bank branch
- Loans from ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore for greenfield enterprises

---

## 15. National Food Security Act (NFSA) 2013

Not a scheme but the legal foundation for India's food subsidy architecture:

- **Coverage:** 75% of rural population + 50% of urban population
- **Entitlement:** 5 kg food grain per person per month at:
  - Rice: ₹3/kg
  - Wheat: ₹2/kg
  - Coarse grains: ₹1/kg
- **Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY):** Poorest of the poor households get 35 kg/month
- **One Nation One Ration Card (ONORC):** Portability — any beneficiary can lift ration from any Fair Price Shop anywhere in India

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **PMJDY = 28 August 2014; zero-balance account; RuPay card; ₹2 lakh accident cover** — all four facts; note accident cover is via RuPay card, not the account itself.
2. **Ujjwala = 1 May 2016; Ballia UP; BPL women; Ujjwala 2.0 = August 2021** — launch town (Ballia) is frequently tested.
3. **PMAY-G ≠ PMAY-U** — G for Gramin (rural, 20 Nov 2016); U for Urban (25 Jun 2015); different dates, different ministries.
4. **MUDRA tiers: Shishu ≤₹50k; Kishore ₹50k–₹5L; Tarun ₹5L–₹10L** — exact amounts; no collateral in all three tiers.
5. **Swachh Bharat launched on 2 October 2014 = Gandhi Jayanti** — both the date and the occasion are tested; ODF target was 2 October 2019 (Gandhi 150th anniversary).
6. **PMSBY = accidental insurance (₹2L cover, ₹20 premium, 18–70 yrs); PMJJBY = life insurance (₹2L cover, ₹436 premium, 18–50 yrs)** — confusing the two is a classic SSC trap.
7. **Both PMSBY and PMJJBY launched on 9 May 2015** — same day as APY; all three are Jan Suraksha schemes.
8. **APY = pension for unorganised sector workers; PFRDA regulated; join age 18–40; pension at 60** — not for government employees or taxpayers post-2022.
9. **PM-KISAN = ₹6,000/year in 3 instalments of ₹2,000 each; launched 24 February 2019** — confusing annual amount with instalment amount is a trap.
10. **Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY = ₹5 lakh per family per year; cashless; 55 crore people; National Health Authority (NHA) implements** — not IRDA or NHB.
11. **Jal Jeevan Mission = Har Ghar Jal; launched 15 August 2019; 55 litres/person/day** — the 55-litre standard is tested.
12. **PM Fasal Bima premiums: Kharif = 2%, Rabi = 1.5%, horticultural = 5%** — Rabi (wheat, mustard) being lower (1.5%) than Kharif (2%) is counter-intuitive and hence tested.
13. **Stand-Up India = at least 1 SC/ST and 1 woman per branch; ₹10L–₹1Cr; launched 5 April 2016** — do not confuse with Start-Up India (16 Jan 2016).
14. **PMAY-G financial assistance: ₹1.2 lakh (plain areas) and ₹1.3 lakh (hilly/NE/difficult areas)** — the ₹10,000 difference is a tiny but testable detail.
15. **Indira Awas Yojana (IAY) was renamed PMAY-G in 2016** — IAY existed since 1985; PMAY-G is its successor, not a new scheme entirely.
16. **Digital India = 1 July 2015; three pillars; UPI under NPCI; BharatNet for gram panchayats** — SSC tests both the date and the implementing agency for specific components (UPI is NPCI, not a government ministry directly).
