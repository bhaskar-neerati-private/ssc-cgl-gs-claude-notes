# Capital Markets — SEBI, Stock Exchanges, Mutual Funds & Insurance (IRDAI)

## Where Savers Meet Borrowers

Every economy has two groups: those with surplus money (savers) and those who need money (borrowers — companies, governments, entrepreneurs). **Financial markets** are the plumbing that connects them.

When a company needs ₹1,000 crore to build a factory, it can borrow from a bank — or it can go directly to millions of savers by issuing shares or bonds in the **capital market**. When an insurance company collects your premium, it invests that pool of money in the same markets to generate returns. When you open a mutual fund SIP, your ₹500/month joins thousands of others and flows into shares of Reliance, Infosys, HDFC.

This web of markets, instruments, and regulators is India's financial system. For SSC, the key areas are: **SEBI** (the market regulator), **BSE and NSE** (the two major exchanges with their indices SENSEX and NIFTY), **Mutual Funds**, **IRDAI** (insurance regulator), and **PFRDA** (pension regulator).

---

## Structure of Financial Markets

```
Financial Markets
├── Money Market (short-term: < 1 year)
│   ├── Treasury Bills (T-bills)
│   ├── Commercial Paper (CP)
│   ├── Certificate of Deposit (CD)
│   └── Call Money (overnight bank lending)
│
└── Capital Market (long-term: > 1 year)
    ├── Primary Market (new issues)
    │   └── IPO, FPO, Rights Issue, Bonds
    └── Secondary Market (trading existing securities)
        └── BSE, NSE (stock exchanges)
```

**Money Market:** RBI is the key regulator here. Short-term instruments for liquidity management.
**Capital Market:** SEBI is the regulator. Long-term financing for businesses and governments.

## SEBI — Securities and Exchange Board of India

**Established (as non-statutory body):** 12 April 1988 (executive resolution — no statutory powers yet)
**Statutory powers granted:** 30 January 1992 under the **SEBI Act, 1992**
**Headquarters:** Bandra Kurla Complex (BKC), Mumbai

SEBI is India's market regulator — not a constitutional body, but a statutory one (created by an Act of Parliament).

### Three Objectives of SEBI
1. **Protect** the interests of investors in securities
2. **Promote** the development of the securities market
3. **Regulate** the securities market

### What SEBI Regulates
- Stock exchanges (BSE, NSE) and their brokers
- Merchant bankers (who manage IPOs)
- **Mutual Funds** (all mutual funds regulated by SEBI, not RBI)
- Depositories (NSDL, CDSL)
- Portfolio Managers, Investment Advisers
- Credit Rating Agencies (CRISIL, ICRA, CARE)
- Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs — formerly called FIIs)
- Venture Capital Funds, Alternative Investment Funds

### What SEBI Does NOT Regulate
- **Banks → RBI**
- **Insurance companies → IRDAI**
- **Pension funds → PFRDA**
- **Commodities market** (earlier Forward Markets Commission; merged with SEBI in 2015 — now SEBI regulates commodities too)

### SEBI's Three Powers (Triple Role)
- **Quasi-legislative:** Issues rules and regulations for market participants
- **Quasi-executive:** Investigates, inspects, enforces compliance
- **Quasi-judicial:** Adjudicates disputes; can impose penalties (fines, bans)

## Stock Exchanges

### BSE — Bombay Stock Exchange
- **Established: 1875** — the **oldest stock exchange in Asia**
- **HQ:** Phiroze Jeejeebhoy Towers, Dalal Street, Mumbai
- **Index: SENSEX** (Sensitive Index) — 30 largest and most actively traded companies
- SENSEX base year: **1978-79 = 100**
- Full form: **Sensitive Index** (popularly "Sensex" or "BSE 30")
- BSE also has BSE 100, BSE 200, BSE 500 indices

### NSE — National Stock Exchange
- **Established: 1992** (recommended by the Pherwani Committee, 1991); began trading: **1994**
- **HQ:** Mumbai
- **Index: NIFTY 50** (also called CNX Nifty / National Fifty) — 50 companies from 13 sectors
- NIFTY base date: **3 November 1995 = 1000**
- NSE introduced **electronic (screen-based) trading** in India — revolutionary; eliminated the old open-outcry system
- NSE has **higher daily trading volume** than BSE (NSE > BSE in turnover)
- NSE runs NSE IFSC at GIFT City, Gujarat

### BSE vs NSE at a Glance

| Feature | BSE | NSE |
|---|---|---|
| **Year** | **1875** | **1992** |
| **Index** | **SENSEX** (30 stocks) | **NIFTY 50** (50 stocks) |
| **Base year** | 1978-79 = 100 | Nov 3, 1995 = 1000 |
| **Location** | Dalal Street, Mumbai | Mumbai |
| **Claim to fame** | Oldest in Asia | Electronic trading pioneer; higher volumes |

## Depositories: Dematerialising Shares

Before 1996, shares were physical paper certificates — prone to forgery, loss, and slow transfer. Depositories solved this.

**NSDL (National Securities Depository Limited)**
- Established: **1996** — India's **first depository**
- Promoted by: NSE, IDBI, UTI

**CDSL (Central Depository Services Limited)**
- Established: **1999** — second depository
- Promoted by: BSE

**Dematerialisation:** Converting physical share certificates → electronic entries in a **Demat Account** held with a depository. Regulated by SEBI; depository participants (banks, brokers) are the interface.

## Mutual Funds

A mutual fund pools money from many investors and invests it in a diversified portfolio (stocks, bonds, money market instruments) managed by professional fund managers.

**Regulator: SEBI** (not RBI — a common mistake)

**AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India):** Self-regulatory industry body; promotes best practices; runs the "Mutual Funds Sahi Hai" investor education campaign. AMFI is NOT the regulator (SEBI is).

**UTI — First Mutual Fund:**
- **Unit Trust of India (UTI):** Established in **1963** under UTI Act, 1963 — India's first mutual fund
- Initially set up under RBI; later transferred to IDBI supervision
- US-64 (Unit Scheme 1964) was its flagship scheme — crisis in 2001 when NAV fell below face value
- Post-crisis: split into **UTI Mutual Fund** (SEBI regulated, private management) and **SUUTI** (Specified Undertaking of UTI — government managed; holds old AS-64 investors)

### Key Mutual Fund Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **NAV (Net Asset Value)** | Per-unit price of a mutual fund = (Assets − Liabilities) / Number of units |
| **SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)** | Fixed periodic investment (monthly, quarterly) |
| **ELSS** | Equity Linked Savings Scheme — tax-saving MF (Section 80C); 3-year lock-in |
| **ETF (Exchange Traded Fund)** | Mutual fund that trades on stock exchange like a share; tracks an index |
| **Index Fund** | Passively managed fund that mirrors an index (NIFTY 50, SENSEX) |
| **Liquid Fund** | Invests in money market; high liquidity; overnight/91-day instruments |

## Insurance: IRDAI

**IRDAI: Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India**
- **Established:** Under IRDA Act, **1999** (statutory body)
- **HQ: Hyderabad** (moved from Delhi in 2001) — the only major financial regulator not in Mumbai
- **Regulates:** Life insurance, general insurance (health, motor, fire, marine), reinsurance companies

### Life Insurance: LIC
**Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC)**
- Established: **1956** under the LIC Act, 1956 — created by nationalising 245 private insurance companies
- **Government-owned** (majority stake); was a monopoly until 2000
- **India's largest financial institution by Assets Under Management**
- LIC IPO: May 2022 — government sold 3.5% stake; at that time the **largest IPO in Indian history** (raised ~₹21,000 crore)

### General Insurance
**GIC Re (General Insurance Corporation of India)**
- India's national reinsurer (insures the insurers)
- Government-owned

Four public sector general insurance companies: **New India Assurance, National Insurance, Oriental Insurance, United India Insurance**

### Insurance FDI
- Insurance sector opened to private participation: **2000** (IRDA Act 1999 implemented)
- FDI in insurance: initially 26% → raised to **74%** (under automatic route for most categories)

## PFRDA and the Pension System

**PFRDA: Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority**
- **Established:** 2003 (as interim; statutory powers from PFRDA Act, **2013**)
- **HQ: New Delhi**
- **Regulates:** National Pension System (NPS), Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

**NPS (National Pension System):**
- Introduced for new **Central Government employees from 2004** (replaced the old Defined Benefit pension)
- Extended to **all citizens from 2009**
- Market-linked pension — employee and employer contribute; invested in equity + debt
- On retirement, up to 60% can be withdrawn; remaining 40% must buy annuity
- Tax benefit under Section 80CCD

**Atal Pension Yojana (APY):**
- Launched: May 2015
- For unorganised sector workers (< 40 years of age)
- Government co-contributes 50% of subscriber's contribution (for 5 years, for eligible)
- Fixed monthly pension: ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 depending on contribution

## GIFT City: India's International Financial Hub

**GIFT City:** Gujarat International Finance Tec-City, Gandhinagar — India's first **International Financial Services Centre (IFSC)**

**IFSCA:** International Financial Services Centres Authority — single unified regulator for all financial activities at IFSCs (replaces RBI + SEBI + IRDAI + PFRDA within the IFSC zone)
**Established under IFSCA Act, 2019**

India Stock Exchange (India INX) at GIFT City is one of the key exchanges operating there.

## Key Terms: Primary vs Secondary Market

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **IPO (Initial Public Offering)** | Company sells shares to public for first time |
| **FPO (Follow-on Public Offer)** | Already listed company issues additional shares |
| **Rights Issue** | Existing shareholders offered new shares at a discount |
| **Bonus Issue** | Free shares to existing shareholders (no cash inflow) |
| **Bull Market** | Prices rising; investor confidence high |
| **Bear Market** | Prices falling; investor confidence low |
| **FPI (Foreign Portfolio Investor)** | Foreign entity investing in Indian securities (stocks, bonds) — no management control; regulated by SEBI |
| **FDI (Foreign Direct Investment)** | Foreign entity acquiring substantial stake + management control in Indian company — regulated by RBI/DPIIT |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **SEBI established 1988 (non-statutory) → statutory from 30 January 1992** — year of statutory status (1992) is most tested; 1988 is the setup date.
2. **SEBI HQ = Mumbai (BKC)** — not Delhi; not Dalal Street (that's BSE's address).
3. **BSE = 1875 = oldest stock exchange in Asia** — SENSEX tracks 30 stocks; base year 1978-79 = 100.
4. **NSE = 1992; began trading 1994** — NIFTY 50 tracks 50 stocks; base November 3, 1995 = 1000.
5. **Mutual Funds regulated by SEBI, not RBI** — the most common confusion; RBI regulates banks, SEBI regulates MFs.
6. **UTI = first mutual fund in India (1963)** — "Unit Trust" not "Mutual Trust" or any other name.
7. **NSDL (1996) = India's first depository** (promoted by NSE, IDBI, UTI); CDSL (1999) promoted by BSE.
8. **IRDAI HQ = Hyderabad** — the only major financial regulator not headquartered in Mumbai.
9. **IRDAI established under IRDA Act 1999** — the "D" in IRDAI stands for Development (not Department).
10. **LIC established 1956** by nationalising 245 private insurers; India's largest financial institution by AUM.
11. **PFRDA Act 2013** (statutory powers) — though PFRDA was created in 2003, it only became fully statutory in 2013.
12. **NPS for Central Govt employees from 2004** — replaced old pension; extended to all citizens 2009.
13. **GIC = national reinsurer** (not a life insurer; not a general insurer — it insures the insurers).
14. **FPI ≠ FDI** — FPI = portfolio investment (regulated by SEBI); FDI = strategic ownership (regulated by DPIIT/RBI).
15. **SEBI regulates commodities too** — after merger with Forward Markets Commission (FMC) in 2015.
16. **IFSCA = unified financial regulator at GIFT City** — established 2019; covers RBI+SEBI+IRDAI+PFRDA functions within IFSC zone.
