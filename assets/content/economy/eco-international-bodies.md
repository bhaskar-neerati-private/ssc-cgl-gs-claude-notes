# International Economic Bodies — WTO, IMF, World Bank Group & ADB

## The Architecture of Global Finance

After two World Wars in thirty years, the surviving powers made a decision: never again would economic nationalism, competitive currency devaluations, and protectionist trade wars poison international relations as they had in the 1930s. In July 1944, at a resort hotel in **Bretton Woods, New Hampshire**, 44 Allied nations gathered and designed three institutions to govern the post-war economic order.

Two were built at Bretton Woods itself — the **IMF** (to manage exchange rates and balance of payments) and the **World Bank** (to rebuild war-devastated economies through development loans). The third — an International Trade Organisation — was never ratified, but a temporary agreement, **GATT**, filled the gap until 1995 when the **WTO** was finally created.

For SSC, the key is knowing: what each body does, where it's headquartered, who leads it, how many members it has, and the key terminology (SDR, TRIPS, IBRD vs IDA, ADB's largest shareholder). These are the perennial exam questions.

## WTO — World Trade Organization

**Established:** 1 January 1995
**HQ:** Geneva, Switzerland
**Members:** 166 (as of 2024; India is a founding member)
**Director-General:** Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala (Nigerian; since 1 March 2021 — first African and first woman to lead WTO)

### From GATT to WTO

**GATT (General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade)** — signed 1948 — was not a formal international organisation. It was an agreement and a negotiating forum where countries voluntarily reduced tariffs through successive "rounds" of negotiations.

After 8 rounds of negotiations, the **Uruguay Round (1986–1994)** created the WTO — a full-fledged international organisation with binding dispute settlement, enforceable rules, and a broader mandate covering goods, services, and intellectual property.

**GATT → WTO:** GATT was provisional (temporary), not legally binding as an organisation. WTO is permanent, binding, and has far greater teeth.

### WTO's Core Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Most Favoured Nation (MFN)** | Any trade benefit given to one member must be given to ALL members — no discrimination between trading partners |
| **National Treatment** | Once imported goods enter a country, they must be treated the same as domestic goods (no hidden discrimination after entry) |
| **Transparency** | Members must publish all trade regulations and laws |
| **Reciprocity** | Trade concessions are negotiated mutually — you give something, you get something |

**MFN is the cornerstone principle** — SSC tests this repeatedly. If India reduces import duty on USA's software services, it must offer the same duty reduction to all WTO members.

### Key WTO Agreements

| Agreement | What It Governs |
|---|---|
| **TRIPS** (Trade-Related Intellectual Property Rights) | Patents, copyrights, trademarks, geographical indications — minimum standards all members must protect |
| **TRIMS** (Trade-Related Investment Measures) | Prevents countries from imposing discriminatory conditions on foreign investors |
| **GATS** (General Agreement on Trade in Services) | Liberalises international services trade (banking, IT, education, health) |
| **Agreement on Agriculture (AoA)** | Reduces agricultural subsidies and trade barriers; contentious for India (food security vs WTO rules) |
| **SPS Agreement** | Food safety and plant/animal health standards |
| **TBT Agreement** | Technical standards for products (prevents standards as hidden trade barriers) |

### WTO's Dispute Settlement
WTO's Dispute Settlement Mechanism (DSB — Dispute Settlement Body) is its most powerful tool — its rulings are **binding** on member countries (unlike most international forums). Countries can impose retaliatory tariffs if the losing country doesn't comply.

### Doha Development Round
Launched: 2001 in Doha, Qatar. Aimed to reform global trade rules to benefit developing countries. **Largely stalled** — rich and poor countries cannot agree on agriculture subsidies and industrial tariffs. The Doha Round remains unfinished.

## IMF — International Monetary Fund

**Established (Articles of Agreement signed):** 27 December 1945 (Bretton Woods Conference: July 1944)
**Began operations:** March 1947
**HQ:** Washington D.C., USA
**Members:** 190 (2024)
**Managing Director:** Kristalina Georgieva (Bulgarian economist; since October 2019)

### Purpose
- Promote international monetary **cooperation**
- Facilitate **exchange rate stability**
- Support countries facing **balance of payments** difficulties
- Provide temporary financing and policy advice
- Monitor the global economy (publishes World Economic Outlook — WEO)

### How IMF Is Governed
- **Board of Governors:** Finance Minister or Central Bank Governor of each member country — highest authority; meets annually (Annual Meetings, jointly with World Bank)
- **Executive Board:** 24 directors representing member countries or groups — day-to-day decisions
- **Voting:** Weighted by **quota** (based on economic size, openness, reserves) — NOT one-country-one-vote
  - USA has the largest quota (~17% of votes)
  - IMF decisions requiring an 85% supermajority give USA an effective **veto**

### Special Drawing Rights (SDR)
The IMF's synthetic reserve asset — **not a currency** but an international reserve asset allocated to members in proportion to their quotas.

**SDR basket (since 2016):** US Dollar + Euro + Chinese Yuan (Renminbi) + Japanese Yen + British Pound
- China's Yuan was added in **2016** — a landmark recognition of China's economic rise
- SDR value fluctuates daily based on the basket currencies

**SDR can be exchanged between members for hard currencies** — it is a claim on freely usable currencies.

### Key IMF Lending Facilities
| Facility | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Stand-By Arrangement (SBA)** | Short-term BoP support; most common |
| **Extended Fund Facility (EFF)** | Medium-term for structural reform |
| **Poverty Reduction and Growth Trust (PRGT)** | Concessional loans for low-income countries |
| **Flexible Credit Line (FCL)** | Precautionary line for well-performing economies |

**India and IMF:** India is a founding member. India borrowed from IMF during **1981 and 1991 crises** (the 1991 crisis included the gold pledge episode). Today, India is a net creditor to the IMF.

## World Bank Group

**Established:** Bretton Woods Conference 1944; began operations June 1946
**HQ:** Washington D.C., USA
**President:** Ajay Banga (Indian-American; since June 2023 — **first person of Indian origin to lead the World Bank**)

The "World Bank Group" is actually **5 separate but related institutions:**

| Institution | Full Name | Established | What It Does |
|---|---|---|---|
| **IBRD** | International Bank for Reconstruction and Development | 1944 | Lends to **middle-income countries** at near-market rates |
| **IDA** | International Development Association | 1960 | **Concessional loans and grants** to the **poorest countries** (near-zero interest, 25–40 year repayment) |
| **IFC** | International Finance Corporation | 1956 | World Bank's **private sector arm** — invests in private companies in developing countries |
| **MIGA** | Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency | 1988 | Provides **political risk insurance** to investors/lenders in developing countries |
| **ICSID** | International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes | 1966 | **Arbitration** for disputes between foreign investors and host governments |

**"World Bank" (common usage)** = IBRD + IDA
**"World Bank Group"** = all 5 institutions

### IBRD vs IDA — Critical Distinction

| Feature | IBRD | IDA |
|---|---|---|
| **Borrowers** | Middle-income & creditworthy low-income | **Poorest countries** (per capita income below a threshold) |
| **Interest** | Near-market rates (low but not zero) | **Near-zero (0–1.25%)** or grants |
| **Repayment** | 15–20 years | **25–40 years** |
| **India's status** | India borrows from IBRD (partly graduated from IDA) | India was an IDA borrower until 2014 |

**Traditionally:** World Bank President is American (by convention since 1944; US nominates; rest vote). IMF Managing Director is European by same convention.

## ADB — Asian Development Bank

**Established:** 19 December 1966
**HQ:** Mandaluyong City, Manila, **Philippines**
**Members:** 68 members (regional + non-regional; includes USA, UK, Germany etc.)
**President:** Traditionally Japanese; current: Masato Kanda (Japanese, 2024)

**Largest shareholders:** Japan and USA — each hold approximately **15.6%** (tied as largest shareholders)

**Purpose:**
- Reduce poverty and promote sustainable development in **Asia and the Pacific**
- Finance infrastructure, energy, transport, agriculture, education, health

**India and ADB:** India is one of ADB's largest borrowers — funds roads, urban infrastructure, energy transition projects.

**ADB vs World Bank:** Both lend for development; ADB's mandate is specifically Asia-Pacific. ADB's President is always Japanese (convention); World Bank's President is always American (convention).

## Other Important Bodies

### UNCTAD
**UN Conference on Trade and Development**
- HQ: Geneva, Switzerland (same city as WTO)
- Part of the UN system; established 1964
- Advocates for developing countries in trade negotiations
- Publishes: **World Investment Report** (annual; tracks global FDI flows)

### WEF — World Economic Forum
- HQ: Cologny (near Geneva), Switzerland
- **Not a UN body** — a private foundation
- Famous for annual **Davos Summit** (January)
- Publishes: **Global Competitiveness Report**, **Global Gender Gap Report**, **Global Risks Report**

### NDB — New Development Bank (BRICS Bank)
- Established: 2014 under BRICS Fortaleza Declaration; operational from 2015
- HQ: Shanghai, China
- Members: BRICS nations (Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa) + new members
- President: Dilma Rousseff (Brazil; former President of Brazil; since 2023)
- Purpose: Fund infrastructure and sustainable development in member countries
- India's first NDB-funded project: Renewable energy projects

### G20 and India
- **G20:** 19 countries + European Union — represents ~85% of global GDP
- India held G20 Presidency: December 2022 – November 2023
- India G20 Summit: **New Delhi, September 9-10, 2023** — theme: "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" (One Earth, One Family, One Future)
- Landmark: African Union (AU) admitted as G20 member at New Delhi Summit

## IMF vs World Bank — The Most Tested Comparison

| Feature | IMF | World Bank (IBRD/IDA) |
|---|---|---|
| **Core purpose** | Monetary stability, BoP support | Development loans, poverty reduction |
| **Lending type** | Short-term; balance of payments | Long-term; development projects |
| **Head title** | **Managing Director** | **President** |
| **HQ** | Washington D.C. | Washington D.C. |
| **Founded** | 1945 (operations 1947) | 1944 (operations 1946) |
| **Convention** | Head is always **European** | Head is always **American** |
| **Key publication** | World Economic Outlook (WEO) | World Development Report (WDR) |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **WTO established 1 January 1995; replaced GATT (1948)** — GATT was not a formal organisation; WTO is.
2. **WTO HQ = Geneva, Switzerland** — NOT New York (that's UN General Assembly); NOT Washington DC (that's IMF/WB).
3. **WTO DG = Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala** — first African, first woman; since 2021. Name and nationality are tested.
4. **MFN (Most Favoured Nation) = trade benefit given to one member must go to ALL members** — the WTO's foundational non-discrimination principle.
5. **TRIPS = intellectual property; GATS = services; TRIMS = investment measures** — three acronyms, three different agreements under WTO.
6. **IMF HQ = Washington D.C.** (alongside World Bank); NOT Geneva (that's WTO/UNCTAD).
7. **IMF Managing Director = Kristalina Georgieva** (Bulgarian); by convention always European.
8. **SDR basket = USD + EUR + CNY + JPY + GBP** — China's Yuan added in 2016; 5 currencies total.
9. **IMF uses Managing Director; World Bank uses President** — title differs even though both based in Washington DC.
10. **IBRD = middle-income countries; IDA = poorest countries** — within the World Bank Group, this distinction is the most tested.
11. **World Bank Group = 5 institutions** — IBRD, IDA, IFC, MIGA, ICSID. "World Bank" (narrow) = IBRD + IDA.
12. **IFC = World Bank's private sector arm** — invests in/lends to private companies (not governments).
13. **ADB HQ = Manila, Philippines** — NOT Tokyo (Japan dominates shareholders but HQ is Philippines).
14. **ADB largest shareholders = Japan AND USA (tied at ~15.6% each)** — both tied; not Japan alone.
15. **Ajay Banga = first Indian-origin World Bank President** (since June 2023) — Indian-American; previously CEO of Mastercard.
16. **NDB (BRICS Bank) HQ = Shanghai, China** — established 2014; distinct from ADB (which is Manila-based and broader membership).
