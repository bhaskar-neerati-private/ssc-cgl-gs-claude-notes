# Taxation — Direct & Indirect Taxes, GST Structure & GST Council

## The Lifeblood of the State

Every public good you use — roads, schools, hospitals, the army — is funded by tax. Taxation is the mechanism by which the state converts private resources into public services. Without it, no government can function. India's tax system has two great branches: **direct taxes** (income tax, corporate tax — where the payer and the bearer are the same person) and **indirect taxes** (GST, customs — where the tax is collected from a business but ultimately borne by the consumer).

The single biggest transformation in India's tax history came at midnight on **30 June–1 July 2017**, when Parliament assembled in its Central Hall and the GST was launched — collapsing dozens of central and state taxes into a single, unified system. Understanding GST — its structure, its Council, its slabs, and what it excludes — is essential for SSC.

---

## Constitutional Foundation of Taxation

- **Article 265:** No tax shall be levied or collected except by authority of law. This is the bedrock — taxation requires a law.
- **Article 246 + 7th Schedule (Union/State Lists):** Parliament taxes what's on the Union List (income tax, customs, excise); State Legislatures tax what's on the State List (stamp duty, land revenue); SGST is concurrent.
- **101st Constitutional Amendment Act, 2016:** Made GST possible — inserted Article 246A (Parliament and State Legislatures both empowered to make laws on GST simultaneously) and Article 279A (established the GST Council).

---

## Direct Taxes

**Definition:** Tax where the **legal incidence (who pays) = economic incidence (who bears the burden)** — you pay it yourself; you can't pass it on to someone else.

**Administered by:** Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT), under the Ministry of Finance.

| Tax | Who Pays | Key Rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Income Tax** | Individuals, HUFs | Progressive slabs (5%–30% old regime; new regime up to 30%) |
| **Corporate Tax** | Companies | ~22% (domestic, new regime); 15% (new manufacturing companies) |
| **Capital Gains Tax** | Seller of assets (shares, property) | Short-term: slab rate; Long-term: 20% (with indexation) |
| **Securities Transaction Tax (STT)** | Stock market traders | Small % on equity transactions |

**Key features of direct taxes:**
- **Progressive:** As income rises, the tax rate rises — designed for redistribution
- **Wealth Tax abolished in 2015** (replaced partly by a surcharge on high incomes)
- **Dividend Distribution Tax (DDT) abolished in 2020** — dividends now taxable in hands of shareholders
- **TDS (Tax Deducted at Source):** Employer/bank deducts tax before paying salary/interest
- **Advance Tax:** Self-employed individuals whose annual tax liability exceeds ₹10,000 must pay in quarterly instalments

## Indirect Taxes

**Definition:** Tax where the **legal incidence ≠ economic incidence** — a business collects and pays the tax, but the burden is ultimately passed on to the consumer through higher prices.

**Administered by:** Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC), under the Ministry of Finance.

**Before GST (pre-2017):**
- **Central:** Central Excise Duty (on manufacture), Service Tax (on services), Central Sales Tax (CST, on inter-state sales)
- **State:** VAT/Sales Tax (on intra-state sales), Entry Tax, Luxury Tax, Entertainment Tax, Octroi
- **Problem:** Cascading effect — tax was levied on a value that already included earlier taxes ("tax on tax"), distorting prices. Multiple compliances for businesses operating across states.

**After GST (post-2017):**
Most indirect taxes folded into GST. What remains outside GST:
- Customs Duty (import/export — Centre levies)
- Petroleum products (petrol, diesel, crude oil, ATF, natural gas) — NOT under GST yet
- Alcohol for human consumption — States levy excise duty
- Electricity duty — States levy
- Stamp duty on property — States levy

## GST: The Midnight Tax Revolution

**Full name:** Goods and Services Tax
**Launched:** **1 July 2017** (midnight launch in Parliament's Central Hall — a rare special session)
**Constitutional basis:** 101st Constitutional Amendment Act, 2016

**The philosophy:** "One Nation, One Tax, One Market." Before GST, a truck crossing multiple state borders was stopped at each checkpost and taxed multiple times. GST eliminated inter-state tax barriers.

---

### GST Architecture: Dual GST

India's federal structure demanded a compromise — both Centre and States needed to tax consumption. The solution: **Dual GST** where both tax the same transaction simultaneously.

| Type | Full Form | Who Collects | When Applied |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CGST** | Central GST | Centre | Intra-state transactions (Centre's share) |
| **SGST** | State GST | State Government | Intra-state transactions (State's share) |
| **IGST** | Integrated GST | Centre (then allocated) | **Inter-state transactions** (replaces CGST+SGST) |
| **UTGST** | Union Territory GST | UT Administration | Transactions in UTs without legislature (Ladakh, D&NH & DD) |

**Example:** You buy a shirt in Delhi from a Delhi shop. The bill shows CGST 6% + SGST 6% = 12% total. If the shirt was made in Mumbai and sold in Delhi (inter-state), the supplier charges IGST 12% instead.

---

### GST Rate Slabs

| Rate | Category | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **0% (Exempt)** | Essential goods | Fresh vegetables, fruits, milk, eggs, unbranded rice/wheat, educational books, newspapers |
| **5%** | Common necessities | Edible oil, sugar, spices, tea, coffee, economy class air travel, life-saving drugs |
| **12%** | Standard goods | Computers, mobile phones, butter, cheese, processed food, business class air travel |
| **18%** | Majority of goods/services | IT services, financial services, AC restaurant meals, most manufactured goods |
| **28%** | Luxury & demerit goods | High-end cars, tobacco products, pan masala, cement, aerated drinks |
| **28% + Cess** | Sin/ultra-luxury | Cigarettes, SUVs, luxury cars — cess funds compensation to states |
| **Special: 3%** | Precious metals | Gold, silver, jewellery |
| **Special: 0.25%** | Diamonds | Rough diamonds |

**Note:** Most items are at 18% — this is the "standard" rate for services and many manufactured goods.

### Input Tax Credit (ITC): How GST Eliminates Cascading

The genius of GST is **Input Tax Credit**:

At each stage of production/distribution, a business can claim credit for the GST already paid on its inputs.

**Example chain:**
1. **Farmer** sells cotton → No GST (exempt)
2. **Mill** buys cotton, makes yarn → charges GST ₹100. Can claim ITC on cotton inputs.
3. **Weaver** buys yarn, makes fabric → charges GST ₹150. Claims ITC of ₹100 → net tax paid = ₹50.
4. **Retailer** buys fabric → charges GST ₹200. Claims ITC of ₹150 → net tax paid = ₹50.
5. **Consumer** pays the full ₹200 GST — it's collected along the chain but only net amounts are paid.

Result: Tax is only on the **value added** at each stage — not on the full price including previous taxes. Cascading eliminated.

### GST Council: The Constitutional Federalism Body

**Constitutional basis:** Article 279A (inserted by 101st Amendment)

**Composition:**
- **Chairperson:** Union Finance Minister
- **Vice-Chairperson:** Elected from among state Finance Ministers
- **Members:** Finance Ministers of all 28 States + Finance Ministers of UTs with legislatures (Delhi, Puducherry, J&K)

**Voting Structure:**
- Vote of Central Government = **1/3 of total votes cast**
- Votes of all State Governments together = **2/3 of total votes cast**
- A decision requires a **3/4 majority of votes cast**

This structure gives states collective power (2/3 of votes) but also ensures the Centre (1/3) can block any decision — a carefully calibrated federal balance.

**What the GST Council decides:**
- GST rates for specific goods/services
- Exemptions and threshold limits
- Registration thresholds
- Rules for composition scheme
- States' compensation mechanism

---

### Other GST Concepts

**GST Network (GSTN):**
- The IT infrastructure backbone of GST
- A not-for-profit company: Government holds 49%; non-government financial institutions hold 51%
- Processes all GST returns, payments, and refunds

**Registration Threshold:**
- Businesses with turnover > **₹40 lakh** (goods) / **₹20 lakh** (services) must register for GST
- For North-Eastern and hilly states (special category): threshold is ₹10 lakh

**Composition Scheme:**
- For small businesses with turnover up to ₹1.5 crore
- Pay a flat GST rate (1%–6%) without the full compliance burden
- Cannot claim Input Tax Credit
- Cannot make inter-state supplies

**HSN Code:**
Harmonised System of Nomenclature — international 6-digit code system for classifying goods, used in GST invoices for standardisation.

## Revenue Sharing: Centre vs States

Before GST, indirect taxes were either purely Centre's or purely States'. GST changed this:

- **CGST** stays with Centre
- **SGST** stays with State where consumption happens
- **IGST** collected by Centre → allocated between Centre and consuming state

**For direct taxes:** The Finance Commission (Article 280) recommends the share of the Centre's divisible tax pool to be given to states.
- 14th Finance Commission: States get **42%** of divisible pool
- 15th Finance Commission: States get **41%** (reduced by 1% due to J&K bifurcation into two UTs)

**Taxes exclusively for Centre (not shared):** Customs Duty, Surcharges (e.g., health and education cess), Cesses

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **GST launched 1 July 2017** — at midnight, in Parliament's Central Hall, a rare midnight session.
2. **101st Constitutional Amendment Act, 2016** enabled GST — inserted Article 246A (dual power) and Article 279A (GST Council).
3. **CBDT administers direct taxes; CBIC administers indirect taxes** — both under Ministry of Finance but separate boards.
4. **Indirect taxes are regressive** — same rate on rich and poor alike; direct taxes are progressive.
5. **CGST + SGST** for intra-state; **IGST** for inter-state — not CGST + IGST or any other combination.
6. **GST Council Chair = Union Finance Minister** — NOT the Prime Minister or President.
7. **GST Council voting: Centre = 1/3; States collectively = 2/3; 3/4 majority needed** — all three numbers tested together.
8. **Petroleum products, alcohol, electricity, stamp duty are OUTSIDE GST** — states still levy their own taxes; this is the most common "what is not under GST" question.
9. **Gold/silver GST rate = 3%; Diamond rate = 0.25%** — special rates, not part of the main slabs.
10. **28% + Cess = highest GST slab** — for cigarettes, luxury cars, SUVs, aerated drinks; cess compensates states.
11. **GST threshold: ₹40 lakh (goods), ₹20 lakh (services), ₹10 lakh (NE/hilly states)** — three different thresholds.
12. **Input Tax Credit eliminates cascading** — this is the key economic justification for GST.
13. **GSTN = 49% government + 51% private financial institutions** — not-for-profit IT body; private ≠ majority means private banks/institutions, not profit motive.
14. **Wealth Tax abolished 2015; DDT abolished 2020** — both were direct taxes now gone.
15. **15th Finance Commission gave states 41% of divisible pool** (14th FC gave 42%) — reduced due to J&K becoming two UTs.
16. **Composition Scheme**: turnover < ₹1.5 crore; flat tax; NO ITC allowed; NO inter-state supply — all four conditions matter.
