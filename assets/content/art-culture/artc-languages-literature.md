# Classical Languages, Scripts & Sanskrit Literature

## The Language That Refused to Die

In 1786, in a Calcutta courtroom, a British judge named Sir William Jones delivered a lecture that changed the world. He had noticed, in his study of Sanskrit, that the word for "father" in Sanskrit was "pitar" — almost identical to Latin "pater" and Greek "patēr." The numbers, the verb conjugations, the grammatical structures — all rhymed. Jones proposed that Sanskrit, Latin, Greek, and Gothic were all descended from a common ancestor — a proto-language now called **Proto-Indo-European**. The lecture launched the entire field of comparative linguistics. Sanskrit wasn't a dead language to Jones; it was a Rosetta Stone for human history. When SSC asks you about classical language status or the Vedas, remember: you're dealing with a linguistic tradition that unlocked the family tree of nearly half of all human languages.

## Classical Language Status in India

The Government of India grants **Classical Language** status to languages that meet these criteria:
- High antiquity of early texts (1,500–2,000 years)
- A body of ancient literature considered a valuable heritage
- Not borrowed from another language community
- Distinct from its modern form

### Current Classical Languages (6 as of 2024)

| Language | Year Granted | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Tamil** | 2004 | **First** to receive classical status; oldest Dravidian literature; Sangam literature (3rd c. BCE – 3rd c. CE) |
| **Sanskrit** | 2005 | Language of Vedas, epics; mother of many North Indian languages |
| **Telugu** | 2008 | Earliest literature: 11th century (Nannaya's Mahabharata translation) |
| **Kannada** | 2008 | Oldest Dravidian script; Kavirajamarga (9th c.) earliest text |
| **Malayalam** | 2013 | Developed from Tamil-Sanskrit blend; Ramacharitam (12th c.) early text |
| **Odia** | 2014 | One of first Indic languages with distinct script; Sarala Das's Mahabharata |
| **Marathi** | 2024 | Recently added (October 2024) |
| **Pali** | 2024 | Language of Theravada Buddhist texts; spoken in ancient Magadha |
| **Prakrit** | 2024 | Middle Indic language; used in Jain texts and Ashoka's edicts |
| **Assamese** | 2024 | Oldest Assamese text: Assam's Charyapada; Northeast India |

> **Note**: As of late 2024, the Indian government announced additional classical languages. For SSC prep, the core six (Tamil, Sanskrit, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Odia) are most examined; the 2024 additions may appear in recent exams.

## Indian Scripts

### Brahmi Script
- **Origin**: One of the oldest writing systems in South Asia; 3rd century BCE or earlier
- Used by Ashoka in his edicts
- **Parent of all modern Indian scripts** (Devanagari, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, Bengali, Gujarati, Punjabi, Sinhala, Tibetan, Thai, Khmer, etc.)
- Deciphered by **James Prinsep** (British scholar) in 1837

### Kharosthi Script
- Right-to-left script; used in northwest India (present-day Pakistan/Afghanistan) in 3rd century BCE
- Used in some Ashoka edicts in northwestern region
- Died out unlike Brahmi; not parent of modern scripts

### Devanagari
- Used for: Sanskrit, Hindi, Marathi, Nepali
- Derived from Brahmi via northern medieval scripts

### Tamil Script
- Ancient; Brahmi-derived; distinct from North Indian scripts; evolved separately; earliest inscriptions 3rd–1st century BCE

## Sanskrit Literature

### The Vedas (1,500 – 1,200 BCE)
The oldest surviving literary tradition in any Indo-European language. Four Vedas:

| Veda | Content | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Rigveda** | Hymns (suktas) addressed to gods | Oldest Veda; oldest known text in any Indo-European language; 1,028 hymns |
| **Samaveda** | Melodies; musical notation for rituals | Most songs derived from Rigveda; origin of Indian classical music |
| **Yajurveda** | Prose formulas for rituals | Rituals and sacrifices; two versions: Krishna and Shukla Yajurveda |
| **Atharvaveda** | Spells, charms, philosophical hymns | More secular content; later addition to Vedic canon |

### Vedic Auxiliary Texts
- **Brahmanas**: Prose explanations of Vedic rituals
- **Aranyakas**: Forest texts; transitional between ritual and philosophy
- **Upanishads**: Philosophical texts; 108 principal Upanishads; core concepts: Brahman, Atman, Maya, Karma, Moksha; "Tat tvam asi" (That thou art)

### Epics (Itihasas)

| Epic | Author | Key Facts |
|---|---|---|
| **Ramayana** | **Valmiki** ("Adi Kavi" — first poet); also called "Adi Kavya" (first poem) | 24,000 shlokas; 7 Kandas; story of Rama |
| **Mahabharata** | **Vyasa** (also called Krishna Dvaipayana); dictated to Ganesha | Longest epic in the world (100,000+ shlokas); includes Bhagavad Gita |

### Puranas
- 18 Mahapuranas; **Bhagavata Purana** most popular (Krishna devotion)
- **Agni Purana**: Encyclopaedic; covers art, architecture, politics

### Classical Sanskrit Literature (2nd–7th century CE)

| Author | Work | Genre |
|---|---|---|
| **Kalidasa** | Abhijnanashakuntalam, Raghuvamsha, Kumarasambhava, Meghadutam, Vikramorvasiyam, Malavikagnimitram | Poet and dramatist; Gupta period; "Indian Shakespeare" |
| **Vishakhadatta** | Mudrarakshasa, Devichandraguptam | Drama; political themes |
| **Shudraka** | Mricchakatika (Little Clay Cart) | Drama; social realism; unusual secular themes |
| **Bharavi** | Kiratarjuniya | Mahakavya (epic poem) |
| **Magha** | Shishupalavadha | Mahakavya |
| **Dandin** | Dasakumaracharita, Kavyadarsha | Prose fiction + literary theory |
| **Chandragupta Maurya's Arthashastra** | **Kautilya/Chanakya** | Political treatise; statecraft |
| **Ashtadhyayi** | **Panini** | Grammar of Sanskrit; ~500 BCE; most analysed grammar text in world |

### Pali and Buddhist Literature
- **Tripitaka** (Three Baskets): Buddhist canon in Pali
  - Vinaya Pitaka (monastic rules), Sutta Pitaka (Buddha's teachings), Abhidhamma Pitaka (philosophical)
- **Dhammapada**: Collection of Buddha's sayings; 423 verses; most read Buddhist text
- **Jataka Tales**: Stories of Buddha's previous lives; 547 stories

### Sangam Literature (Tamil)
- Period: **3rd century BCE – 3rd century CE**
- Named after legendary **Sangam** (academy of poets) in Madurai
- **Two major collections**:
  - **Ettuttogai** (Eight Anthologies): Akam (love) and Puram (war/public)
  - **Pattupppattu** (Ten Idylls)
- **Five landscapes (Tinai)**: Kurinji (hills, union), Mullai (forest, patience), Marudam (farmland, infidelity), Neytal (coast, separation), Palai (desert, separation)
- Major works: **Tolkappiyam** (oldest Tamil grammar, before BCE), **Tirukkural** (Thiruvalluvar), **Silappatikaram** and **Manimekalai** (twin Tamil epics of Sangam era)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Tamil** was the **first** language to receive Classical Language status in India in **2004**.
2. **Kalidasa** = Gupta period; greatest Sanskrit poet and playwright; "Shakuntala" = most famous play; called "Indian Shakespeare."
3. **Valmiki** = "Adi Kavi" (first poet); **Ramayana** = "Adi Kavya" (first poem); 24,000 shlokas.
4. **Mahabharata** = **Vyasa** (dictated to Ganesha); longest epic in world (100,000+ shlokas — also counts Bhagavad Gita).
5. **Ashtadhyayi** = **Panini**'s Sanskrit grammar (~500 BCE); not to be confused with Kautilya's Arthashastra.
6. **Arthashastra** = **Kautilya/Chanakya**; statecraft; Maurya period.
7. **Rigveda** = oldest Veda = oldest known Indo-European text; 1,028 hymns.
8. **Samaveda** = musical/melodic Veda; most songs borrowed from Rigveda.
9. **Brahmi** deciphered by **James Prinsep** in 1837 — this is a frequently tested historical fact.
10. **Kharosthi** = right-to-left; northwest India; did NOT survive into modern scripts (Brahmi did).
11. **Sangam literature** = 3rd c. BCE – 3rd c. CE; Tamil; five Tinai landscapes; Silappatikaram is the most famous Sangam epic.
12. **Tirukkural** (Thiruvalluvar) = Tamil ethical text; called "Tamil Bible"; 1,330 couplets.
13. **Tripitaka** = Buddhist canon in Pali; Three Baskets; Dhammapada is part of Sutta Pitaka.
14. **Upanishads** = 108 principal; core philosophy; part of Vedic literature (Vedanta).
15. **Mricchakatika** (Shudraka) = secular Sanskrit drama; unusual because it features a courtesan as a key character.
16. **Classical language status** requires high antiquity + distinct heritage + not borrowed — only 6 (core) + recent 2024 additions; do not mistake all Scheduled Languages as Classical Languages.
