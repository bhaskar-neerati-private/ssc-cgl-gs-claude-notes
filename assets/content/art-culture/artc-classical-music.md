# Classical Music — Hindustani & Carnatic

## The Night Tansen Charmed Akbar

The legend says Tansen, the greatest musician of Mughal court, could light lamps by singing Raga Deepak and bring rain by singing Raga Megh Malhar. Akbar was so besotted that he made Tansen one of the Navaratnas — the Nine Gems of his court. Whether the legends are true or not, Tansen's presence in Gwalior and Agra shaped the entire trajectory of Hindustani classical music: the raga system crystallised, the gharana tradition took root, and North India's music became formally distinct from its South Indian counterpart. SSC loves asking which music system originates where, who the founding figures were, and which instrument belongs to which tradition.

India's classical music split into two great schools after the 12th-century Turkic invasions: **Hindustani** in the North (absorbed Persian, Arabic, Turkish elements) and **Carnatic** in the South (remained closer to ancient Vedic roots). Both rest on two shared foundations: **Raga** (melodic framework) and **Tala** (rhythmic cycle).

## Hindustani vs Carnatic — Core Differences

| Feature | Hindustani Classical | Carnatic Classical |
|---|---|---|
| **Region** | North India | South India (TN, AP, Karnataka, Kerala) |
| **Roots** | Vedic + Persian/Arabic influence | Purely Indian/Vedic |
| **Improvisation** | Heavy emphasis on improvisation (alap) | More structured compositions |
| **Vocal form** | Khayal, Dhrupad, Thumri, Tappa | Kriti, Varnam, Javali, Padam |
| **Scale** | 72 Melakarta ragas (theoretical basis) | 10 Thaats (parent scales in Hindustani) |
| **Key instrument** | Sitar, Sarod, Tabla, Sarangi | Veena, Mridangam, Violin, Ghatam |
| **Festival form** | Khayal the dominant form today | Kriti (composition by saint-composers) |
| **Founding figure** | Amir Khusrau (14th c.), Tansen | Purandaradasa (15th c.), Trinity |

## Hindustani Classical Music in Detail

### Key Vocal Forms

- **Dhrupad**: Oldest, purest form; no film influence; 4 sections (Sthayi, Antara, Sanchari, Abhog); Dagar tradition, Darbhanga tradition
- **Khayal**: Main form today; introduced by Amir Khusrau, developed by Sultan Hussain Sharqi; Vilambit (slow) and Drut (fast) versions
- **Thumri**: Semi-classical; romantic/devotional; associated with Kathak dance; Lucknow and Benaras styles
- **Tappa**: Fast, ornamental style; originated in camel drivers' songs of Punjab; developed by Shori Mian
- **Chaiti, Kajri, Hori**: Seasonal/folk-influenced semi-classical forms

### Raga System

- **Raga**: Not just a scale but a framework including characteristic phrases, ascending (Aroh) and descending (Avaroh) rules, time of day/season for performance, mood (rasa)
- **10 Thaats** (parent scales in Hindustani): Bilawal, Kafi, Bhairavi, Bhairav, Kalyan, Marwa, Poorvi, Todi, Asawari, Khamaj
- **Raga Bhairav**: Morning raga. **Raga Yaman**: Evening. **Raga Bhairavi**: End of concert raga. **Raga Deepak** and **Raga Megh Malhar**: Tansen legends.

### Gharana System (Schools of Music)

| Gharana | Location | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| Gwalior | Madhya Pradesh | Oldest; clear diction; direct style |
| Agra | Uttar Pradesh | Dhrupad influence; heavy style |
| Jaipur-Atrauli | Rajasthan | Complex patterns; Alladiya Khan tradition |
| Kirana | UP/Karnataka border | Slow, meditative; Bhimsen Joshi |
| Patiala | Punjab | Thumri; Bade Ghulam Ali Khan |

## Carnatic Classical Music in Detail

### The Carnatic Trinity (18th century)

These three saint-composers are the pillars of Carnatic music:

| Composer | Period | Key Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Tyagaraja** (Tiruvarur) | 1767–1847 | 600+ kritis; devotion to Lord Rama; in Telugu mostly; Pancharatna Kritis most famous |
| **Muthuswami Dikshitar** | 1775–1835 | Sanskrit compositions; Mantra music; kritis dedicated to all Shiva temples of Tamil Nadu |
| **Syama Sastri** | 1762–1827 | Compositions in Telugu and Sanskrit; devotion to Goddess Bangaru Kamakshi |

- **Purandaradasa** (15th century, Karnataka): "Father of Carnatic Music"; standardised music curriculum; composed in Kannada and Telugu

### Key Carnatic Vocal Forms

- **Kriti**: Main composition form; Pallavi, Anupallavi, Charanam structure
- **Varnam**: Training piece; full display of raga
- **Javali, Padam**: Love/devotional songs in semi-classical style
- **Tillana**: Counterpart of Hindustani Tarana; rhythmic piece

### Melakarta System

- **72 Melakarta ragas**: Parent scales in Carnatic music (vs 10 Thaats in Hindustani)
- Highly mathematical system worked out by Venkatamakhi (17th century)
- All 72 derived from 7 swaras (notes): Sa, Ri, Ga, Ma, Pa, Da, Ni

## Key Amir Khusrau (1253–1325)

- Disciple of Sufi saint Nizamuddin Auliya
- Credited with: creating **Khayal** form, **Qawwali**, **Tarana**, **Tabla** (splitting the two-headed pakhawaj), and **Sitar** (mixing Indian vina with Persian setar)
- Called "Parrot of India" (Tuti-e-Hind)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Hindustani**: 10 Thaats. **Carnatic**: 72 Melakarta ragas. Don't mix them up.
2. **Amir Khusrau** is credited with **Khayal, Qawwali, Tarana, Sitar, and Tabla** — SSC loves asking "who invented tabla."
3. **Carnatic Trinity**: Tyagaraja, Muthuswami Dikshitar, Syama Sastri — not Purandaradasa (he is "Father of Carnatic Music").
4. **Bismillah Khan** → Shehnai; **Zakir Hussain** → Tabla; **Ravi Shankar** → Sitar — three most tested pairings.
5. **Santoor** is from **Kashmir** — Shivkumar Sharma popularised it in Hindustani classical.
6. **Sarod has no frets**; Sitar has frets — this trick question appears in SSC.
7. **Dhrupad** is the OLDEST form of Hindustani music, not Khayal.
8. **Tansen** = Hindustani; associated with **Gwalior** and **Akbar's court** — Navaratna.
9. **Purandaradasa** is "Father of Carnatic Music" from **Karnataka** — 15th century, not 18th century Trinity.
10. **Pakhawaj** is NOT the same as Tabla — Pakhawaj accompanies Dhrupad (older), Tabla accompanies Khayal.
11. **Nadaswaram** (not Shehnai) is the traditional South Indian temple wind instrument.
12. **Ghatam** = clay pot percussion = unique to Carnatic music.
13. **Sangeet Natak Akademi** established **1952** — same year Republic took shape; separate from Lalit Kala Akademi (visual arts) and Sahitya Akademi (literature), also both 1954.
14. **Mridangam** = two-headed drum, barrel-shaped = Carnatic. Not to be confused with Tabla (two separate drums).
15. **Khayal** dominates Hindustani today; **Kriti** dominates Carnatic — these are the main forms in each tradition.
16. **Pancharatna Kritis** = five jewel compositions = by **Tyagaraja** = most celebrated compositions in Carnatic music.
