# Festivals, Fairs & Cultural Celebrations

## The Fire That Divides the Year

For the people of the Indus Valley, the winter solstice was a crisis. Nights were lengthening, the sun was retreating, crops could freeze. Then, on the shortest day, the priests lit fires. The fires spread through the village. People danced and feasted. And then — day by day — the sun began returning. The fires had "won." Three thousand years later, people across India still light fires in January: Lohri in Punjab, Makar Sankranti across the country, Bhogi in Tamil Nadu, Uttarayan in Gujarat. They no longer fear the sun retreating — but the fire persists, because festivals don't just mark time; they transmit meaning across generations. SSC tests festivals constantly, particularly the state associations, the calendar (month/season), and the related deity or purpose.

## Major National/Pan-India Festivals

| Festival | Calendar | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Diwali** | Kartik Amavasya (Oct–Nov) | Festival of lights; Rama's return to Ayodhya; Lakshmi worship; Kali Puja in Bengal; new year for merchants |
| **Holi** | Phalguna Purnima (Feb–Mar) | Festival of colours; Holika Dahan (fire burning) eve; Radha-Krishna celebration; spring arrival; Lathmar Holi in Mathura |
| **Dussehra / Vijayadashami** | Ashwina Dashami (Sept–Oct) | Victory of Rama over Ravana (North); Durga's victory over Mahishasura (Bengal = Durga Puja); weapon/tool worship; Mysore Dasara = most famous |
| **Navratri** | Ashwina (9 nights) | Nine forms of Goddess Durga; Garba in Gujarat; Ramlila begins; ends with Vijayadashami |
| **Makar Sankranti** | January 14 | Sun enters Capricorn; kite flying (Gujarat = Uttarayan); sesame/til sweets; harvest festival; Pongal equivalent in South |
| **Raksha Bandhan** | Shravana Purnima (Aug) | Sibling bond; sister ties rakhi on brother's wrist |
| **Janmashtami** | Bhadrapada Krishna Ashtami (Aug) | Krishna's birth; fasting, midnight puja; Dahi Handi in Maharashtra |
| **Mahashivaratri** | Phalguna Krishna Chaturdashi (Feb–Mar) | Shiva's favourite night; fasting; night-long vigil |
| **Ganesh Chaturthi** | Bhadrapada Shukla Chaturthi (Aug–Sept) | Ganesha's birthday; 10-day festival; Mumbai (Bal Gangadhar Tilak popularised public celebration in 1893) |
| **Krishna Janmashtami** | Same as Janmashtami | Mathura, Vrindavan most famous celebrations |
| **Eid-ul-Fitr** | After Ramadan | End of Ramadan fasting; communal prayer; feasting |
| **Eid-ul-Adha (Bakrid)** | 10th Zilhijja | Ibrahim's sacrifice; animal sacrifice; Hajj connection |
| **Christmas** | December 25 | Jesus Christ's birth; Goa = most elaborately celebrated in India |
| **Guru Nanak Jayanti (Gurpurab)** | Kartik Purnima | Sikh; birthday of Guru Nanak; langar; Amritsar Golden Temple illuminated |
| **Baisakhi** | April 13/14 | Sikh new year; Khalsa Panth foundation (1699); harvest in Punjab; Bhangra |

## Major Regional Festivals

| Festival | State | When | Significance |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Onam** | Kerala | Aug–Sept (Chingam) | Harvest; boat races (Vallamkali); Mahabali's annual return; floral carpet (Pookalam); 10-day festival |
| **Pongal** | Tamil Nadu | January 14–17 | Harvest; 4-day festival — Bhogi (1st), Thai Pongal (2nd, main), Mattu Pongal (cattle worship, 3rd), Kaanum Pongal (4th); "Pongalo Pongal!" — rice boiling ritual |
| **Bihu** | Assam | April (Rongali), Oct (Kongali), Jan (Bhogali) | Three forms; Rongali = spring, most celebrated; Magh Bihu = harvest feast |
| **Durga Puja** | West Bengal | Ashwina (Oct) | 5-day; elaborate idol; UNESCO ICH (2021); cultural performances |
| **Kali Puja** | West Bengal | Diwali night | Goddess Kali; fire crackers on same night as Diwali |
| **Losar** | Ladakh, Sikkim, Arunachal | Jan–Feb | Tibetan New Year; Buddhist celebration; Hemis festival (Ladakh, in June) |
| **Hemis Festival** | Ladakh | June | Celebrates birth of Guru Padmasambhava; Hemis Monastery; masked dances (Cham); biggest in Ladakh |
| **Lohri** | Punjab, Haryana | January 13 | Winter solstice celebration (eve of Makar Sankranti); bonfire; sesame, popcorn, rewari; Bhangra dances |
| **Basant Panchami** | North India | Magh Shukla 5th (Jan–Feb) | Goddess Saraswati; yellow worn; spring beginning; kite flying in some regions |
| **Ram Navami** | March–April | Rama's birthday; Ayodhya; fasting; temple celebration |
| **Ugadi** | Andhra, Telangana, Karnataka | March–April | New year for Deccan; Panchangam (almanac) recited; neem leaves + jaggery + tamarind (mixed flavours = life's variety) |
| **Gudi Padwa** | Maharashtra | March–April | Marathi new year; same day as Ugadi; Gudi (decorated bamboo) erected outside home |
| **Vishu** | Kerala | April 14 | Malayalam new year; "Vishukkani" — auspicious sight first thing in morning |
| **Baisakhi** | Punjab | April 13–14 | Sikh new year + harvest |
| **Rath Yatra** | Odisha (Puri) | June–July (Ashadha Shukla 2nd) | Lord Jagannath's chariot procession; **world's largest chariot festival**; 3 chariots: Jagannath (Nandighosa), Balabhadra (Taladhwaja), Subhadra (Darpadalana) |
| **Pushkar Fair (Camel Fair)** | Rajasthan | November | World's largest camel fair; Kartik Purnima; Brahma Temple at Pushkar |
| **Kumbh Mela** | Prayagraj, Haridwar, Nashik, Ujjain | Every 3 years (rotation); Maha Kumbh every 12 years at Prayagraj | Largest religious gathering on Earth; UNESCO ICH (2017); based on Jupiter's cycle |
| **Thrissur Pooram** | Kerala (Thrissur) | April–May (Medam) | Largest temple festival in Kerala; two groups of elephants (Thiruvambadi vs Paramekkavu) face off; fireworks |
| **Chapchar Kut** | Mizoram | March | Post-clearing-jungle festival; traditional music, dance |
| **Hornbill Festival** | Nagaland (Kohima) | December 1–10 | Cultural showcase of all Naga tribes; "Festival of Festivals"; named after the Hornbill bird (revered in Naga culture) |
| **Wangala** | Meghalaya | November | Garo tribe harvest festival; "100 Drums Festival"; dance to honour Sun deity Saljong |
| **Losoong / Namsoong** | Sikkim | December | Sikkimese New Year; Lepcha and Bhutia communities; archery contests |
| **Chapchar Kut** | Mizoram | March | Spring festival; bamboo dance (Cheraw) performed |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Onam** = Kerala harvest festival; king **Mahabali's** return; Pookalam (floral carpet); **Vallamkali** (boat race).
2. **Pongal** is a 4-day festival; **Mattu Pongal** (3rd day) = cattle worship — SSC asks what day.
3. **Rath Yatra** = Puri, Odisha; **Ashadha** month; 3 chariots; Jagannath's chariot = **Nandighosa**.
4. **Pushkar Fair** = world's largest **camel** fair; Kartik Purnima; Brahma Temple nearby (only major Brahma temple).
5. **Sonepur Fair** (Bihar) = **largest animal fair in Asia** (not Pushkar — Pushkar is camel fair; Sonepur includes elephants).
6. **Kumbh Mela** = UNESCO ICH **2017**; largest gathering on Earth; Maha Kumbh every 12 years at **Prayagraj**.
7. **Durga Puja** = UNESCO ICH **2021** — very recent, likely tested.
8. **Hornbill Festival** = Nagaland, **December 1–10** = "Festival of Festivals" = all Naga tribes together.
9. **Mysore Dasara** = Karnataka's most famous celebration of Vijayadashami; state festival.
10. **Ganesh Chaturthi** public celebration = popularised by **Bal Gangadhar Tilak** in **1893** in Pune.
11. **Lohri** = January 13 (day before Makar Sankranti); Punjab/Haryana; bonfire + sesame = winter festival.
12. **Ugadi** = new year for Andhra/Telangana/Karnataka; **Gudi Padwa** = new year for Maharashtra — SAME day, different names.
13. **Bihu**: Rongali (April, spring), Kongali (October, autumn, quiet), Bhogali/Magh (January, harvest feast).
14. **Wangala Festival** = Meghalaya, **Garo tribe**, "100 Drums Festival" — not to be confused with Hornbill (Nagaland).
15. **Hemis Festival** = Ladakh, June, largest festival in Ladakh — celebrates Guru Padmasambhava's birth.
16. **Baisakhi** = April 13–14 = BOTH Sikh New Year AND Khalsa Panth foundation (1699 by Guru Gobind Singh) — dual significance always tested.
