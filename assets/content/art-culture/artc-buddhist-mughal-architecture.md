# Buddhist, Jain, Sultani & Mughal Architecture

## The Emperor Who Built in Grief

In 1631, Mumtaz Mahal died giving birth to their fourteenth child. Shah Jahan, the Mughal emperor who had already built the Peacock Throne and filled his palace with the most costly jewels on earth, was inconsolable. He ordered the construction of a mausoleum that would capture eternal beauty in marble. Twenty-two years, 20,000 workers, and 1,000 elephants later, the Taj Mahal stood complete. When the Venetian traveller Niccolò Manucci saw it, he wrote: "I never saw anything more magnificent." But the Taj was not created in a vacuum — it was the culmination of centuries of Indian architectural evolution that began with simple earthen Buddhist stupas and passed through Persian domes and pointy arches before arriving at the perfect white marble dome of Agra. To understand Mughal architecture, you must trace the full journey.

## Buddhist Architecture

### Stupa

The fundamental Buddhist monument — a hemispherical dome over relics of the Buddha or Buddhist monks.

**Anatomy of a Stupa**:
- **Anda**: Hemispherical mound (main dome body)
- **Medhi**: Raised terrace/platform around the anda
- **Harmika**: Square railing atop the anda (symbolises heaven)
- **Yasti/Chatravali**: Central post topped with parasols (umbrella tiers); represents the Cosmic Axis
- **Torana**: Carved gateway (4 gateways at 4 cardinal points)
- **Vedika**: Outer railing

**Major Stupas**:

| Stupa | Location | Key Facts |
|---|---|---|
| **Sanchi Stupa** | Madhya Pradesh | Originally built by **Ashoka** (250 BCE); enlarged in 1st c. CE; **UNESCO WHS** (1989); finest toranas; stories of Jataka tales carved on gateways |
| **Amaravati Stupa** | Andhra Pradesh | 2nd–3rd century CE; marble casing; Satavahana dynasty; now partially dismantled; sculptures in Chennai museum |
| **Dhamek Stupa** | Sarnath, UP | Marks spot where Buddha gave **first sermon** (Dhammachakrapravartana); 3rd century BCE Ashoka, expanded later |
| **Bharhut Stupa** | Satna, MP | Sunga dynasty (2nd c. BCE); sculpture panels now in Indian Museum, Kolkata |
| **Nalanda Monastery** | Bihar | Buddhist university complex; UNESCO WHS; 5th–12th century; Pala period |
| **Bodh Gaya** | Bihar | Where Buddha attained Enlightenment; **Mahabodhi Temple** = UNESCO WHS (2002); Vajrasana (diamond throne) |

### Rock-Cut Buddhist Caves

- **Chaitya**: Prayer hall with stupa at one end; nave and aisles; apsidal shape
- **Vihara**: Monastery; rooms for monks around a central hall

Key sites: **Ajanta, Ellora** (Maharashtra), **Karla, Bhaja** (Maharashtra), **Nasik, Junnar** (Maharashtra), **Kanheri** (Mumbai)

**Karla Caves** (Maharashtra): Largest rock-cut chaitya in India

## Jain Architecture

- Jain temples emphasise white marble and intricate internal carving
- **Mount Abu (Rajasthan)**: **Dilwara Temples** — Vimal Vasahi (1031 CE, Chalukya Vikramaditya I) and Luna Vasahi (1230 CE); white marble; intricate ceiling rosettes (no external decoration; all beauty is interior)
- **Shatrunjaya Hill (Palitana, Gujarat)**: 900+ Jain temples; holiest Jain pilgrimage site
- **Ranakpur Jain Temple** (Rajasthan): 29 halls, 80 domes, 1444 pillars, no pillar identical; Adinath Temple
- **Gomateshwara (Bahubali) Statue**: Shravanabelagola, Karnataka; 57-foot monolithic statue; **Mahamastakabhisheka** (grand anointing) every 12 years

## Sultani / Delhi Sultanate Architecture

The Delhi Sultanate (1206–1526) introduced a new architectural vocabulary to India: pointed arches, domes, minarets, geometric decoration, and Arabic calligraphy — replacing the sculptural richness of Hindu temples.

### Key Terms
- **Trabeate**: Post-and-lintel construction (Hindu/Buddhist) — no arch, no dome
- **Arcuate**: Arch-and-dome construction (Islamic) — introduced by Delhi Sultanate
- **True arch**: Self-supporting semicircular arch using keystones
- **Corbelled arch**: Not a true arch; used in early Indian mosques adapted from local stone

### Major Structures

| Monument | Dynasty | Key Facts |
|---|---|---|
| **Qutb Minar** | Slave Dynasty (Qutbuddin Aibak began, Iltutmish completed) | Tallest stone minaret in India (72.5m); UNESCO WHS; 5 storeys; Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque adjacent |
| **Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque** | Slave Dynasty | "Might of Islam"; first mosque in India; built using columns from 27 Hindu/Jain temples |
| **Iron Pillar** | Gupta period (4th c. CE) | Inside Qutb complex but NOT Sultanate; testament to Gupta metallurgy; rust-free for 1600 years |
| **Alai Darwaza** | Alauddin Khalji (1311 CE) | First true arch and true dome in Indian subcontinent; red sandstone |
| **Iltutmish Tomb** | Iltutmish | First decorated tomb in India; arabesque carving |
| **Balban's Tomb** | Balban | First true arch used in India (debate — vs Alai Darwaza) |
| **Tughlaqabad Fort** | Ghiyasuddin Tughlaq | Rough construction; sloping walls (batter) = Tughlaq feature |
| **Firoz Shah Kotla** | Firoz Shah Tughlaq | Contains Ashoka Pillar brought from Meerut |
| **Lodi Gardens / Tombs** | Lodi dynasty | Octagonal tombs; double domes; transitional to Mughal |

### Tughlaq Architecture Features
- Battered walls (sloping inward)
- Grey stone / rough construction
- Austere style (no decorative carvings compared to Khalji)
- Mix of Hindu and Muslim elements

## Mughal Architecture

### Evolution Through Emperors

| Emperor | Style | Key Monuments |
|---|---|---|
| **Babur** | No major surviving monuments; laid gardens (charbagh) | — |
| **Humayun** | Persian influence; transition period | Humayun's Tomb = first Mughal garden tomb; UNESCO WHS |
| **Akbar** | Red sandstone; blend of Hindu and Islamic; **syncretic** style | Fatehpur Sikri, Agra Fort, Buland Darwaza |
| **Jahangir** | Refined decoration; garden-builder | Shalimar Garden (Srinagar), Itmad-ud-Daulah |
| **Shah Jahan** | White marble; perfection; Indo-Islamic apex | Taj Mahal, Red Fort, Jama Masjid Delhi |
| **Aurangzeb** | Austere; decline of grand patronage | Bibi ka Maqbara (Aurangabad) — "Poor man's Taj" |

### Key Mughal Monuments

| Monument | Location | Key Facts |
|---|---|---|
| **Humayun's Tomb** | Delhi | First complete Mughal garden tomb; built by **Haji Begum** (wife); UNESCO WHS (1993); template for Taj |
| **Fatehpur Sikri** | UP | Built by Akbar; capital 1571–1585; abandoned (water shortage); UNESCO WHS; includes Buland Darwaza, Jodha Bai's Palace, Diwan-i-Khas |
| **Buland Darwaza** | Fatehpur Sikri | Tallest gateway in India (54m); built by Akbar to commemorate Gujarat victory (1601) |
| **Agra Fort** | Agra | Akbar (red sandstone outer), Shah Jahan (white marble inner apartments); UNESCO WHS |
| **Itmad-ud-Daulah** | Agra | Jahangir's period; father of Nur Jahan; first major Mughal structure in white marble; called "Baby Taj" |
| **Taj Mahal** | Agra | Shah Jahan; mausoleum of Mumtaz Mahal; 1632–1648; UNESCO WHS; Chief architect: **Ustad Ahmad Lahauri**; white Makrana marble |
| **Red Fort (Lal Qila)** | Delhi | Shah Jahan; built 1638–48; shifting capital from Agra to Shahjahanabad; UNESCO WHS (2007) |
| **Jama Masjid** | Delhi | Shah Jahan; largest mosque in India; capacity 25,000 |
| **Moti Masjid (Pearl Mosque)** | Inside Agra Fort | Shah Jahan; white marble; private mosque |

### Charbagh (Four-Garden Layout)
- Persian design: garden divided into 4 quadrants by channels representing the 4 rivers of Paradise
- Used in: Humayun's Tomb, Taj Mahal
- Water channels symbolise rivers of Paradise (Kosar, Salsabil, etc.)

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Qutb Minar** began by **Qutbuddin Aibak**, completed by **Iltutmish** — not built by a single sultan.
2. **Alai Darwaza** (1311, Alauddin Khalji) = **first true arch** in India. Key date for "arcuate" vs "trabeate" question.
3. **Iron Pillar** inside Qutb complex = **Gupta period** metallurgy, NOT part of Sultanate construction.
4. **Humayun's Tomb** = first complete Mughal garden tomb; template for Taj Mahal; built by **Haji Begum**.
5. **Fatehpur Sikri** was built by **Akbar**, not Shah Jahan. Abandoned due to water shortage.
6. **Buland Darwaza** = tallest gateway in India (54m); Akbar built it to celebrate **Gujarat conquest**.
7. **Itmad-ud-Daulah** = called "Baby Taj"; Jahangir period (NOT Shah Jahan); father of Nur Jahan.
8. **Taj Mahal architect**: **Ustad Ahmad Lahauri**; marble from **Makrana** (Rajasthan).
9. **Red Fort** UNESCO WHS = **2007** (not 1983 like Ajanta/Ellora, not 1993 like Humayun's Tomb).
10. **Sanchi Stupa**: built by Ashoka, but the famous **toranas (gateways)** were added in **Sunga period** (1st c. BCE), not by Ashoka.
11. **Dhamek Stupa** (Sarnath) = marks Buddha's first sermon site; **Mahabodhi Temple** (Bodh Gaya) = site of enlightenment.
12. **Dilwara Temples** (Mount Abu) = Jain, not Hindu; white marble; **all decoration is interior**, not exterior.
13. **Trabeate** = Hindu/Buddhist (post and beam); **Arcuate** = Islamic (arch and dome) — never reverse these.
14. **Bibi ka Maqbara** (Aurangabad) = built by Aurangzeb for his wife; called "Poor Man's Taj" — NOT the Taj Mahal.
15. **Gomateshwara statue** (Shravanabelagola) = 57 feet, Jain, Karnataka; **Mahamastakabhisheka** every 12 years.
16. **Tughlaq architecture** = sloping/battered walls (diagnostic feature) + grey stone; contrast with Khalji (red sandstone, decorative).
