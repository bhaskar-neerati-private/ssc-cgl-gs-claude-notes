# Folk Arts, Puppetry & Traditional Theatre

## The Puppeteer Who Taught History

In Rajasthan's villages in the 16th century, when most people could not read and no newspaper existed, Kathputli puppeteers carried the news. Their cloth-and-string marionettes depicted the heroism of Maharana Pratap, the treachery of Mughal forces, the romance of Dhola-Maru. Every fair, every festival, the Nat community would arrive with their Kathputli trunk and perform for hours. The entire village's understanding of history, ethics, and mythology was shaped not by texts but by these puppets. This is why India's folk arts matter: they were the mass media of pre-literate India, and SSC expects you to know which art form, which puppet type, and which theatre tradition belongs to which state.

## Puppetry Traditions of India

India has four main types of puppetry. Each type is found in specific regions.

### 1. String Puppets (Marionettes)
Controlled by strings from above.

| Name | State | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Kathputli** | Rajasthan | Most famous Indian puppet; cloth body, no legs; Nat community performers; stories of Rajput heroes |
| **Gombeyatta** | Karnataka | Wooden puppets; stories from Puranas |
| **Putala Naach** | West Bengal, Odisha | String marionettes; religious themes |
| **Kundhei** | Odisha | Similar to Putala Naach; Jagannath themes |

### 2. Shadow Puppets (Tolu Bommalata / Chaya Nataka)
Flat leather puppets projected on a backlit screen.

| Name | State | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Tholu Bommalata** | Andhra Pradesh | Colourful, translucent leather; large size (up to 5 ft); Ramayana stories |
| **Tolu Bommalu** | Telangana | Similar tradition |
| **Togalu Gombeyaata** | Karnataka | Monochromatic leather puppets; smaller size |
| **Tolpavakoothu** | Kerala | Performed in temple compounds (Koothumadam); shadow on white cloth; dedicated to Goddess Bhagavati |
| **Ravanachhaya** | Odisha | Single-coloured leather; no joints; rigid puppets; Ramayana stories |
| **Chamadyacha Bahulya** | Maharashtra | Leather shadow puppets |

### 3. Glove Puppets (Hand Puppets)
Cloth puppet worn on hand like glove.

| Name | State | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Pavakathakali** | Kerala | Miniature version of Kathakali; same costume/makeup style; performed by a single puppeteer |
| **Kundhei** | Odisha | Also glove form exists |
| **Beni** | Bengal | Simple glove puppets |

### 4. Rod Puppets
Puppets controlled by rods from below.

| Name | State | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Putul Naach** | West Bengal | Large rod puppets; Jatra-style performances |
| **Yampuri** | Bihar | Rod puppets; stories from mythology |
| **Kathi Kundhei** | Odisha | Rod puppet tradition |

## Traditional Theatre Forms of India

India's traditional theatre predates modern drama by centuries. These forms blend music, dance, dialogue, and spectacle.

### North India

| Form | State | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Ramlila** | Uttar Pradesh (Varanasi, Ramnagar) | 10-day enactment of Ramayana; Ramnagar's Ramlila is most famous; UNESCO ICH 2008 |
| **Ramman** | Uttarakhand (Chamoli district) | Religious festival of Saloor-Dungra village; UNESCO ICH 2009 |
| **Nautanki** | UP, Rajasthan, Haryana | Melodramatic folk opera; loud, colourful staging; social and historical themes; also called Swang in Haryana |
| **Maach** | Madhya Pradesh | Night-long musical plays; raised platform stage; devotional and social themes; Ujjain tradition |
| **Tamasha** | Maharashtra | Street theatre; Lavani dance integral; social satire; male performers (Kolhati community) traditionally |
| **Kirtan** | Maharashtra, West Bengal | Devotional musical narrative; storytelling about gods and saints |
| **Bidesia** | Bihar | Themes of migrant labour and separation; created by Bhikhari Thakur in 20th century |
| **Swang** | Haryana, Punjab | Similar to Nautanki; songs and dialogues; religious and social themes |

### South India

| Form | State | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Yakshagana** | Karnataka | Night-long theatrical; elaborate costumes, makeup; Puranic stories; distinct from Kathakali |
| **Koodiyattam** | Kerala | Oldest surviving Sanskrit theatre; UNESCO ICH 2001/2008; only Chakyar community performers traditionally |
| **Therukoothu** | Tamil Nadu | Street theatre; performed in open air; Mahabharata/Ramayana themes; Draupadi cult festivals |
| **Bhagavata Mela** | Tamil Nadu (Thanjavur) | Temple-based drama; Vaishnavite themes; elaborate costumes |
| **Krishnanattam** | Kerala | Predecessor of Kathakali; 8-day cycle plays on Krishna's life |
| **Mudiyettu** | Kerala | Ritual drama enacting battle of Goddess Bhadrakali and demon Darika; UNESCO ICH 2010 |
| **Ankiya Nat** | Assam | Vaishnavite dance-drama by Srimanta Sankardeva; performed in Sattra courtyards |
| **Bhaona** | Assam | Dramatisation of Ankiya Nat plays; community performances |

### East India

| Form | State | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Jatra** | West Bengal, Odisha | Most popular Bengali theatre form; loud, dramatic; performed in open ground; social and mythological themes; also: Odia Jatra |
| **Gambhira** | West Bengal | Mask dance-drama; Shiva themes; Malda district |

## Koodiyattam — The UNESCO First

- **Oldest surviving Sanskrit theatre** in the world
- Performed by **Chakyar** (male, performs Chakyar Koothu solo) and **Nangyar** (female) communities
- Traditionally only in temple stages (**Koothambalam**)
- **Mizhavu** = pot-shaped drum unique to Koodiyattam
- UNESCO listed in 2001 under "Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity" (2008 in standard ICH list)

---

## Traditional Music-Drama of Northeast

- **Ankiya Nat**: Created by Srimanta Sankardeva (15th century); single-act plays in Assam; performed in Sattra
- **Manikyadhar**: Manipuri ritual performance
- **Oja-Pali**: Assam; oral narration with gestures; two types — Byah Oja and Daina Oja

---

## Storytelling Traditions

| Tradition | State | Form |
|---|---|---|
| **Pandavani** | Chhattisgarh | Solo narration of Mahabharata; Teejan Bai = famous exponent; Padma Vibhushan awardee |
| **Phad Painting + Performance** | Rajasthan | Scroll painting depicting stories of Devnarayan and Pabuji; Bhopa priest sings while showing the scroll by lamplight |
| **Burra Katha** | Andhra Pradesh | Group narration; 3-person performance; lead narrator (kathakar) with two assistants |
| **Harikatha** | Karnataka, TN, AP | Solo devotional discourse blending music, narration, philosophy |
| **Villu Pattu** | Tamil Nadu | Bow song; singer uses a musical bow (villu); narrative songs about deities |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Kathputli** = string puppet from **Rajasthan** (not rod or shadow puppet). Cloth body, no legs.
2. **Tholu Bommalata** = shadow puppet from **Andhra Pradesh**. Translucent colourful leather.
3. **Tolpavakoothu** = shadow puppet from **Kerala**, performed in temple compounds for Goddess Bhagavati.
4. **Ravanachhaya** = Odisha shadow puppet; rigid (no joints), single-coloured leather.
5. **Pavakathakali** = Kerala **glove puppet** — miniature Kathakali, NOT string puppet.
6. **Koodiyattam** = Kerala, oldest Sanskrit theatre, UNESCO ICH; performed by Chakyar community; **Mizhavu** drum.
7. **Ramlila** was inscribed in UNESCO ICH in **2008** — same year as Koodiyattam in standard list.
8. **Mudiyettu** = Kerala, UNESCO 2010 — ritual drama about Goddess Bhadrakali vs demon Darika.
9. **Ankiya Nat** and **Bhaona** = Assam, created by **Srimanta Sankardeva** — same creator as Sattriya dance.
10. **Yakshagana** = Karnataka night theatre; NOT the same as Kerala's Kathakali despite similar appearance.
11. **Jatra** = West Bengal's popular folk theatre form (also in Odisha as Odia Jatra).
12. **Tamasha** = Maharashtra; always includes **Lavani** dance; performed by Kolhati community.
13. **Nautanki** = UP, Rajasthan, Haryana — called **Swang** in Haryana and Punjab.
14. **Pandavani** = Chhattisgarh; solo Mahabharata narration; **Teejan Bai** is the most famous exponent (Padma Vibhushan).
15. **Phad** = Rajasthan scroll painting + sung storytelling; **Bhopa** priest performs while unrolling scroll at night.
16. **Putul Naach** = West Bengal **rod puppets** (NOT string). Bengali rod puppets, not marionettes.
