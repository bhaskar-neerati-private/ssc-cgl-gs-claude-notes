# Intangible Heritage, Cultural Bodies & Awards

## The Art Form That Almost Died in a Filing Cabinet

In 2001, when UNESCO launched its first list of "Oral and Intangible Heritage of Humanity," one of the first 19 items selected was **Koodiyattam** — the 2,000-year-old Sanskrit theatre tradition from Kerala performed by the Chakyar community in temple stages. Fewer than 10 families in all of Kerala still knew the art. The inscriptions in the temple archives, the mudra sequences, the voice modulation — all of this rested in the minds of fewer people than can fit in a small classroom. UNESCO's listing did two things: it brought international funding, and it created the moral pressure on the Indian government to fund revival. By 2015, there were Koodiyattam training centres in Thiruvananthapuram. This is what cultural awards and institutional support do — they rescue traditions that are dying in living rooms.

## Major Cultural Bodies / Institutions of India

### Akademis (National Academies)

| Institution | Established | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Sangeet Natak Akademi (SNA)** | 1952 | Performing arts: music, dance, drama; headquartered in Delhi; grants fellowships and awards |
| **Sahitya Akademi** | 1954 | Literature; publishes in 24 languages; Sahitya Akademi Award; National Academy of Letters |
| **Lalit Kala Akademi** | 1954 | Visual arts: painting, sculpture, graphic arts; National Academy of Art |

All three established in **1952–1954**; all headquartered in **New Delhi**.

### Other Key Institutions

| Institution | Role |
|---|---|
| **Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)** | Est. 1861; protects and preserves monuments; under Ministry of Culture; Director General is head |
| **National Museum, Delhi** | Largest museum in India; established 1949; Indus Valley to modern collections |
| **Indian Museum, Kolkata** | Oldest and largest museum in India (established 1814); housed Bharhut stupa sculptures |
| **Indira Gandhi National Centre for the Arts (IGNCA)** | 1985; research in arts; under Ministry of Culture |
| **National Gallery of Modern Art (NGMA)** | Delhi/Mumbai/Bengaluru; modern and contemporary Indian art |
| **Kalakshetra Foundation** | Chennai; Rukmini Devi Arundale's institution; Bharatanatyam and Carnatic music |
| **Sangeet Natak Akademi** | Delhi; performing arts | 
| **Zonal Cultural Centres (ZCCs)** | 7 zones across India; promote regional folk and traditional arts |
| **National School of Drama (NSD)** | Delhi; 1959; premier theatre training institute |
| **Film and Television Institute of India (FTII)** | Pune; 1960; premier film training |
| **Satyajit Ray Film and Television Institute** | Kolkata; film training; named after legendary filmmaker |

## Major Cultural Awards

### Padma Awards (India's Civilian Honours)

The Padma awards are the highest civilian honours. They include three tiers relevant to culture:

| Award | Significance |
|---|---|
| **Padma Vibhushan** | Exceptional and distinguished service; 2nd highest civilian award |
| **Padma Bhushan** | Distinguished service of high order; 3rd highest |
| **Padma Shri** | Distinguished service in any field; 4th highest |
| **Bharat Ratna** | Highest civilian award; initially for arts/literature/science; now any field |

### Sangeet Natak Akademi Awards

- **Sangeet Natak Akademi Award**: Annual; performing arts
- **Sangeet Natak Akademi Fellowship** (Sangeet Natak Akademi Ratna): Highest SNA honour; lifetime achievement
- **SNA Ustad Bismillah Khan Yuva Puraskar**: For young artists under 40

### Sahitya Akademi Awards

- **Sahitya Akademi Award**: Annual; in 24 languages; cash prize + copper plaque
- **Sahitya Akademi Fellowship**: Highest SNA literary honour; lifetime achievement
- **Bhasha Samman**: For classical and medieval literature

### Other Culture-Specific Awards

| Award | Given By | Field |
|---|---|---|
| **Kalidas Samman** | Madhya Pradesh Government | Classical arts, literature |
| **Tulsi Samman** | MP Government | Performing arts |
| **Lalit Kala Akademi Award** | Lalit Kala Akademi | Visual arts |
| **Filmfare Awards** | Film industry (private) | Hindi cinema |
| **National Film Awards** | Government of India (DGFT) | Cinema; Swarna Kamal = best picture |
| **Dada Saheb Phalke Award** | Government of India | Highest film honour; lifetime achievement in cinema |

### Dada Saheb Phalke Award

- Established: **1969**
- Named after: **Dhundiraj Govind Phalke** = "Father of Indian Cinema"; directed **Raja Harishchandra** (1913) = **first Indian film**
- Recipients include: Devika Rani (first, 1969), Satyajit Ray (1992), Lata Mangeshkar (1989), Dilip Kumar (1994), Amitabh Bachchan (2018), Asha Parekh (2023)

## Constitutional Provisions for Culture

| Article | Provision |
|---|---|
| **Art. 29** | Right to conserve distinct language, script, culture (minority rights) |
| **Art. 30** | Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions |
| **Art. 51A(f)** | Fundamental Duty to value and preserve the rich heritage of composite culture |
| **Art. 343** | Official language of Union = Hindi in Devanagari script |
| **8th Schedule** | 22 scheduled languages of India |

### 22 Scheduled Languages (8th Schedule)
Assamese, Bengali, Bodo, Dogri, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Kashmiri, Konkani, Maithili, Malayalam, Manipuri, Marathi, Nepali, Odia, Punjabi, Sanskrit, Santali, Sindhi, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu.
- **Maithili** and **Bodo** were added in **2004** (by 92nd Amendment).
- Sindhi was added in 1967; Konkani, Manipuri, Nepali added in 1992.

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Sangeet Natak Akademi** = 1952; **Sahitya Akademi** and **Lalit Kala Akademi** = 1954. SNA is 2 years older.
2. **Indian Museum, Kolkata** = oldest and largest museum in India (est. **1814**); NOT National Museum Delhi (1949).
3. **Koodiyattam** = **first Indian element** on any UNESCO ICH list (2001); Kerala Sanskrit theatre by Chakyar community.
4. **Dada Saheb Phalke Award** est. in **1969**; named after maker of **first Indian film** (Raja Harishchandra, 1913).
5. **Bharat Ratna** = highest civilian award = above Padma Vibhushan (2nd).
6. **ASI (Archaeological Survey of India)** = est. **1861** under British; first Director General = **Alexander Cunningham**.
7. **Garba** (Gujarat) added to UNESCO ICH in **2023** — among most recent; likely tested.
8. **Kumbh Mela** = UNESCO ICH **2017**; NOT 2019 or 2021 — common year confusion.
9. **National Film Awards** = Swarna Kamal (best film), Rajat Kamal (others) — SSC asks what the awards are called.
10. **22 Scheduled Languages** in 8th Schedule — NOT all 28 states' languages; Bodo and Maithili added in 2004 (92nd Amendment).
11. **Article 51A(f)** = Fundamental **Duty** to value and preserve cultural heritage — it's a DUTY, not a RIGHT.
12. **Article 29** = minority right to conserve **language, script, culture** (not religion separately — that's Art. 25).
13. **Sahitya Akademi Award** given in **24 languages** — not all 22 scheduled languages; includes some non-scheduled.
14. **National School of Drama** = Delhi, established **1959**; separate from FTII (Pune, 1960).
15. **Kalidas Samman** = given by **Madhya Pradesh** government (not Union government) — state award, not national.
16. **Durga Puja** = UNESCO ICH **2021**, listed as "Kolkata Durga Puja" — note it's city-specific, not all Durga Puja.
