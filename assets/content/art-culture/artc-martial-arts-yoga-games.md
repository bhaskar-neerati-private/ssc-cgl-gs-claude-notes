# Martial Arts, Yoga & Traditional Sports

## The System That Defeated the British

In 1916, a young student in Travancore (present-day Kerala) was expelled from school for practising Kalaripayattu — the ancient Kerala martial art. The colonial authorities had banned it in 1804 as a threat to public order, just as they had banned Silambam in Tamil Nadu. The British understood what many modern scholars have only recently documented: that these martial traditions were not merely sporting practices but complete pedagogical systems that taught self-discipline, body mastery, and in some cases, armed resistance. The expulsion of that student — named C. V. Narayanan Nair, who later became the greatest Kalaripayattu teacher of the 20th century — only spread the art's influence. Today, Kalaripayattu is studied by martial arts researchers from Japan and Korea as possibly the oldest surviving martial art in the world.

## Indian Martial Arts

| Martial Art | State | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Kalaripayattu** | Kerala | Oldest martial art; 2,000+ year old history; 3 stages: Meithari (body exercises), Kolthari (wooden weapons), Angathari (metal weapons); also therapeutic tradition (Kalari massage); Bruce Lee reportedly studied its influences |
| **Silambam** | Tamil Nadu | Stick-fighting art; bamboo staff; ancient Sangam period references; also mentions in Cilappatikaram |
| **Gatka** | Punjab | Sikh martial art; weapons: kirpan, stick, shield; associated with Nihang Sikhs; practised at festivals |
| **Thang-Ta** | Manipur | Sword (thang) and spear (ta) combat art; also called Huyen Langlon; performed as a dance-drama form (Thang-Ta dance) |
| **Mardani Khel** | Maharashtra | Traditional armed fighting; sword and shield; Maratha warrior tradition; Rajput tradition |
| **Chhau** | WB/Jharkhand/Odisha | Semi-classical martial dance; uses warrior techniques; acrobatics + dance + martial poses |
| **Pari-khanda** | Bihar | Sword and shield fighting; agricultural community tradition |
| **Musti Yuddha** | Uttar Pradesh (Varanasi) | Ancient boxing tradition; Varanasi's wrestling ghats |
| **Sarit Sarak** | Manipur | Unarmed combat complementary to Thang-Ta |
| **Lathi Khela** | West Bengal | Bamboo staff fighting; traditional martial form |

## Yoga

### Origin and History
- **Yoga** = Sanskrit word meaning "union" (union of individual consciousness with universal consciousness)
- Oldest references: **Rigveda** (~1500 BCE) — word yoga appears
- **Patanjali's Yoga Sutras** (~400 BCE – 400 CE): Systematised yoga into 8 limbs (Ashtanga)
- **Hatha Yoga Pradipika** (15th century): Physical yoga; Swami Swatmarama
- **Modern yoga**: Swami Vivekananda introduced yoga to the West at Chicago Parliament of World Religions (1893)

### Patanjali's Ashtanga Yoga (8 Limbs)
1. **Yama** — ethical restraints (non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, non-possessiveness)
2. **Niyama** — personal observances (purity, contentment, discipline, self-study, surrender)
3. **Asana** — postures
4. **Pranayama** — breath control
5. **Pratyahara** — withdrawal of senses
6. **Dharana** — concentration
7. **Dhyana** — meditation
8. **Samadhi** — absorption/liberation

### Types of Yoga
- **Hatha Yoga**: Physical; asanas and pranayama; most widely practised in the West
- **Raja Yoga**: Mental/meditative; Patanjali's 8-limb path
- **Karma Yoga**: Path of selfless action (Bhagavad Gita)
- **Bhakti Yoga**: Path of devotion
- **Jnana Yoga**: Path of knowledge/wisdom
- **Kundalini Yoga**: Energy awakening through chakras

### International Day of Yoga
- **Date**: **June 21** (longest day of year in Northern Hemisphere — summer solstice)
- **Proposed by**: PM Narendra Modi at UN General Assembly (September 2014)
- **First celebrated**: June 21, 2015
- **UN Resolution**: Adopted unanimously by UN General Assembly; co-sponsored by 177 nations (record)
- **UNESCO ICH**: Yoga inscribed in 2016

## Traditional Indian Sports and Games

### Major Traditional Sports

| Sport/Game | Region | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Kabaddi** | Pan-India; origin Tamil Nadu/South India | Contact team sport; "raid" mechanic; national game of Bangladesh; played in Asian Games since 1990 (Kabaddi World Cup); India = most dominant |
| **Kho-Kho** | Maharashtra/Karnataka | Tag-based team sport; Indian equivalent of tag; chasing and dodging; Kho-Kho Federation of India |
| **Wrestling (Kushti / Malla-Yuddha)** | Pan-India | Pehlwani = wrestler; Akhara = training ground; Varanasi, Delhi, Kolhapur famous |
| **Mallakhamb** | Maharashtra; also Gujarat | Gymnastics on a wooden pole or rope; "Malla" (gymnast) + "Khamb" (pole); Maharashtra's state sport |
| **Gilli-Danda** | Pan-India | Rural; two sticks (gilli = small, danda = long); precursor of cricket/baseball theories |
| **Pittu / Saat Pathar** | Pan-India | Stack of stones + ball; team game |
| **Lagori** | South India | Same as Pittu/Saat Pathar; regional name |
| **Vallamkali** | Kerala | Snake Boat Race; Onam festival; Nehru Trophy Race = most famous; Ashtamudi and Alappuzha backwaters |
| **Bull Taming (Jallikattu)** | Tamil Nadu | Ancient sport; Tamil culture; Pongal celebrations; controversy and Supreme Court interventions |

### Board Games of Indian Origin

| Game | Origin | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Chess (Chaturanga)** | India (~6th century CE) | Original form: Chaturanga (Sanskrit for "four divisions" = infantry, cavalry, elephants, chariots); spread to Persia as Chatrang/Shatranj, then to Europe |
| **Snakes and Ladders (Moksha Patam)** | India | Created by saint **Gyandev** in 13th century CE; moral teaching (virtues = ladders; vices = snakes); British took it to England |
| **Pachisi** | India | 6th–7th century; board game using cowrie shells as dice; imperial game (Akbar played on huge boards with harem women as pieces); ancestor of Ludo |
| **Playing Cards (Ganjifa)** | India/Persia | Indian playing cards; circular cards; depicted Navratna treasures |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Kalaripayattu** = **Kerala**; considered one of the **oldest martial arts** in the world; NOT Tamil Nadu.
2. **Silambam** = Tamil Nadu stick-fighting; referenced in **Sangam literature** (Cilappatikaram).
3. **Gatka** = Sikh martial art from **Punjab** — distinct from Kalaripayattu (Kerala).
4. **International Day of Yoga** = **June 21** (summer solstice); proposed by Modi at UNGA **September 2014**; first celebrated **2015**.
5. **Patanjali's Yoga Sutras** = defines **8 limbs (Ashtanga)** — not just asanas; Dhyana is the 7th limb.
6. **Chess** = Indian origin as **Chaturanga** (~6th c. CE); NOT Chinese or Arabic origin.
7. **Snakes and Ladders** = Indian origin ("Moksha Patam"); created by **Gyandev** (13th c.); moral/religious teaching tool.
8. **Pachisi** = ancestor of **Ludo**; cowrie shell dice; Indian origin; Akbar played it.
9. **Mallakhamb** = **Maharashtra's state sport**; gymnastics on a pole; "gymnast + pole."
10. **Kabaddi** = India's most dominant international sport; national game of **Bangladesh** (not India — India's national game is hockey, though that designation is sometimes debated).
11. **Nehru Trophy Boat Race** = **Kerala** (Alappuzha/Alleppey); held on Punnamada Lake; snake boats; part of Onam celebrations.
12. **Jallikattu** = Tamil Nadu; bull-taming; **Mattu Pongal** connection; banned and reinstated controversially.
13. **Major Dhyan Chand Khel Ratna** = renamed from Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna in **2021** — know both names.
14. **National Sports Day** = **August 29** = Major Dhyan Chand's birthday.
15. **Dronacharya Award** = for **coaches** (named after Drona from Mahabharata, who taught Arjuna).
16. **Thang-Ta** = Manipur; sword (thang) + spear (ta) = the name itself tells the weapons — SSC tests this.
