# Classical Dance Forms of India

## The Night Rukmini Devi Shocked All of Madras

In 1935, a Brahmin woman named Rukmini Devi Arundale stepped onto the stage of the Music Academy in Madras and performed Bharatanatyam — a form that Brahmins had for centuries left to the Devadasis alone. The audience was scandalised. Conservative Madras declared it sacrilege. Yet within a generation, this single act had propelled Bharatanatyam from near-extinction inside temple corridors to the most widely practised classical dance of independent India, performed on stages from New York to Tokyo. Today, when SSC asks you which dance originates from Tamil Nadu, it is asking you about that revolution.

India's classical dances are not merely art — they are codified systems with roots in the **Natyashastra**, the 2,000-year-old treatise attributed to sage **Bharata Muni**. The Natyashastra defines three elements common to all classical dance: **Nritta** (pure rhythmic movement), **Nritya** (expressive movement blending rhythm with emotion — abhinaya), and **Natya** (dramatic storytelling). This trinity is the test filter SSC returns to repeatedly.

The Sangeet Natak Akademi officially recognises **8 classical dance forms** of India. Know each one precisely — state, origin, and key markers.

---

## The 8 Classical Dance Forms

| Dance Form | State / Region | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Bharatanatyam** | Tamil Nadu | Oldest classical form; Devadasi tradition; tandava + lasya; performances: Alarippu → Jatiswaram → Shabdam → Varnam → Padam → Tillana; Margam sequence |
| **Kathak** | Uttar Pradesh (North India) | Blends Hindu (Bhakti) and Mughal court styles; fast footwork (tatkar); pirouettes (chakkar); two major gharanas: Lucknow (lyrical) and Jaipur (energetic); also Benaras gharana |
| **Kuchipudi** | Andhra Pradesh | Originated in Krishna district village of same name; Brahmin male tradition (Bhagavatham style); drama + dance; unique item: dancing on brass plate (Tarangam) |
| **Odissi** | Odisha | Derived from Mahari tradition (temple dancers of Jagannath temple); sculptural poses (tribhangi posture — head, torso, hips in S-curve); chauka (square stance) and tribhangi (three-bend) |
| **Kathakali** | Kerala | Story-telling through elaborate makeup (chutti) and costumes; Pacha (noble hero), Kathi (villain), Kari (evil female); eye movements and hand gestures (mudras) crucial; drawn from Ramayana, Mahabharata |
| **Mohiniyattam** | Kerala | Lasya (feminine, graceful) style; swaying movement like coconut palm in breeze; name means "dance of the enchantress Mohini"; white and gold costume |
| **Manipuri** | Manipur | Ras Lila (Radha-Krishna theme); Lai Haraoba festival origins; no vigorous stamping; circular, gentle movements; distinctive drum: Pung (barrel drum) |
| **Sattriya** | Assam | 15th-century Vaishnavite tradition by Srimanta Sankardeva; performed in Sattras (monasteries); recognised as classical form only in **2000** (latest to be added) |

> **Critical fact**: Sangeet Natak Akademi (SNA) was established in **1952**. It added Sattriya as the 8th form in 2000. Before Sattriya, 7 forms were recognised.

## Bharatanatyam — The Deepest Examined

- **Origin**: Temple devadasi tradition of Tamil Nadu; earlier called **Sadir** or **Dasi Attam**
- **Revivalists**: Rukmini Devi Arundale (1935, Kalakshetra Chennai) and E. Krishna Iyer
- **Margam** (performance sequence): Alarippu → Jatiswaram → Shabdam → Varnam → Padam → Javali → Tillana
- **Tandava vs Lasya**: Tandava = masculine/vigorous (Shiva); Lasya = feminine/graceful (Parvati/Radha)
- **Abhinaya** (expression): 4 types — Angika (body), Vachika (speech), Aharya (costume/makeup), Sattvika (emotional)
- **Carnatic music** accompaniment; **Mridangam** and **Veena** are key instruments

---

## Kathak — The Storyteller's Dance

- **Kathakas** were storytellers in North Indian temples; Mughal patronage added Persian elements (spins, ghungroo)
- **Gharanas**:
  - **Lucknow Gharana**: Founded by Thakur Prasad; emphasis on grace, lyrical expression, thumri
  - **Jaipur Gharana**: Founded by Bhanu Ji; emphasis on vigour, fast footwork, complex compositions
  - **Benaras Gharana**: Third major school; strong in bhajan-based compositions
- **Tatkar**: Footwork sequence; **chakkar**: spin/pirouette
- Notable exponents: Birju Maharaj (Lucknow Gharana), Sitara Devi

---

## Odissi — The Tribhangi Posture

- Based on sculptures at **Konark Sun Temple** and **Bhubaneswar temples**
- Three stances: **Samabhangi** (straight), **Abhanga** (one side bent), **Tribhangi** (S-curve: head, chest, hips in three different directions)
- **Chauka**: square stance representing Bhubaneswar (earth); **Tribhangi**: representing Jagannath
- Revived by Guru Kelucharan Mohapatra and Sanjukta Panigrahi
- Music: **Odissi classical music** (Odishi music); **Pakhawaj** drum

---

## Kathakali — The God-King on Stage

- Originated in **Kerala** in 17th century; associated with **Zamorin of Calicut**
- **Chutti**: elaborate makeup using rice paste applied layer by layer
- **Character types**: Pacha (green face = noble hero like Rama/Krishna), Kathi (green with red marks = anti-hero), Kari (black face = evil/forest dwellers), Minukku (golden yellow = women)
- Performance starts at dusk, runs through the night
- **Chenda** (cylindrical drum) and **Maddalam** (barrel drum) are accompanying instruments
- Stories drawn from: Ramayana, Mahabharata, Bhagavata Purana

## Natyashastra — The Foundation Text

- Author: **Bharata Muni**
- Dated: ~200 BCE – 200 CE
- Covers: drama, dance, music, stagecraft, costumes, makeup
- Defines: **Navarasas** (9 emotions/rasas) — Shringara, Hasya, Karuna, Raudra, Vira, Bhayanaka, Bibhatsa, Adbhuta, Shanta
- **Rasa theory**: Emotion evoked in audience through **Bhava** (feeling expressed by performer)

---

## Mudras and Abhinaya

- **Mudras**: Hand gestures — **Asamyukta Hasta** (single-hand) and **Samyukta Hasta** (double-hand)
- **Hasta Mudras**: 28 single-hand + 24 double-hand = 52 classical hand gestures (Bharatanatyam tradition)
- **Abhinaya Darpana**: Text by Nandikesvara defining facial expressions, glances, neck movements, etc.

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Sattriya** was added to the classical list **in 2000** — last to be recognised. Don't confuse with Manipuri.
2. **Mohiniyattam** and **Kathakali** are BOTH from Kerala — Mohiniyattam is lasya (feminine), Kathakali is energetic, dramatic.
3. **Kuchipudi** village is in **Andhra Pradesh** (not Telangana historically, though now in different administrative states — origin is Andhra Pradesh).
4. **Rukmini Devi Arundale** revived Bharatanatyam in 1935, not 1947 or 1952.
5. **Sangeet Natak Akademi** established in **1952** — same year as Sangeet Natak Akademi for performing arts.
6. **Manipuri** drum is **Pung** (barrel-shaped) — not mridangam.
7. **Odissi** tribhangi = three-body-bend, chauka = square stance.
8. **Kathak** has THREE gharanas: Lucknow, Jaipur, Benaras — SSC sometimes asks which is NOT a gharana.
9. **Bharatanatyam** was earlier called **Sadir** or **Dasi Attam** — devadasi tradition.
10. **Natyashastra**: Bharata Muni, ~200 BCE–200 CE, defines all three — nritta, nritya, natya.
11. **Navarasas**: 9 emotions — Shanta is the 9th rasa (peace/serenity), added later to original 8.
12. **Kathakali** makeup character: Pacha face = green = noble hero. Don't mix up Kathi with Pacha.
13. **Birju Maharaj** = Kathak, Lucknow Gharana. Don't confuse with his father's Jaipur connection.
14. **Srimanta Sankardeva** created Sattriya in 15th century in **Assam** — Vaishnavite movement.
15. **Mahari** dancers of Jagannath Puri = source of Odissi tradition.
16. Total classical dance forms = **8** (not 6 or 9) — as per Sangeet Natak Akademi.
