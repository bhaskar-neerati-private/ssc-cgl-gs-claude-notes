# Temple Architecture — Nagara, Dravida & Vesara

## The King Who Ran Out of Vision, Not Stone

In 1017 CE, the Paramara king Bhoja began building a temple to Shiva in Bhojpur (present-day Madhya Pradesh). The construction went on for decades. The unfinished shikara — tower — still stands today, a colossus of grey sandstone that would have been India's tallest temple. King Bhoja died in 1055 CE; the temple was never completed. Yet even unfinished, the Bhojpur temple reveals the genius of the Nagara school: the pure verticality of the tower, the precise mathematical ratios encoded in the Vastu Shastra, the progression from the sanctum upward to the sky as a literal model of Mount Meru. When SSC asks you which temple style has a curved tower vs a flat-topped tower, this question reaches back to debates among medieval Indian architects that were never trivial — they were debates about how stone could touch the divine.

India's temple architecture is classified into three primary styles: **Nagara** (North), **Dravida** (South), and **Vesara** (mixed, Deccan).

---

## The Three Styles — Core Comparison

| Feature | **Nagara** (North) | **Dravida** (South) | **Vesara** (Deccan/Mixed) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Region** | North India (Himalaya to Vindhyas) | South India (below Krishna River) | Deccan (Vindhyas to Krishna) |
| **Tower** | **Shikhara** — curvilinear, tapering upward | **Vimana** — pyramidal, stepped tiers | Mix of both |
| **Entrance Tower** | No large gateway tower | **Gopuram** — massive entrance gateway | Variable |
| **Sanctum** | Garbhagriha; no water tank required | Often with tank (**Pushkarini**) | Variable |
| **Examples** | Khajuraho, Konark, Lingaraj, Kedarnath | Brihadeeswarar, Meenakshi, Rameshwaram | Hoysala temples (Belur, Halebidu) |
| **Key Text** | Manasara, Mayamata | Manasara, Agama texts | — |

## Nagara Style — Details

The Nagara shikhara is divided into sub-types based on the tower form:

### Sub-types of Nagara
- **Latina (Rekha Prasada)**: Single, curvilinear shikhara over sanctum; simplest form; Odisha temples (Lingaraj), UP temples
- **Sekhari**: Multiple mini-shikharas (ureushringas) clustered around the main shikhara; Khajuraho temples
- **Bhumija**: Vertical rows of mini-turrets on all four sides of shikhara; Central India

### Nagara Temple Parts
- **Garbhagriha**: Inner sanctum (womb-house); houses the main deity
- **Shikhara**: Tower above garbhagriha
- **Mandapa**: Pillared hall(s) for congregation; can be multiple (Antarala, Mahamandapa)
- **Pradakshinapatha**: Circumambulatory path around the sanctum
- **Amalaka**: The ribbed, disc-shaped crown stone just below the finial (kalasha); typical of Nagara

### Key Nagara Temples

| Temple | Location | Dynasty | Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kandariya Mahadeva** | Khajuraho, MP | Chandela | Tallest Khajuraho temple; finest Sekhari Nagara |
| **Lingaraj Temple** | Bhubaneswar, Odisha | Somavamshi | Classical Odisha Nagara (Deula style) |
| **Konark Sun Temple** | Odisha | Ganga dynasty (Narasimhadeva I, 13th c.) | Designed as chariot with 24 wheels; UNESCO WHS; major shikhara collapsed |
| **Brihadeshwara / Mukteshwara** | Bhubaneswar | Somavamshi | Major complex |
| **Somnath Temple** | Gujarat | Rebuilt multiple times | Destroyed 17 times, rebuilt; Solanki style |
| **Dilwara Jain Temples** | Mount Abu, Rajasthan | 11th–13th century | White marble; intricate ceiling carving; Vimal Vasahi and Luna Vasahi |
| **Kedarnath Temple** | Uttarakhand | Ancient | Nagara in Himalayan hills |
| **Badami Cave Temples** | Karnataka | Chalukya | Early cave-cut temples with Nagara + Dravida blend |

## Dravida Style — Details

### Dravida Temple Parts
- **Garbhagriha**: Inner sanctum
- **Vimana**: Stepped pyramid tower above garbhagriha; multiple tiers (talas)
- **Gopuram**: Monumental gateway tower(s) at the boundary walls; often taller than vimana
- **Mandapa**: Pillared halls; **Mahamandapa** is the main hall
- **Pushkarini**: Sacred temple tank for ritual bathing
- **Prakaras**: Concentric boundary walls; multiple rings in large temples
- **Nandi Mandapa**: Pavilion for Nandi bull (in Shiva temples)

### Key Dravida Temples

| Temple | Location | Dynasty | Feature |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brihadeeswarar (Thanjavur)** | Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu | Chola (Raja Raja Chola I, 1010 CE) | Tallest vimana in India (66m); UNESCO WHS; no shadow at noon |
| **Gangaikondacholapuram** | Tamil Nadu | Chola (Rajendra Chola I) | Twin to Thanjavur; slightly smaller |
| **Airavatesvara Temple** | Darasuram, Tamil Nadu | Chola | UNESCO WHS; miniature; Chola micro-architecture |
| **Meenakshi Amman Temple** | Madurai, Tamil Nadu | Nayaka period | 14 gopurams; tallest = 52m South Tower |
| **Shore Temple** | Mahabalipuram, TN | Pallava | Oldest surviving stone temple in South India; UNESCO WHS |
| **Virupaksha Temple** | Hampi, Karnataka | Vijayanagara | Still-functioning; at heart of UNESCO WHS complex |
| **Rameshwaram (Ramanathaswamy)** | Tamil Nadu | Nayaka/Madurai | Longest corridor (1200m) of any temple in India |
| **Srirangam** | Trichy, TN | Multiple dynasties | Largest functioning Hindu temple complex in the world; 7 concentric prakaras |

## Vesara Style (Mixed/Hybrid)

Vesara = "hybrid" (Sanskrit); features of both Nagara and Dravida.
- **Main Example**: **Hoysala temples** of Karnataka

### Hoysala Architecture

| Temple | Location | Key Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Chennakesava Temple** | Belur, Hassan dist., Karnataka | Built by Vishnuvardhana (1117 CE); intricate friezes; star-shaped plan |
| **Hoysaleswara Temple** | Halebidu, Hassan dist., Karnataka | Double shrine; no gopuram/shikhara; horizontal layers of friezes; most elaborate Hoysala carvings |
| **Kesava Temple** | Somanathapur, Mysuru dist. | Best-preserved Hoysala temple; trikuta (3 shrines) on star plan |

**Hoysala Key Features**:
- **Stellate (star-shaped) plan** — temple plan and tower both have pointed projections
- **Chloritic schite (soapstone)** material — soft when quarried, hardens after exposure; allows intricate carving
- No large shikhara — tower is vesara (short, decorative)
- Friezes at base: bottom to top = elephants (stability), lions (bravery), horses (speed), scrolling creepers, Puranic stories, Makaras, Hamsas

---

## Chalukyan Architecture (Transitional)

- **Early Western Chalukyas** (Badami Chalukyas, 6th–8th c.): Both rock-cut and structural; Aihole = "cradle of Indian architecture" (studied temples, experimented with forms); Badami, Pattadakal (UNESCO WHS)
- **Pattadakal temples**: Some Nagara, some Dravida — literally a testing ground where medieval architects tried both styles

| Vimana | South Indian stepped pyramid tower |

| Mandapa | Pillared assembly hall |
| Amalaka | Ribbed disc just below finial in Nagara style |

| Pradakshinapatha | Circumambulatory passage |
| Pushkarini | Temple tank |
| Tala | Each tier/storey of a vimana |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **Nagara = curvilinear shikhara**; **Dravida = stepped vimana** — this is the most fundamental distinction.
2. **Gopuram** (massive gateway tower) = Dravida style. SSC sometimes says Nagara has gopurams — it doesn't in the same sense.
3. **Brihadeeswarar Temple** (Thanjavur) = **Chola** dynasty, **Raja Raja Chola I**, 1010 CE. Tallest vimana in India.
4. **Konark Sun Temple** = **Ganga dynasty** (not Chola/Chalukya/Pallava). Designed as chariot with **24 wheels**.
5. **Khajuraho temples** = **Chandela** dynasty, Madhya Pradesh. Kandariya Mahadeva = tallest.
6. **Lingaraj Temple** = Bhubaneswar, Odisha = Nagara (Deula style).
7. **Shore Temple** (Mahabalipuram) = **Pallava** dynasty; oldest stone structural temple in South India.
8. **Srirangam** = largest functioning temple complex in the world; Tamil Nadu.
9. **Hoysala temples** = **Belur, Halebidu, Somanathapur** — Karnataka; star-shaped plan; **soapstone (chloritic schite)**.
10. **Aihole** in Karnataka = "Cradle of Indian Architecture" (Chalukya experiments).
11. **Dilwara Jain Temples** = Mount Abu, Rajasthan; **white marble**; not related to Nagara or Dravida Hindu style.
12. **Pattadakal** = UNESCO WHS; has both Nagara and Dravida style temples side-by-side = transition point.
13. **Amalaka** = ribbed disc crown stone at top of Nagara shikhara — below the kalasha.
14. **Vesara = neither purely Nagara nor Dravida** — Hoysala is the classic Vesara example.
15. **Meenakshi Temple** (Madurai) = 14 gopurams; Nayaka period. The gopurams are taller than the vimana — classic Dravida feature.
16. **Rameshwaram** has the **longest corridor** (~1200m) of any temple — not the largest complex (that's Srirangam).
