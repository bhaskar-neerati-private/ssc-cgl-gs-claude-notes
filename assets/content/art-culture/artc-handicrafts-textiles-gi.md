# Handicrafts, Textiles & GI Tags

## The Weavers Who Wove the Stars

In 1302, Sultan Alauddin Khalji's spies reported a strange fact: a single Dhaka muslin sari cost more than the annual salary of 30 soldiers. The fabric was so sheer that when worn, it seemed to disappear. Mughal emperors called it "Woven Air" (Woven of Air = *baft hawa*) and "Running Water" (*ab-e-rawan*). Dhaka muslin was woven from a single variety of cotton — **Phuti karpas** — that grew only in the Meghna river basin in Bengal. When the British textile industry mechanised in the 1800s, they deliberately broke the thumbs of Dhaka weavers to eliminate competition, according to some accounts. The fabric nearly vanished. In 2013, Dhaka Muslin received a **Geographical Indication (GI) tag** — a legal protection that says: only fabric made in Dhaka from this cotton can be called Dhaka Muslin. This is why GI tags exist: to protect not just products, but civilisations.

## Geographical Indication (GI) Tags

A **GI tag** is a legal protection for products that originate from a specific geographical area and possess qualities attributable to that origin. India is signatory to the TRIPS Agreement (WTO).
- **First GI tag in India**: **Darjeeling Tea** (2004–05) — the very first product registered under India's GI Act 1999.

## Major Indian Textiles

### Silk Varieties

| Silk | Origin | Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Mulberry Silk** | Karnataka (largest producer), Assam, Jammu | Standard silk; Bombyx mori silkworm |
| **Muga Silk** | Assam | Golden colour; natural lustrous; Antheraea assamensis silkworm; world's finest natural golden silk; GI tag |
| **Eri Silk** | Assam/Meghalaya | "Peace silk" — silkworm not killed; woven from broken cocoons; warm fabric |
| **Tasar Silk** | Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh | Coarser; Antheraea mylitta silkworm; tribal communities |
| **Kanchipuram Silk** | Tamil Nadu | Temple jewellery motifs; gold zari borders; wedding sarees |
| **Banarasi Silk** | Uttar Pradesh | Rich gold/silver brocade; Mughal floral designs; GI tag |
| **Mysore Silk** | Karnataka | Smooth; pure silk + gold zari |
| **Paithani** | Nashik, Maharashtra | Pure silk with real gold/silver zari; peacock motif; Satvahana origin |

### Weave Types

| Weave | Region | Feature |
|---|---|---|
| **Ikat** | Odisha (Sambalpuri), Andhra/Telangana (Pochampally) | Tie-dye before weaving; geometric patterns; warp ikat, weft ikat, or double ikat |
| **Jamdani** | West Bengal/Bangladesh | Fine muslin with brocade motif; UNESCO ICH of Bangladesh; similar tradition in India |
| **Khadi** | National | Hand-spun, hand-woven; Gandhi's symbol of self-reliance; promoted by KVIC (Khadi and Village Industries Commission) |
| **Chanderi** | MP | Fine cotton/silk blend; translucent; Chanderi town |
| **Maheshwari** | MP (Maheshwar) | Silk-cotton mix; Ahilya Bai Holkar's court weavers |
| **Kota Doria** | Rajasthan | Square checks (khat); very light cotton-silk; extreme heat adaptation |

## Exam Traps — 16 Things SSC Will Test

1. **First GI tag in India** = **Darjeeling Tea** (2004–05), NOT Banarasi Silk or Kanchipuram Silk.
2. **Muga Silk** = Assam only; natural golden colour; different from regular mulberry silk; world's finest golden silk.
3. **Tasar Silk** = Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh (not Assam — Assam = Muga and Eri).
4. **Phulkari** = Punjab; Bagh is the denser variety where entire cloth is covered — don't mix these.
5. **Chikankari** = Lucknow, UP; white-on-white shadow embroidery; NOT from Kashmir.
6. **Bidriware** = Bidar, **Karnataka** (not Rajasthan); black metal + silver inlay.
7. **Blue Pottery** = **Jaipur, Rajasthan**; made of quartz powder (NOT clay) — that's the diagnostic fact.
8. **Ikat** = tie-dye before weaving; double ikat (both warp and weft dyed) = **Patan Patola** (Gujarat) — world's most complex.
9. **Dhokra** = lost-wax cast; 4,500 years old; tribal craft; WB and Chhattisgarh are key states.
10. **Meenakari** = enamel on gold/silver = Jaipur; brought by Rajput kings — NOT to be confused with Minakari (same thing, alternate spelling).
11. **Kantha** = recycled fabric embroidery = West Bengal / Odisha; running stitch.
12. **Pipli Appliqué** = Odisha; associated with **Jagannath Rath Yatra** canopy decoration.
13. **Channapatna** = Karnataka = "Toy Town of India"; wooden lacquered toys.
14. **Maheshwari** sarees = **Maheshwar, MP**; silk-cotton blend; revived by Ahilya Bai Holkar — often tested alongside Chanderi.
15. **Paithani** = Maharashtra; real gold/silver zari; peacock motif; **Nashik/Yeola** districts; Satavahana origin.
16. **Kashmiri Pashmina** = from **Changthangi goat** underfur; GI tag; hand-spun/hand-woven — NOT the same as machine-made "pashmina."
